Arcane magic is the science of energy manipulation. Through rigorous study, you learn to recognize the patterns the God-Dragons left in the world \- the way fire always rises, water always flows, earth always grounds. By understanding these fundamental laws, you can reshape energy into controlled effects.

The elemental schools (Aeromancy, Geomancy, Pyromancy, Hydromancy) are taught first because they follow the clearest, most stable patterns. Shadowmancy is an advanced study \- manipulating absence and dissolution.

**For core mechanics (Scribing, Instability, Mana), see The Nature of Magic.**

### **Why Scribing?**

Arcane magic requires precision \- every symbol, every line of power precisely inked. This is why arcane casters carry scrolls, blank parchment, ink and notes, need quiet time each morning, and why a ruined spellbook is a disaster.

The deeper reason is that Arcane power is *concentrated* \- pulled out of the caster and fixed into ink and parchment \- while Divine power stays diffuse, carried in the caster's own body and soul. Concentration is what makes a spell portable and shareable, but it's also what makes it volatile: the tighter the magic is packed, the more unstable it gets. A Divine caster never risks their own faith detonating in their pocket.

**The Advantage:** Your spells are tangible. Hand a Searing Heat scroll to your warrior companion. Sell them, trade them, or build a permanent reserve through Stable Scribing. Magic becomes a commodity.

**The Disadvantage:** Your spells only last the day. A Quick-Scribed scroll fades the moment you next Field Rest or Long Rest, so every morning is a real choice about what you'll need \- guess wrong and you're stuck with the wrong tools, or none at all if you're ambushed before you get to scribe. Only a trained professional, working slowly at a Mage's Guild workshop, can produce a scroll that survives past that reset (Stable Scribing), and even that isn't safe to hoard forever \- nor is the parchment itself free.
