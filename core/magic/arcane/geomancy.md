Geomancy bends stone, earth, and all that is grounded. Its practitioners understand how the God-Dragons anchored the world, and they exploit those principles to reshape terrain, raise walls, or crush enemies beneath slabs of rock. Geomancy is methodical, patient, and brutal \- like the earth itself. Lords and merchants value Geomancers for fortifications and mining, but the common folk remember the stories: buried villages, triggered landslides, and tunnels that collapsed "mysteriously" during labor disputes. Useful? Absolutely. Trustworthy? Only when you're paying them more than your enemies are. People nod respectfully to Geomancers in public and bar their doors at night.

### **SPELLS**

**Terra Lancet \- Mana Cost 1**

- **Casting Time:** Major Action
- **Range:** 30 ft
- **Duration:** Instant
- **Effect:** You tear a dense chunk of stone or hardened earth from a nearby surface and hurl it at a target you can see. Make a spell attack roll vs. target's Evasion. On hit, deal 1d6 \+ ARC bludgeoning damage. If the target is Large or smaller they are knocked back 5 ft.

**Earthen Barricade \- Mana Cost 2**

- **Casting Time:** Reaction
- **Range:** Self or adjacent ally
- **Duration:** Until start of your next turn
- **Effect:** A slab of stone erupts to intercept the attack. The protected creature gains \+5 AR against the triggering attack only. This bonus does not cause the creature's own armor to degrade.

- After the attack resolves, the stone remains in place, providing Cover from attacks originating from the direction of the triggering attacker until the start of your next turn. The slab can be destroyed (HP 10, AR 8\) or circumvented by movement.

**Terrae Motus Pulse \- Mana Cost 3**

- **Casting Time:** Major Action
- **Range:** Self (20 ft radius)
- **Duration:** Instant
- **Effect:** You strike the ground and send a shockwave rippling through the earth. Make a spell overcome roll vs. the DEX Ward of all creatures within 20 ft who are standing on earth or stone.

  - **Overcome:** 2d8 \+ ARC bludgeoning damage and knocked Prone.
  - **Resist:** Half damage and remain standing.


- The tremor doesn't discriminate between friend and foe \- any wall, barricade, or structure within range, including your own Earthen Barricade or Lithic Ruina, takes 4d8 bludgeoning damage. Unsecured objects are knocked over, and the affected area becomes difficult terrain until cleared.

**Stoneflesh \- Mana Cost 3**

- **Casting Time:** Major Action
- **Range:** Self
- **Duration:** 1 minute
- **Effect:** Your flesh hardens to the texture and resilience of granite.

- While active you gain the follow effects:
  - You gain \+2 AR (does not degrade)
  - Your speed is reduced by 10 ft
  - You sink in water and cannot swim

**Lithic Ruina \- Mana Cost 4**

- **Casting Time:** Major Action
- **Range:** 60 ft
- **Duration:** Instant
- **Effect:** Raise a wall of solid stone from the ground. The wall can be up to 20 ft long, 10 ft high, and 1 ft thick. It can be shaped (corner, curve, stepped) but must be continuous and anchored to the ground along its entire length. (Wall Statistics: HP: ARC × 8, AR: 10\)

- The wall provides cover. When the spell ends or the wall is destroyed, it crumbles into rubble (difficult terrain in its space).

**Terra Sepultura \- Mana Cost 5**

- **Casting Time:** Major Action
- **Range:** 30 ft
- **Duration:** Channel
- **Effect:** The earth opens beneath a Medium sized or smaller creature you can see standing on earth or stone, then closes around them like a fist. Make a spell overcome roll vs. the target's STR Ward.

  - **Overcome:** Pulled underground and encased in stone: Paralyzed, unable to breathe, and completely buried. An encased creature can hold their breath for a number of rounds equal to their END (minimum 1\) before beginning to suffocate. The stone prison can be broken out of (AR 15, 25 HP).
  - **Resist:** Only partially buried \- Restrained and able to breathe. On their turn, the target can use a Major Action to attempt to break free: roll 1d12 \+ STR \+ best STR skill vs. your original overcome roll. On success, they free themselves.


- When used on Large creatures, the Overcome effect becomes the Resisted effect, as they cannot be completely buried. And if they were to Resist, instead they are unaffected.
