Maneuvers are special combat techniques that go beyond basic attacks. They allow you to defend yourself more effectively, control the battlefield, and create tactical advantages. Everyone has access to these abilities as long as they fulfil the prerequisites.

---

## Basic Maneuvers

### **Dashing**

Sprint as fast as your body allows.

**Action:** Major  
**Effect:** Increase your movement this turn by an amount equal to your current speed (after all modifiers, difficult terrain, etc.).

---

### **Disengaging**

Move carefully and swiftly to avoid attacks.

**Action:** Major  
**Effect:** Until the end of your current turn, your movement does not provoke **Opportunity Attacks**.

---

## Offensive Maneuvers

### **Shoving**

Push an enemy back or knock them down.

**Action:** Major (replaces attack as a Martial Weapon Attack)  
**Check:** Athletics or Brawling vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics  
**Success:** Push 5 ft OR knock prone

---

### **Disarming**

Force an enemy to drop their weapon or a held item.

**Action:** Major (replaces attack as a Martial Weapon Attack)  
**Check:** Weapon skill vs. target's weapon skill  
**Success:** Target drops one held item of your choice

---

### **Power Attack**

Sacrifice accuracy for devastating power.

**Action:** Major (as part of a melee weapon attack)  
**Effect:** Before making the attack roll, you may choose to take disadvantage on it. If the attack hits, add \+5 to the damage roll. You must declare this before rolling the attack (cannot decide after seeing the result), and cannot make another weapon attack this turn.

---

### **Precise Strike**

Aim for the gaps in an opponent's armor.

**Action:** Major (as part of an attack with a finesse or ranged weapon)  
**Effect:** Before making the attack roll, you may take a \-2 penalty to it. If the attack hits, ignore up to 5 AR on that attack.

---

### **Feint**

Use guile to open an opponent up to your strikes.

**Action:** Minor  
**Check:** Deception vs. target's Insight  
**Success:** You gain advantage on all weapon attacks you make this turn.

---

### **Blind**

Throw dirt, sand, or grit into an opponent's eyes.

**Action:** Minor (one hand free)  
**Check:** Target makes a DEX Ward (DC \= 5 \+ your DEX \+ weapon skill)  
**Failure:** Target is Blinded until the end of their next turn.

---

### **Grappling**

Seize and restrain an opponent.

**Action:** Major (replaces attack as a Martial Weapon Attack)  
**Check:** Athletics or Brawling vs. Athletics or Acrobatics  
**Restriction:** Target at most one size larger  
**Prerequisites:** Must have one hand free

**Success:**

- Target is **Restrained**  
- You can drag and move target at half speed

**While Grappling:**

- Advantage on melee attacks against the target  
- Casting requires a MIND Ward check (DC 20\) or spell fails  
- Your melee attacks ignore half the target's AR

**Escape:** Major Action, repeat contested check.

---

## Defensive Maneuvers

### **Defensive Stance**

Adopt a protective stance that makes you difficult to hit.

**Action:** Minor  
**Effect:** Until the start of your next turn, attacks against you have Disadvantage and your Parry attempts have Advantage. Your damage rolls suffer \-4 until the start of your next turn.

---

### **Dodging**

Use your adroit agility to escape danger.

**Prerequisites:** Nothing.  
**Action:** Reaction (an attack you can see)  
**Effect:** When an incoming attack would hit past your Passive Evasion, you may use a Reaction to gain a \+5 bonus to your Evasion. You must immediately move the minimum possible distance outside of the attack's range to do so \- if you cannot move out of range, you cannot Dodge (This movement does not consume your movement speed.)

**Maximum Dodges per Short Rest \= DEX ÷ 2**

**Overtime Dodges:** When regular dodges are exhausted, you may use up to your maximum again in overtime dodges. Each stores 1 level of Exhaustion, applied after combat. Cannot use if it would bring stored Exhaustion \+ current levels of Exhaustion to 10 or higher.

**Limitations:**

- If you cannot move out of range of the attack using Dodge fails (such as an Area of Effect attack) this does not expend your dodges.  
- Criticals automatically hit, bypassing Evasion, however it does not bypass Dodges.  
- You cannot use the Agility skill, Evasion or Dodging while wearing Heavy Armor.

---

### **Blocking**

Use your shield to absorb incoming attacks.

**Prerequisites:** A shield in your hand, or a melee weapon with the Two-Handed property. A Versatile weapon being wielded with both hands does not count as a Two-Handed Weapon for this purpose - only a melee weapon whose property list includes Two-Handed qualifies (a two-handed ranged weapon or firearm does not).  
**Action:** Reaction (physical attack you can see)  
**Effect:** Your AR briefly increases by END \+ Shields Skill ÷ 2 for that attack. (Half it again if you are using a Two-Handed Weapon to block)

**Limitations:**

- Works against melee. and ranged physical attacks if you are using a shield  
- Does **not** work against AoE or unseen attacks  
- Critical hits bypass Block

**Protecting Allies:** You may block for an adjacent ally; attack targets you instead, your armor degrades, you take excess damage.

---

### **Parrying**

Deflect an incoming melee attack with your weapon.

**Prerequisites:** A finesse melee weapon in your hand  
**Action:** Reaction (melee attack that would hit you that you can see)  
**Effect:** Make an attack roll with your weapon. If your roll is greater than or equal to the incoming attack roll, roll your weapon’s damage and reduce the incoming damage by that amount. Otherwise, the parry fails.

**Limitations:**

- Melee attacks only  
- Does **not** work against AoE, ranged or unseen attacks  
- Critical hits bypass Parry  
- Failed parry; full damage, Reaction spent

---

### **Taunting**

Draw an enemy's focus entirely onto you, making it harder for them to threaten anyone else.

**Action:** Minor Action  
**Check:** Deception or Intimidation vs. target's MIND Ward  
**Restriction:** Target must be able to see and hear you. Target must be within 30 ft.

**Success:** Until the start of your next turn, the target has disadvantage on all attack rolls made against creatures other than you.

**Failure:** The target is unaffected and cannot be Taunted by you again until the start of your next turn.

**Limitations:**

- Does **not** work against creatures that cannot be frightened, charmed, or otherwise psychologically affected (e.g. constructs, undead without INT, etc.) \- GM's discretion.  
- Does **not** stack with itself. Taunting an already Taunted creature refreshes the duration rather than adding additional effects.
