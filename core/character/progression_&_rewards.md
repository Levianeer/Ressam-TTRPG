### **Experience & Advancement**

- Gain XP through completing missions, defeating enemies, roleplay  
- XP required for your next level is **(Current Level \+ Next Level) × 30** \- each level costs more than the last  
- Max level is **12 (4320 XP total)**.

**How XP is Earned:**

- **Encounter XP:** Award XP per the Encounter tiers under Rewards & Treasure below to every character who took part in the fight.  
- **Objective XP:** Before a session or arc, the GM sizes each quest objective to a Minor, Average, or Grand tier (below) and awards that XP to every character present and meaningfully involved when it's cleared, it isn't divided among the party.  
- **Spotlight XP:** Once per session, the GM may award a small flat bonus (15-30 XP) to a character whose skill, career, or race feature was the reason a scene went the way it did.

**Note:** XP is tracked per character, not shared out of a pool. If someone missed a session or an objective, give them a way to catch up, don't leave them behind.

**Per Session:** Most sessions should award \~90 \- 120 XP per player, combining Encounter, Objective, and Spotlight XP. Because each level costs more than the last, expect a level up every session or two early on, and several sessions per level in the double digits.

### **Per Level Advancement**

- **Every level:** Gain \+2 Skill Points every level  
  - Skill Maximum increases by \+1 (maximum cap of 10 reached at level 8\)  
- **Every 2 levels (and at level 12):** Gain a Feat  
- **Every 4 levels:** Gain \+2 Attribute Points  
  - Attribute Maximum increases by \+1 (maximum cap of 10 reached at level 12\)

### **Rewards & Treasure**

**Minor Encounter (30-75 XP):**

- 2d10 Crown  
- Chance of 1 minor item (torch, rope, rations)

**Average Encounter (75-105 XP):**

- 5d10 Crown  
- 1d3 minor items  
- Chance of 1 weapon/armor piece

**Grand Encounter (105-300 XP):**

- 10d10 Crown  
- 2d4 minor items  
- 1 decent weapon/armor piece  
- Chance of minor magic item

# Itemization

Ressam is a mostly mundane place, however, it is possible to find magical equipment. They primarily come from deities given to their soldiers, occasionally from rituals and rarely from incredibly talented craftsmen having their works blessed or, in some cases, cursed.

## Nonmagical Weapons

Nonmagical weapons can have a \+1 to \+3 bonus to hit, representing their superior craftsmanship, balance, and sharpness.

**Crafting Weapons:** Players can commission master blacksmiths to create a weapon of quality or upgrade their current weapons for a cost or as a quest reward.

## Magical Weapons

Magical weapons do not provide a \+1 to \+3 bonus to hit.

**Magical Effects:** Instead Magical weapons have unique and powerful effects.

## Enchanted Weapons

Nonmagical weapons can be enhanced through enchantment, a process available to skilled Enchanters or players trained in the art. To enchant a weapon, you’ll need a weapon, a significant amount of gold, and a rare and costly scroll containing the desired enchantment. The process is time-consuming and labor-intensive, requiring dedication and resources.

**Enchantment Scrolls:** Specific scrolls are essential for enchanting weapons. These scrolls are highly sought after and often difficult to acquire, sometimes necessitating a quest or significant effort to obtain. Scrolls are difficult to make and only made in large quantities by master Mages. They require a part of a person’s innate energy to craft, meaning there is only a finite number of scrolls an Mage can make.

**Enchantment Process:** A proficient Mage or enchanter must conduct the ritual, which may involve additional costs, rare materials, or a skill-based challenge to succeed.

**Result:** Once enchanted, the weapon maintains its \+1 to \+3 bonus (if it had any), and gains the magical effect from the scroll.

## Legendary Weapons

Legendary weapons always have a \+3 bonus to hit along with an enchantment.

**Magical Effects:** Legendary weapons also have unique and powerful effects. They are the closest items in power to Enchanted weapons that can be found.
