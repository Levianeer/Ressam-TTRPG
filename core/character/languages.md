## Human Languages

### **Plethic**

Plethic is a regional human dialect, most common in the North of the Holy Reino Empire.

Plethic and the other Human languages are transferable enough to make out intended meaning, however details are often lost in translation.

**Characteristics:** Evolved from a local pidgin that grew into a full language, out of the heart of Reino. Relatively simple grammar with flexible word order, minimal conjugations, and extensive borrowing from other languages. Uses a practical alphabetic script.

**Script:** Originally runic, but has adopted and adapted dozens of writing systems throughout its history. Modern Plethic uses a hybrid alphabet that incorporates flowing curves for common words and angular marks for emphasis, creating text that appears to breathe on the page.

**Sound:** Remarkably fluid and musical, shifting between crisp consonant clusters borrowed from mountain dialects and flowing vowel sequences that echo oceanic languages. Speakers often unconsciously mirror the accent of whoever they're addressing, making every conversation a subtle dance of linguistic accommodation. The rhythm rises and falls like tide patterns, with stressed syllables that seem to invite others to join the conversation.

### **Batakluhk**

Batakluhk is a regional human dialect, most common in the South of the Holy Reino Empire.

Batakluhk and the other Human languages are transferable enough to make out intended meaning, however details are often lost in translation.

**Characteristics:** A flowing language that seems to bend and shift like water around stone. Highly inflected with several distinct cases that capture subtle relationships between concepts, making single words carry the weight of entire phrases in other tongues. Rich in aspectual distinctions that express not just when something happens, but how it unfolds through time \- whether complete, ongoing, or cyclical like the seasons. Features an intricate system of diminutives and augmentatives that can transform any word into expressions of endearment, reverence, or imposing grandeur.

**Script:** Graceful curved letters that seem to grow organically across parchment, resembling intertwining vines or flowing water. The script adapts beautifully to both ceremonial illuminated texts and hurried everyday writing, with flourishes that reflect the writer's regional origins.

**Sound:** Soft sibilants that whisper like wind through birch forests, punctuated by palatalized consonants that give the language its distinctive liquid quality. Consonant clusters flow together like rushing streams, while the interplay of hard and soft sounds creates a musical contrast reminiscent of church bells echoing across snowy valleys. The rhythm rises and falls like the chanting of ancient ceremonies, with stressed syllables that seem to call forth the very essence of the words being spoken.

### **Mafari**

Mafari is a regional human dialect, most common in the City-State of Mafsoleios.

Mafari and the other Human languages are transferable enough to make out intended meaning, however details are often lost in translation.

**Characteristics:** An ancient scholastic language with intricate agglutinative structure, building meaning through cascading suffixes and prefixes. Exceptionally rich in philosophical and poetic terminology, with distinct vocabularies for different levels of formality and sacred contexts. Features complex verb conjugations that encode not just tense but emotional undertones and degrees of certainty. The language distinguishes between different types of knowledge \- experiential, inherited, and divine.

**Script:** Curved, interconnected syllabic system where each character represents a complete syllable. The script flows in continuous lines without breaks between words, creating tapestry-like texts. Sacred texts often incorporate geometric patterns between verses.

**Sound:** Melodic with distinctive retroflex consonants created by curling the tongue. Features nasal undertones and liquid consonants that create a rhythmic, almost musical quality. Vowels are pure and sustained, giving the language a chant-like resonance when spoken formally. The abundance of 'l' and 'r' sounds creates rolling, layered textures.

## Feliid Languages

### **Tagaraal**

Tagaraal is a widely used seafaring language, originally born from the Alven Aurkhani language but has long since lost its roots. One of the more common languages, especially among sailors.

**Characteristics:** Tonal language with subtle pitch variations that convey emotional context and social relationships. Rich in terms describing scent, movement, and territorial concepts.

**Script:** Flowing script that curves elegantly, with decorative elements representing different emotional undertones.

**Sound:** Includes purring consonants, hissing sibilants, and distinctive vowel shifts that can indicate the speaker's mood or intent.

## Dwergan Languages

### **Khazduul**

Spoken nearly exclusively by the Dwergaz, however it has started to spread as the Tor’Az Triumvirate’s influence has grown along with its trades and crafts, a relatively simple language to learn, comparatively.

**Characteristics:** Ancient and conservative language with complex honorific systems reflecting clan hierarchies. Rich in technical terminology for crafting, mining, and engineering. Uses compound words extensively.

**Script:** Runic system, adapted for contracts and record-keeping. Each clan has traditional variations in their runes.

**Sound:** Deep, resonant consonants with rolling Rs and guttural stops that echo well in mountain halls.

## Aelven Languages

### **Aurkhani**

An ancient Aelven language, possibly one of the oldest spoken languages. Common enough but hard to learn.

**Characteristics:** Highly inflected language with complex verb aspects relating to time, certainty, and the speaker's relationship to natural cycles. Contains archaic forms preserved in songs and rituals.

**Script:** A simple logographic script that often condenses words and meanings into complex symbols; poetry and stories are often mistaken as abstract art as opposed to writing.

**Sound:** Musical quality with vowel harmonies that seem to resonate with natural environments.

---

## Orkhish Languages

### **Khorian**

The Orkhish language of Khorian is a shockingly difficult language to learn, and not just because of its lack of teachers. It is ironically quite complex but easy once you’ve learned its basics, thanks to its consistency.

**Characteristics:** Aggressive, direct language with short, punchy words. Grammar emphasizes action and immediacy. Rich vocabulary for combat, raiding, and tribal politics.

**Script:** Bold, angular marks often carved or branded rather than written.

**Sound:** Harsh consonants, shouted rather than spoken, with intimidating growls and barks.

---

### **Grazn**

If Khorian is difficult to learn, the Skegg’s Grazn is near impossible, it has all of the troubles as its cousin but with none of the convenience of being directly spoken through words. One of the harder languages for a Human to learn.

**Characteristics:** A distant dialect of Khorian. A clicking, chittering language with rapid syllable clusters. Incorporates chemical/pheromonal concepts that don't translate well to other languages.

**Script:** Pattern-based writing resembling maze structures or tunnel networks.

**Sound:** Clicks, snaps, and rapid chittering that can sound like machinery to other races.

## Alsahli Languages

### **Zakhrafan**

Zakhrafan is a regional Alsahli/Human dialect, most common in the Great Sand used exclusively by both the Alashar Tribes and the human Ederani Al’Nur.

**Characteristics:** Poetic language with extensive metaphorical expressions relating to desert survival, star navigation, and hospitality customs. Verb tenses reflect the cyclical nature of nomadic life.

**Script:** Cursive script designed to be written quickly on temporary materials.

**Sound:** Flowing, wind-like quality with aspirated consonants and elongated vowels that carry across desert distances.

## Rare Languages

### **Dzinariel**

To speak Dzinariel is to speak in riddles wrapped in history and culture \- every phrase assumes the listener knows which ancient oath was broken, which secret was kept, and why it matters. The single hardest language for any race to learn.

**Characteristics:** Archaic scholarly language with precise grammatical structures. Incorporates mathematical and geometric concepts, and ancient cultural references into its basic grammar. Requires a deep understanding of Dzinari culture and history, non-native speakers will struggle to converse.

**Script:** Geometric symbols that can be arranged in complex patterns for enhanced meaning.

**Sound:** Measured, ceremonial cadence with carefully enunciated syllables.

---

### **Daemonic**

To learn Daemonic is to let it learn you \- its grammar of command and submission rewires the speaker's thoughts over time, and even those who master it find themselves wondering whether they chose those words, or the words chose them.

**Characteristics:** Corrupting language that subtly influences listeners. Grammar based on dominance and submission. Speaking it for extended periods may have psychological effects.

**Script:** Twisted symbols that seem to writhe on the page. Dangerous to write without proper protections.

**Sound:** Seductive whispers that turn threatening, with undertones that make listeners uncomfortable.

---

### **Dragon’ Script**

Dragon' Script cannot be learned so much as understood \- each glyph contains a complete truth that reveals itself differently to every reader, and what a novice sees as 'fire' a master might read as 'the moment before transformation'; no one has yet learned how it was spoken.

**Characteristics:** Pure conceptual language where each glyph represents complete ideas rather than sounds or words. Said to be the language of creation itself.

**Script:** Complex glyphs that seem to shift meaning based on the reader's understanding and magical attunement.

**Sound:** Not yet known how to verbalise.
