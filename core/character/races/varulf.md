# Varulf

## *Night-Terror \- The fear of losing control*

## Overview

The Varulf are cursed reflections of humanity \- twisted beings born from terror and instinct, neither fully man nor wholly beast. Their origins lie in a nightmare shared by mankind: the fear of losing control, of the savage lurking beneath civility. Transformed through bite and blood, the Varulf walk a tormented path between monstrous hunger and human conscience. Some exile themselves to the wilds in shame, others revel in their power, becoming legends of the deep forests and haunted hills. Wherever they go, the howl of the predator follows.

**Homeland:** Lustralis  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 STR, \+1 END, \-1 FAI  
**Age:** Lives 100s of years  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 30 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Plethic  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 Athletics, \+1 Survival

**Level Adjustment:** \+1

### **Features**

**Human Heritage:** You appear human when not transformed  
**Low-light Vision:** You treat dim light as bright light within 60 ft.  
**Bestial Form:** Once per Field Rest or Long Rest, you can use a Minor Action to transform for 10 minutes. While transformed:

- \+10 ft speed, \+1 STR, \+1 END.  
- \+2 Natural AR.  
- Gain 10 temporary HP.  
- Gain natural weapons: Claws (1d8+STR slashing), Bite (1d10+STR piercing). These count as magical.

When the transformation ends, you lose any remaining temporary HP and revert to your human form. If you are unconscious when the transformation ends, you remain unconscious.

**Curse of the Beast:** When you fail a MIND Ward, make an additional END Ward DC 23 or enter Feral Frenzy:

- Transform immediately if not transformed.  
- Must attack the nearest creature each turn.  
- Lasts 1 minute or until knocked unconscious.  
- This can force additional transformations beyond the Field Rest or Long Rest limit.

**Silver Vulnerability:** Vulnerable to silvered weapons.

## Physical Appearance

In human form, Varulf are indistinguishable from ordinary humans, though some bear subtle signs: restless eyes, an unusually sharp canine, or a strange stillness before dusk. When transformed, they become towering hybrids of man and wolf \- muscular and hunched, covered in bristled black, grey, or rust-coloured fur. Their eyes burn with unnatural crimson or gold light, and their claws and fangs shimmer faintly with cursed magic. Despite their monstrous bulk, they move with terrifying speed and fluidity.

Varulf age slowly once afflicted, with lifespans extending beyond two centuries, though few live long due to the violent lives they lead. The transformation into full Varulf typically manifests weeks or months after the initial bite, marked by fever dreams and behavioural changes.

## Origins and Mythology

The Varulf were not painted by the Four Artists, nor shaped with divine purpose \- they are the unintentional spawn of fear itself. Legend tells that during the Age of Sundering, when shadows crept longest and trust waned, the Varulf curse was birthed from a collective human dread: the fear of becoming what they most despise. Some myths whisper that an ancient god-beast of the Wild was slain by mortal hands, and with its dying breath, it seeded the curse into the hearts of men as revenge.

In Varulf oral tales, there is no redemption \- only mastery or surrender. Some believe the curse can be used as a weapon against greater darkness. Others call it a punishment for ancient sins.

In function, the curse is both a disease and a hex, only communicable under the moonlight as a blood-borne pathogen every 5 years \- when the two moons eclipse each other.

## Culture and Society

Varulf rarely form true societies. Most wander or live in secret within other settlements, hiding their nature with caution. Others gather into loose, brutal packs that claim forgotten woods, cursed hills, or borderland ruins.

**Social Structure:** Among packs, dominance is earned through challenge and blood. Leadership is often held by the strongest or most lucid. In rare cases, a Varulf shaman or "Silent Fang" guides the pack spiritually, helping members manage the Curse.

**Traditions and Rites:** Rites of Passage include the "Howling," where a new Varulf experiences their first transformation under supervision (or exile), and the "Red Hunt," a grim ritual where a pack must hunt one of their own who has lost all reason. Death rites vary \- some bury their dead in shallow forest graves; others leave them to rot as warnings.

**Language and Names:** Varulf retain their human language, but some packs use a low, growling pidgin of howls, body signals, and sharp whistles. Names are typically human in origin, though some adopt feral titles like Ashfang, Thornback, or Redjaw.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Artistry among Varulf is rare but raw. They mark territory with claw-scratched runes or blood-painted symbols. Some carve bones into charms, or etch moon-phases into stone to track their curse.

## Homeland and Distribution

The Varulf are most commonly found in Lustralis, particularly in its misted woods and forgotten valleys, where light rarely breaks through the canopy. They haunt the borders of civilization, trailing caravans, lurking near graveyards, or watching lonely roads from the dark.

Some have migrated, where they become whispered legends or feared bounty targets. Others are exiled by their own kind, especially if they seek cures or live openly among humans.

## Relations with Other Races

Varulf are feared and hated, often seen as little more than monsters. Even those who retain their humanity face suspicion, exile, or violence if discovered. Humans view them with horror, often because their own kin fall victim to the curse, while Aelfs treat them as aberrations, unnatural blemishes upon the world's art. Dwergaz are more pragmatic, offering coin for pelts or heads, and Tembels regard them with a mix of pity and fascination, especially given their own Curseborn status.

Despite this, some secret Varulf have brokered alliances or even saved villages during times of greater threat, but such acts are often buried beneath layers of myth or denial.

## Faith and Philosophy

Varulf rarely follow traditional gods, feeling abandoned or forsaken. However, some whisper prayers to Vessel, hoping their eventual death serves some greater balance. Others turn to darker powers, trading what's left of their soul for control over the beast.

A rare few cling to Al'Nur Lehovil, interpreting the desert god's trials as kindred tests of the self. But most Varulf live by a grim creed: "Harm none unless they chase you; chase none unless they bleed you."
