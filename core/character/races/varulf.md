# Varulf

## *Night-Terror \- The fear of losing control*

## Overview

The Varulf are cursed reflections of humanity \- neither fully man nor wholly beast, born from a nightmare shared by mankind: the fear of losing control, of the savage lurking beneath civility. Transformed through bite and blood, a Varulf walks a tormented line between monstrous hunger and human conscience. Some exile themselves to the wilds in shame; others make a legend of their power instead. Either way, the howl follows them.

**Homeland:** Lustralis  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 STR, \+1 END, \-1 FAI  
**Age:** Lives 100s of years  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 30 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Plethic  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 Athletics, \+1 Survival

**Level Adjustment:** \+1

### **Features**

**Human Heritage:** You appear human when not transformed  
**Low-light Vision:** You treat dim light as bright light within 60 ft.  
**Bestial Form:** Once per Field Rest or Long Rest, you can use a Minor Action to transform for 10 minutes. While transformed:

- \+10 ft speed, \+1 STR, \+1 END.  
- \+2 Natural AR.  
- Gain 10 temporary HP.  
- Gain natural weapons: Claws (1d8+STR slashing), Bite (1d10+STR piercing). These count as magical.

When the transformation ends, you lose any remaining temporary HP and revert to your human form. If you are unconscious when the transformation ends, you remain unconscious.

**Curse of the Beast:** When you fail a MIND Ward, make an additional END Ward DC 23 or enter Feral Frenzy:

- Transform immediately if not transformed.  
- Must attack the nearest creature each turn.  
- Lasts 1 minute or until knocked unconscious.  
- This can force additional transformations beyond the Field Rest or Long Rest limit.

**Silver Vulnerability:** Vulnerable to silvered weapons.

## Physical Appearance

In human form, a Varulf is indistinguishable from an ordinary human, save for subtle signs \- restless eyes, an unusually sharp canine, a strange stillness before dusk. Transformed, it becomes a towering man-wolf hybrid, muscular and hunched beneath bristled black, grey, or rust fur, eyes burning crimson or gold, claws and fangs shimmering with cursed magic; despite the bulk, the movement is fast enough to close a gap in an instant. Once afflicted, a Varulf ages slowly, though few live long given the violence of the life; the full transformation typically sets in weeks or months after the initial bite, marked by fever dreams and shifting behaviour.

## Origins and Mythology

The Varulf were not painted by the Four Artists \- they are the unintentional spawn of fear itself, a collective human dread of becoming what one most despises, bled through from the Fields of Nocturne during the Age of the Sundering. Some myths instead credit an ancient god-beast of the Wild, slain by mortal hands, who seeded the curse into men as revenge with its dying breath. Varulf oral tradition offers no third option beyond the myth's own terms: mastery of the beast, or surrender to it. The curse itself spreads as both disease and hex, transmissible under moonlight once every five years, when the twin moons eclipse.

## Culture and Society

Varulf rarely form true societies \- most wander alone or hide within other settlements, though some gather into loose, brutal packs claiming forgotten woods or borderland ruins.

**Social Structure:** Dominance is earned through challenge and blood, leadership held by whoever is strongest or most lucid. A pack's **Vitki** \- part shaman, part elder \- sometimes emerges to guide it spiritually and help its members manage the curse.

**Traditions and Rites:** The **Galdr** marks a new Varulf's first supervised transformation. The **Raudaveidr** is a grim ritual in which a pack hunts down one of its own who has lost all reason. Death rites vary, from shallow forest graves to leaving the dead as a warning.

**Language and Names:** A Varulf keeps its human language, though some packs use a growling pidgin of howls and signals. Names are usually human, though some adopt feral titles like Ashfang or Redjaw.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Rare and raw \- claw-scratched territory runes, bone charms, moon-phases etched into stone to track the curse.

## Homeland and Distribution

Varulf are most common in Lustralis, in its misted woods and forgotten valleys where light rarely breaks the canopy, haunting the borders of civilisation near graveyards and lonely roads. Some migrate and become whispered legends or bounty targets; others are exiled by their own kind, especially those who seek a cure or choose to live openly among humans.

## Relations with Other Races

Varulf are feared and hated as little more than monsters, facing suspicion or violence even when they retain their humanity. Humans view them with horror, often having lost kin to the curse; Aelfs treat them as unnatural blemishes on the world's art; Dwergaz pragmatically pay coin for pelts or heads; and Tembels regard them with pity and fascination, given their own Curseborn status. A rare few Varulf have secretly saved villages or brokered alliances, though such acts are usually buried under myth or denial.

## Faith and Philosophy

Varulf rarely follow traditional gods, feeling abandoned or forsaken. Some whisper prayers to Vessel, hoping their eventual death serves some greater balance; others turn to darker powers, trading what's left of their soul for control over the beast. A rare few cling to Al'Nur Lehovil, reading the desert god's trials as kindred tests of the self. Most simply live by a grim creed: "Harm none unless they chase you; chase none unless they bleed you."

## Variations and Subgroups

Varulf oral tradition draws its sharpest line between mastery and surrender, and most of a Varulf's life is spent proving which side of it they've landed on. A Varulf who achieves mastery keeps enough control to hold a trade, a family, even a place in human society between transformations, and is the rare kind who might broker peace or hide in plain sight for decades. One who surrenders stops resisting the Curse of the Beast entirely, transforms on ever-thinner provocation, and is usually only a season or two from becoming the target of a Raudaveidr \- either its own pack's or a hired hunter's. A pack led by a Vitki exists mainly to keep as many of its members on the mastery side of that line as it can, for as long as it can.
