# Aelves

## *Windborne Artefolk*

## Overview

Aelves are swift, graceful, and aloof deeply valuing tradition. Their culture tends to be rooted in philosophy and art, and many see the Wind as a source of wisdom, guiding them toward enlightenment. Aelves live for centuries, but their long lives have made them somewhat detached from the everyday struggles of other races, resulting in a cool, often distant demeanour, however, not to be mistaken for disdain, they just struggle to build attachment to those with such short lives. They are highly valued as scouts and archers, moving unseen like the Wind itself.

## Physical Appearance

Aelves are slender and lissom, with warm olive skin and eyes gone entirely black \- iris and sclera alike evolved to drink in light rather than reflect it, giving their gaze an unreadable, unblinking quality other races find unsettling.

## Origins and Mythology

One of the races born from the Painters' energy in the Age of Mythos, Aelves consider themselves the Painters' only true heirs and often regard other races with disdain. In Aurkhan the wind is not just weather but a living spiritual network of the Aelven dead, and Aelves commune with it as ancestry and revelation both.

That unity broke after the Sundering. The Sundrift, a spiritual schism, split off a faction who came to believe the wind was "polluted" by the suffering dead \- exiled and stricken from the wind's record, they became the **Ash'shene**. Led by Vaeltheron Ashwake, a Windsor who performed a forbidden Flesh Rite during a storm and claimed to hear the true Painters "beneath the ashes," they gather at bone-fires in remote volcanic caves or charred forests, wearing White Ash Masks of powdered bone to "deafen" the wind, and reading Fire Divination in scorched bone. The once-unified Aelven empire never fully recovered, reorganising into the semi-autonomous Groves that persist today.

## Culture and Society

Aelves organise into semi-autonomous **Groves**, led by councils of elders called **The Circle**, chosen for their ability to "hear" the wind rather than by birthright. Among their people, **Windsors** interpret the wind's whispers and advise on law; **Keepers** preserve tradition through art and craft; and **Black Blades**, elite warrior-scouts, defend the Groves and strike preemptively at threats \- feared by some as butchers, revered by others as saints, and in occasional quiet tension with the Windsors over whether the wind's warnings or its wisdom matters more.

**Traditions and Rites:** Newborns are named from "signs" read in the dawn wind (*e.g., "Sylphine" \= "the sighing breeze"*). Couples marry through the Knotting of Threads, weaving a tapestry from their hair for the wind and ancestors to judge. The dead are laid on high platforms to be "taken by the wind," joining the ancestral network; the worst punishment is to be *shackled* \- buried whole with ears sealed shut, cut off from the wind forever. The Turning Gale celebrates the Painters' first breath into the Aelves; the Veil of Ash mourns the Sundering.

**Language and Names:** Aurkhani is fluid and melodic, and some words require an exhale to pronounce correctly (*e.g., "Llyrrh" \= "the pause between gusts"*). Names are wind-given at birth (*Thalorien \= "one who walks with storms"*) and gain earned titles with milestones; outsiders never speak a dead Aelf's full name, believed to trap their soul.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Aelven art is ephemeral and sacred: Living Tapestries that ripple like wind-swept grass, Skycaller Instruments left in groves for the wind itself to play, and Mourning Masks worn during the Veil of Ash. Techniques are jealously guarded as "the last legacy of the Painters." The Elar'laevien work only in natural, replanted materials; the Ash'shene hold metal, fire, and ash as a necessary, cleansing evil.

## Homeland and Distribution

Aurkhan is a realm of towering forests, mist-shrouded valleys, and floating plateaus, its air humming with ancestral voices. Its heartland is the Eversong Basin, home to the oldest Groves among spiralling, wind-shaped willows. Leadership rotates every decade among the Five Eternal Groves: Sylph's Rest, Veilspire, Llyrranor, Gale's End, and the current capital, Thalorien's Reach.

## Relations with Other Races

Aelves rarely leave Aurkhan, keeping to self-imposed isolation and preferring to strike preemptively rather than expand. They trade Windseed Silk and Strombrew for Renos and Crowns, often paid to human mercenary proxies, and require outsiders entering the Groves to swear a Voice-Bound Pact of silence \- violators are hunted by the Black Blades.
