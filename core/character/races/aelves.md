# Aelves

## *Windborne Artefolk*

## Overview

Aelves are swift, graceful, and aloof deeply valuing tradition. Their culture tends to be rooted in philosophy and art, and many see the Wind as a source of wisdom, guiding them toward enlightenment. Aelves live for centuries, but their long lives have made them somewhat detached from the everyday struggles of other races, resulting in a cool, often distant demeanour, however, not to be mistaken for disdain, they just struggle to build attachment to those with such short lives. They are highly valued as scouts and archers, moving unseen like the Wind itself.

## Physical Appearance

Aelves are slender and lissom beings, averaging around six feet in height, with a striking appearance defined by their warm olive skin and pitch-black eyes \- eyes that have permanently dilated, with irises and even sclera evolved to absorb light more efficiently, giving them a deep, all-black look. They mature slowly, typically reaching adulthood around the age of 100, and live remarkably long lives, averaging around 500 years, though some individuals surpass even that.

## Origins and Mythology

One of the original races born from the Painters' energy back in the Age of Mythos, the Aelves are beings deeply tied to the wind \- both spiritually and metaphysically. In Aurkhan, the "wind" is more than a natural force; it is a vast spiritual network composed of the Aelven dead. Considering themselves the only true heirs of the Painters, they believe they alone carry on their creators' will. As a result, they often regard the other races with disdain.

While most Aelves revere the wind as the sacred Breath of the Painters, a long-forgotten heresy divides them. The Sundrift, an ancient spiritual and philosophical split that occurred after the Sundering, saw an entire faction sever themselves from ancestral communion, believing the wind to be corrupted by centuries of unrested dead. These exiles, known today as the followers of *Ash'shene*, were cast out for sacrilege, their names stricken from the wind.

The followers of *Ash'shene* believe the wind is "polluted" by the dead's suffering. They gather around **bone-fires**, burning relics of the past to commune with what they claim are the *true* voices of the Painters \- untainted by the Sundering's corruption. Their rituals include donning White Ash Masks, made from powdered bone and ceramic and worn to "deafen" the wind's whispers, and practicing Fire Divination, the interpreting of cracks in scorched bones as messages from the Painters. They gather in Hidden Enclaves, found in remote regions, often volcanic caves or charred forests, that the Aelven empire, weakened by the Sundering, can no longer fully root out.

This fracture has left the once mighty and eternal Aelven empire in shattered ruins, now scattered across smaller clans that collectively can just barely match the other nations they once claimed superiority over.

After the Sundering, many Aelves returned to find the winds filled with rage and confusion, not wisdom. A visionary named Vaeltheron Ashwake, once a Windsor, claimed the Breath was broken \- that the spirits were not guiding but screaming, and that only fire could cleanse the ancestral corruption. He performed a forbidden Flesh Rite during a storm and survived it unscarred, claiming to have "burned away the noise" and heard the true Painters beneath the ashes. His followers became the first Ash'shene.

## Culture and Society

The Aelves organise themselves into "Groves" \- semi-autonomous, clan-like communities led by a council of elders known as "The Circle." These elders are chosen not by birthright but by their ability to "hear" the wind's guidance, a trait believed to signify the Painters' favour.

Though the Groves present a unified face, whispers persist of enclaves \- Aelven groves who dwell in silence, forsaking ancestral guidance. The Circle denies their existence, but Black Blades are sometimes dispatched under mysterious pretences to quiet "echoes" that should not be heard.

The most influential Groves, like Thalorien's, reside in ancient forest sanctuaries or mountaintop citadels, while lesser Groves act as scouts, diplomats, or outposts in contested lands. Among their people, the *Windsors*, philosopher-priests, interpret the whispers of the dead in the wind and advise on law and destiny; the *Keepers*, artisans and historians, preserve Aelven traditions through art, song, and craftsmanship; and the *Black Blades*, warrior-scouts, are elite soldiers and spies who defend the Groves or execute preemptive strikes against threats. Both feared and revered, some Groves see the Black Blades as shadowy saints, others whisper they are "butchers," and a mild tension exists between them and the Windsors, akin to military vs. clergy \- the Black Blades prioritise "the wind's warnings of danger," while the Windsors seek "its wisdom."

Power is fluid; reverence is given to age and wisdom, but younger Aelves who display exceptional intuition or skill can rise quickly. Their detachment from mortal struggles often makes their leadership seem mercurial or indifferent to outsiders.

**Traditions and Rites:** Newborns are presented to the winds at dawn, where elders listen for "signs" in the breeze \- a gust taken as a blessing, a lull as an omen of a life of trials \- and names are chosen from the wind's murmurings (*e.g., "Sylphine" \= "the sighing breeze"*). Aelves bond in marriage through the "Knotting of Threads," in which the couple weaves a tapestry from their hair while the wind and ancestors "judge" the union; if the wind unravels it, the match is deemed ill-fated. The deceased are placed atop high platforms to be "taken by the wind," their voice joining the spiritual network to guide future generations, and mourning lasts until the next storm when their essence is fully absorbed. A common criminal punishment is to be *forgotten* or *shackled* \- the body buried whole and the ears ritually sealed with thread, considered an immortal shame. In rare, whispered cases, an Aelf may undergo a Flesh Communion, a sacred devouring of bone, flesh, or hair to inherit the memories of the deceased; though outlawed by most Circles, Black Blades are rumoured to perform this rite before deep infiltration missions. Among their holidays, the Turning Gale marks the supposed day the Painters breathed life into the first Aelves, celebrated with dances and wind-chime symphonies, while the Veil of Ash is a solemn remembrance of the Sundering's catastrophe, where Aelves don grey veils and fast until the wind "cleanses" them at dusk. Children are taught by the Grove collectively, playing games like "Wind Chase" to interpret wind patterns and find hidden objects; gender roles are egalitarian, though wartime sees a cultural push to "shelter the bearers of future voices" (childbirth-capable Aelves) - this is pragmatic, not dogmatic.

**Language and Names:** Their native tongue, "Aurkhani," is fluid and melodic, with vowels that mimic wind patterns. Outsiders struggle to pronounce words correctly, as some require exhaling while speaking (*e.g., "Llyrrh" \= "the pause between gusts"*). Wind-Blessed Names are given at birth (*Thalorien \= "one who walks with storms"*), and Earned Titles are added after milestones (*e.g., "of the Greater Groves" denotes leadership*). Outsiders speaking the full name of the dead is forbidden \- it's believed to "trap" their soul in the mortal world.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Aelves view art as a sacred dialogue with the Painters. Their works are ephemeral or designed to "sing": Living Tapestries woven from silk and enchanted to ripple like wind-swept grass, often depicting moments in history; Skycaller Instruments, harps and flutes designed to be played by the wind itself and left in sacred groves to "compose" ancestral messages; and Mourning Masks, worn during Veil of Ash, carved from pale wood and inlaid with obsidian to represent the void of the Painters' absence.

Their craftsmanship is both coveted and resented by other races \- Aelves refuse to teach their techniques, guarding them as "the last legacy of the Painters."

Just like their culture, there has been a schism in their arts and crafts. While the Elar'laevien are devoted to doing all things in a proportional and balanced manner, using only natural materials for their homes and replanting all they use, the Ash'shene believe that metal, steel and bone are a necessary evil, and that the fire, wood, and ash that created their weapons and homes are a divine cleansing of the corrupted land they inhabit.

## Homeland and Distribution

The Aelves are deeply tied to the ancient lands of Aurkhan, a realm of towering forests, mist-shrouded valleys, and floating plateaus where the air itself hums with ancestral voices. Their homeland is both a sanctuary and a fortress \- a place where the past lingers in every breeze.

Their heartland is the Eversong Basin, a vast, bowl-shaped expanse of silver-barked willows and luminous blue moss where the oldest and most sacred Groves reside; the trees here grow in spiralling patterns, believed to be shaped by the wind's will. Every decade, the seat of the Greater Groves shifts to one of the Five Eternal Groves: Sylph's Rest, a town built into the roots of a mile-wide tree; Veilspire, a citadel of woven branches atop the trees of a sprawling forest; Llyrranor, a subterranean Grove lit by bio-luminescent fungi; Gale's End, a coastal fortress where storms rage endlessly; and Thalorien's Reach, the current capital, a cliff-side city of bridges that sing in the wind.

## Relations with Other Races

The Aelves rarely leave Aurkhan, keeping to a self-imposed isolation broken only by those who were exiled, and they avoid territorial expansion, preferring instead to preemptively strike at threats. Though they prefer barter in trade, they will accept Renos and Crowns, often using the coin to bribe human mercenaries as proxies; their exports include Windseed Silk, lighter than linen yet stronger than steel, and Strombrew, a mild hallucinogenic wine fermented in lightning-struck barrels. Outsiders are forbidden from entering the Groves without a Voice-Bound Pact \- a vow of silence about what they witness \- and violators are hunted by the Black Blades.

## Faith and Philosophy

After the Sundering the Aelves broke up into two groups, the Elar'laevien and the Ash'shene. The current Aelven "government" and society is made up of the Elar'laevien, while the Ash'shene are considered outcasts.

The majority believe the world is a living painting \- Elar'laevien \- shaped by the brushstrokes of the Four Painters. They don't pray in words but through breath, silence, and art. Sacred groves act as temples, each reflecting one Painter's essence: wind, flame, stone, or water. And they revere the Wind as the collective soul of the ancestors.

Those of the Ash'shene faith claim the Wind is polluted with the madness of the dead from the Sundering. Instead, they worship Ash as the purified form of spirit, and Fire as a sacred destroyer and revealer of truth, treating the Wind as a liar whose howls hide pain. They wear white painted masks made of the bones of their ancestors to hide from it.
