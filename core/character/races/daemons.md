# Daemons

## *Night-Terror \- The fear of the dark*

## Overview

Born not of the flesh but of fracture, Daemons are parasitic horrors \- manifestations of entropy and madness that claw their way into the waking world through mortal vessels. They are not conquerors nor creators, but unmakers, intent on unravelling the world thread by thread. Some whisper that Daemons are not invaders from beyond but reflections of the worst within \- nightmares made animate by mortal guilt, fear, and desire.

**Homeland:** Fields of Nocturne  
**Starting Attributes:** 3 points to distribute among mental Attributes (MIND, ARC, FAI, CHA)  
**Age:** Immortal (return to Fields of Nocturne instead of dying)  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Dependent on host race  
**Base Speed:** Dependent on host race  
**Starting Languages:** Demonic, host race's primary language  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 Arcane Lore, \+1 Deception

**Level Adjustment:** \+1

### **Features**

**Natural Liar:** You gain \+1 to Deception.  
**Parasitic Existence:** You have no physical form and cannot interact with the material world without a host. When you possess a host, their consciousness is suppressed \- they are unaware of events during possession and remember nothing afterward. You use the host's physical Attributes (STR, DEX, END), size, speed, and natural abilities (such as darkvision or natural weapons), but not their learned Skills or Feats. The host body ages normally.  
**Starting Host:** At character creation, choose a host (DM approval required). You use the host's physical Attributes (STR, DEX, END) and your own mental Attributes.  
**Host Death:** When your host dies, you are expelled and become a disembodied haunt bound to the location of death. While haunting: you are invisible and incorporeal, you can perceive your surroundings but cannot interact with physical objects, you cannot move more than 100 feet from where the host died, and you can only be detected by magical means. You may attempt possession as described. If 7 days pass without obtaining a new host, you dissipate permanently, returning to Nocturne.  
**Possession:** While haunting a location, you may attempt to possess any sleeping creature with MIND 3+. The target makes a FAI Ward (DC \= 15 \+ your CHA). On failure, you possess them at dawn. Willing targets may forgo the Ward. Creatures immune to charm are immune to possession.  
**Host Switch Ritual:** You can perform an 8-hour Possession ritual (requires 1,000 Crown in materials and a sleeping target) to transfer to a host with superior physical Attributes.

## Physical Appearance

In their unstable, unbound state, Daemons are malignant spirit entities visible only in flickers of shadow and warped reflections, affecting the world only through possession. Possessed hosts show distorted flesh, bone twisted into unnatural geometry, and black ichor weeping from every orifice, their voice a layered chorus of agony. Those who anchor themselves as true-bodied Daemons instead take vast, mind-bending forms \- multi-limbed, eyes where mouths should be, skin that moves like fabric. Daemons do not age or get born; they awaken, usually within a dream, a murder, or a moment of total despair, their forms reflecting the trauma that birthed them.

## Origins and Mythology

Daemons hail from Nocturne, thought to be the byproduct of a broken universe sloughed off like dead skin from the edge of sanity. Some scholars date their origin to the Sundering's fracture of reality; others believe them older still, remnants of a failed idea buried beneath creation itself. All are tethered to the Fields of Nocturne, a realm of surreal dread where time dissolves and memory festers, bound by a wound in reality that must be severed to destroy one permanently.

## Culture and Society

Daemons form no societies in a mortal sense, though they recognise each other and sometimes coalesce into warped hierarchies of shared obsession or torment \- "courts" of madness that never last, and often end in annihilation.

**Types of Daemonic Influence:** Possessors, the most common, whisper promises or dread until a mortal host invites them in. Manifested Daemons have stabilised enough to act without a host, becoming localised terrors or regional tyrants. Sovereigns are near-divine Daemons whose forms rival gods \- legends name Azelthael the Blooming Maw and Chir-Gharun, the Choir of Teeth, whose presence can unravel reality in wide swathes.

**Common Patterns of Behaviour:** Mimicry sees Daemons wear the shape of a loved one or a child's voice to manipulate; powerful Daemons reshape the world into impossible landscapes \- hollow cities, stairways into mirrors, forests of weeping statues; and some mortal cults practice entropy worship, revering Daemons as sacraments of unravelling.

**Language and Names:** Daemons speak *Demonic*, heard in cracking glass or the weeping of the dying \- true Daemonic speech fractures minds, and true names are mathematically unspeakable, causing migraines or seizures if spoken aloud. Mortals give them descriptive names instead (*"The Mawing One"*), while among themselves names are conceptual: *Hunger-as-Form*, *Ash of First Memory*.

## Homeland and Distribution

The Fields of Nocturne are a semi-mythical pocket-realm of endless dusk where gravity fails and memory echoes \- where Daemons gestate and return when exorcised, unless their tether is severed. Beyond Nocturne, they appear at manifestation sites saturated with pain, death, or despair: mass graves, battlefields, cursed ruins, haunted keeps.

## Relations with Other Races

No race trusts Daemons \- most actively hunt or seal them, even the most amoral factions treating them as existential threats. Some mortals still bargain with them for power, contracts never honoured, and extended contact causes Daemon-Bleeding, a slow unravelling of sanity, voice, and memory.

## Faith and Philosophy

Daemons do not believe \- they are belief's negation, existing to dismantle meaning and the illusion of safety. Mortals interpret them religiously anyway: the Cult of the Spiral Gate worships them as agents of cosmic freedom, believing the universe must end to be reborn; the Order of the Ninth Seal is sworn to bind or destroy them through soul-binding rituals; and the Solace-Born, a heretical sect, believe Daemons are angels misunderstood by a flawed world.
