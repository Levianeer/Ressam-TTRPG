# Daemons

## *Night-Terror \- The fear of the dark*

## Overview

Born not of the flesh but of fracture, Daemons are parasitic horrors \- manifestations of entropy and madness that claw their way into the waking world through mortal vessels. They are not conquerors nor creators, but unmakers, intent on unravelling the world thread by thread. Some whisper that Daemons are not invaders from beyond but reflections of the worst within \- nightmares made animate by mortal guilt, fear, and desire.

**Homeland:** Fields of Nocturne  
**Starting Attributes:** 3 points to distribute among mental Attributes (MIND, ARC, FAI, CHA)  
**Age:** Immortal (return to Fields of Nocturne instead of dying)  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Dependent on host race  
**Base Speed:** Dependent on host race  
**Starting Languages:** Demonic, host race's primary language  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 Arcane Lore, \+1 Deception

**Level Adjustment:** \+1

### **Features**

**Natural Liar:** You gain \+1 to Deception.  
**Parasitic Existence:** You have no physical form and cannot interact with the material world without a host. When you possess a host, their consciousness is suppressed \- they are unaware of events during possession and remember nothing afterward. You use the host's physical Attributes (STR, DEX, END), size, speed, and natural abilities (such as darkvision or natural weapons), but not their learned Skills or Feats. The host body ages normally.  
**Starting Host:** At character creation, choose a host (DM approval required). You use the host's physical Attributes (STR, DEX, END) and your own mental Attributes.  
**Host Death:** When your host dies, you are expelled and become a disembodied haunt bound to the location of death. While haunting: you are invisible and incorporeal, you can perceive your surroundings but cannot interact with physical objects, you cannot move more than 100 feet from where the host died, and you can only be detected by magical means. You may attempt possession as described. If 7 days pass without obtaining a new host, you dissipate permanently, returning to Nocturne.  
**Possession:** While haunting a location, you may attempt to possess any sleeping creature with MIND 3+. The target makes a FAI Ward (DC \= 15 \+ your CHA). On failure, you possess them at dawn. Willing targets may forgo the Ward. Creatures immune to charm are immune to possession.  
**Host Switch Ritual:** You can perform an 8-hour Possession ritual (requires 1,000 Crown in materials and a sleeping target) to transfer to a host with superior physical Attributes.

## Physical Appearance

In their earliest state, Daemons lack a stable form. They exist as malignant spirit entities, visible only in flickers of shadow and warped reflections. Only through possession can they affect the material world \- seizing control of a mortal body and corrupting it into an aberrant shell of what it once was. Possessed hosts show distorted flesh, bone twisted into unnatural geometry, and black ichor leaking from every orifice, and their voice becomes a layered chorus of agony, the eyes \- if they remain \- nothing but weeping pits of obsidian. Those who anchor themselves fully as true-bodied Daemons take on vast and terrible forms instead \- often towering, multi-limbed, and draped in features that bend the mind, with eyes where there should be mouths, skin that moves like fabric, and spines blooming like flowers.

Daemons do not age, nor are they born \- they awaken, usually within a dream, a murder, or a moment of total despair. Their forms often reflect the nature of the trauma that birthed them.

## Origins and Mythology

Daemons hail from Nocturne. They are thought to be the byproduct of a broken universe, sloughed off like dead skin from the edges of sanity. Some scholars claim they were created during the Sundering when reality was first fractured. Others believe they are older still \- remnants of a failed idea, a scream buried beneath creation itself.

All are tethered to the Fields of Nocturne \- a realm of surreal dread and endless twilight where time dissolves and memory festers. It is said that each Daemon is bound by a wound in reality and that severing this connection is the only way to destroy one permanently.

## Culture and Society

Daemons do not form societies in any mortal sense \- but they do recognise each other, and in rare cases coalesce into warped hierarchies based on shared obsessions or themes of torment. These "courts" of madness are never permanent and often end in annihilation.

**Types of Daemonic Influence:** Possessors require mortal hosts, whispering promises or dread until the host invites them in \- these are the most common form. Manifested Daemons have stabilised enough to act without hosts, often becoming localised terrors or regional tyrants. Sovereigns are near-divine Daemons whose forms rival gods; legends name a few \- Azelthael the Blooming Maw, Chir-Gharun, the Choir of Teeth \- whose presence can unravel reality in wide swathes.

**Common Patterns of Behaviour:** Mimicry sees Daemons mimic mortal behaviours to manipulate, wearing the shape of a loved one or speaking in a child's voice. Powerful Daemons act as architects of madness, reshaping the world around them into impossible landscapes \- hollow cities, stairways into mirrors, forests of weeping statues. Some mortal cults practice entropy worship, revering Daemons as divine truths and embracing their unravelling touch as a sacrament.

**Language and Names:** Daemons speak *Demonic*, a language layered in whispers over reality \- their voices heard in glass cracking, the rustle of dry leaves, or the weeping of the dying. True Daemonic speech fractures minds; only trained exorcists or madmen can perceive it without breaking. True Daemonic names are mathematically unspeakable \- spoken aloud, they cause migraines, seizures, or spontaneous bleeding. They are often given names by mortals, typically descriptive (*"The Mawing One"*, *"Skinwalker of Barrow Hill"*, *"We Who Burn the Sky"*). Among themselves, names are conceptual, like *Hunger-as-Form*, *Joy Rendered Backward*, or *Ash of First Memory*.

## Homeland and Distribution

The Fields of Nocturne are a semi-mythical pocket-realm of endless dusk where gravity fails and memory echoes \- this is where Daemons gestate and return when exorcised, unless their tether is severed. Beyond Nocturne, Daemons often appear at manifestation sites saturated with pain, death, or despair \- mass graves, battlefields, cursed ruins, or haunted keeps.

## Relations with Other Races

No race trusts Daemons \- most actively hunt or seal them, and even the most amoral factions see them as existential threats. Still, some mortals give in to temptation, bargaining with Daemons and trading their bodies or souls for power; these contracts are never honoured. Extended contact with a Daemon often causes Daemon-Bleeding, a slow unravelling of one's sanity, voice, and memory \- the price of corruption paid by anyone who lingers too close.

## Faith and Philosophy

Daemons do not believe \- they are belief's negation. They exist to dismantle meaning, structure, and the illusion of safety. However, mortals interpret them religiously. The Cult of the Spiral Gate is a nihilistic faith that worships Daemons as agents of cosmic freedom, believing the universe must end to be reborn. The Order of the Ninth Seal is an ancient sect sworn to bind or destroy Daemons, using soul-binding rituals and sacrificial rites to seal rifts. The Solace-Born, a heretical offshoot of a major religion, believe Daemons are angels misunderstood by a flawed mortal world.
