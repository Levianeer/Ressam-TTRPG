# Humans

## *Waterborne Artefolk*

## Overview

Humanity is the most widespread and adaptable of the Artefolk, defined less by a singular origin than by their relentless will to survive and thrive. Though short-lived compared to other races, humans possess a flexibility that allows them to flourish in nearly every climate, every political structure, and every philosophical pursuit. They are engineers of cities, architects of empire, bearers of faith, and wielders of both sword and spirit. Whether united under common banners or fractured into resistance cells, humans are rarely idle, and their impact on Ressam \- whether through war, worship, or wonder \- can be felt in every corner of the world.

**Homeland:** Lustralis  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 to any Attribute, \-1 any Attribute  
**Age:** Adult \~18, Lives to \~70  
**Alignment:** Law  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 30 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Two of the following: Plethic, Batakluhk, Mafari or Zakhrafan  
**Starting Skills:** Gain \+1 rank in any one weapon Skill

### **Features**

**Adaptable:** Gain \+1 rank in any two different Skills of your choice, excluding weapon Skills (the Brawn & Melee and Finesse & Ranged categories).  
**Indomitable Spirit:** You have 3 Indomitable points, which you regain after a Field Rest or Long Rest. Immediately after you make an Attribute or Skill roll but before the DM declares the outcome, you can spend any number of remaining Indomitable points to increase the die result by 1 per point spent (to the maximum roll of the die). This only affects the die result, not any modifiers, and does not count as a natural roll for the purpose of critical hits or other effects that require a specific natural die result.

## Physical Appearance

Humans come in a wide range of skin tones, facial structures, and physiques depending on region and heritage. On average, they stand between 5 and 6 feet and tend toward lean or robust builds shaped more by labour and environment than any intrinsic racial trait. Their hair ranges from tight curls to straight strands, and eye colour varies widely, from deep brown to pale blue and even stormy grey, especially in those with stronger magical ties to water. Humans mature quickly, typically reaching adulthood by seventeen and rarely living beyond eighty without the aid of magic or medicine. Though they bear no horns, tails, or fangs, humans have always relied on something subtler: the ability to mirror, to adapt, to reshape themselves into whatever the moment demands.

## Origins and Mythology

Unlike the Aelves, whose lineage traces directly to the Painters, or the Orkhs, shaped in violence and divine fury, humans hold a more ambiguous place in myth. Some traditions claim they were born from the leftover breath of the Four Painters, intended not as an act of art, but as a happy accident of inspiration. Others, like the followers of Al'Nur Lehovil, see themselves as directly created by the divine essence of Lehovil \- the First Flame, the Last Oasis, the Source and the Destination.

More imperial myth, such as that preached by the Holy Reino Empire, ties humanity to the Four God-Dragons, who are said to have offered the first humans shelter and purpose after the Sundering. These traditions speak often of an approaching Second Sundering \- a judgement day that will separate the worthy from the lost. In contrast, cultures like Mafsoleios reach back even further, to an age of deathless kings and blood rites long forgotten by the surface world.

Whether bound by prophecy, spiritual duty, or ancestral memory, humans are always seeking meaning behind their presence in the world \- and they often find it in faith, philosophy, or fire.

## Culture and Society

Human societies are as varied as the seas they sail and the continents they claim. Though unified by a shared capacity for hope and reinvention, each human culture shapes that potential differently.

**Social Structure:** From the rigid hierarchies of the Holy Reino Empire to the loosely governed nomadic tribes of the Great Sand, humans are comfortable in both monarchies and anarchies. Where some peoples entrench themselves in tradition, others shed it like old skin in pursuit of something new. But whether empire or tribe, most human cultures place value on kinship, hospitality, and continuity \- what one generation builds, the next must preserve or improve.

**Traditions and Rites:** In Mafsoleios, death is a rite of passage honoured with reverent silence and necromantic ritual, while in Vaelrundar, a child's birth might be marked by casting runes in snow-melt and firelight. The Ederani Al'Nur treat guests as sacred, but consider theft a wound to the entire community. In the wet marshes of Batakluhk, funerals are rarely final, with guerilla fighters remembered in whispered chants long after their names vanish from record.

**Language and Names:** Each human culture maintains its own language or dialect, though the Gran Reino tongue has become a de facto trade language due to imperial spread. Names often reflect regional influence: desert-born Ederani may use long, flowing vowels, while Paigndo surnames carry sharp consonants and noble prefixes. Still, humans have a remarkable tendency to borrow, adopt, and even reinvent naming traditions to suit their own stories.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Humans are master builders and storytellers, known for their architecture, written histories, and spiritual artistry. The Halig people craft instruments that mimic birdsong and storm, while Vaelrundar carves magic into stone with ritual blades. The Gran Reino constructs cathedrals that stretch into the clouds \- symbols of divine power and human ingenuity alike.

## Homeland and Distribution

Humans are found in nearly every corner of Ressam, though they are most dominant in the Holy Reino Empire, which spans from the cold wetlands of Vaelrundar to the sun-washed shores of the Gran Mar. The Paigndo rule lush forests and fertile plains, while the Halig dwell in jungle-covered lands shared with the enigmatic Tembels. The Great Sand belongs to the Ederani Al'Nur, who traverse it in caravans bound by sun, wind, and faith.

The City-State of Mafsoleios stands apart \- an ancient hub of necromantic study and preserved tradition, older than the Empire that now watches it warily. Meanwhile, the people of Batakluhk continue their resistance against imperial assimilation, proving that not all migrations are peaceful, nor all occupations final.

Wherever they go, humans build. Cities, shrines, roads, memories \- monuments to both pride and perseverance.

## Relations with Other Races

Humans are both admired and resented across Ressam. Their adaptability and sheer numbers make them formidable allies and dangerous enemies. The Dwergaz respect their engineering but scoff at their inconsistency. Aelfs often find humanity's short memory and spiritual impatience frustrating. The Dzinari see them as dangerously curious, easily swayed by beauty and deceit alike. The Orkhs remember them mostly as prey or resistance.

Yet humans trade with nearly everyone, fight beside almost anyone, and have learned to speak a dozen tongues. To some, they are the face of empire. To others, they are freedom-seekers, rebel kings, and storm-born prophets. Stereotypes abound \- greedy, fickle, brave, brilliant \- but none fully contain the truth. Humanity is, above all, a race of contradictions.

## Faith and Philosophy

Human faith is as widespread and multifaceted as the people who hold it. The most dominant religion is Fe Alada, centred around the Four God-Dragons and widely practised within the Holy Reino Empire. It preaches judgement, order, and preparation for the Second Sundering \- a time when all souls will be measured.

Older still is the Light of Lehovil, the faith of the Desert Nomads. It is a monotheistic, oral tradition founded on acts of radical hospitality, moral integrity, and reverence for the divine source of all life. Its laws are as much social codes as spiritual doctrine, enforced not by temples but by the collective weight of communal memory.

In Mafsoleios, the line between death and worship blurs, and undeath is seen not as a curse but a continuation. Vaelrundar honors Orrvath, a runic god of ice, fate, and magical inheritance. Each human culture binds spirit to soil in different ways, but the through-line remains: to be human is to believe in something greater \- often fiercely, sometimes blindly, always deeply.

## Variations and Subgroups

Humanity splinters into dozens of distinct cultures, many of which have resisted or adapted to the cultural weight of the Gran Reino Empire. The Ederani Al'Nur maintain tribal independence and spiritual fire in the desert. The Vaelrundar safeguard their magical traditions in the frozen north. The Batakluhk remain locked in resistance, fighting to preserve a way of life already half-swallowed by imperial conquest. Meanwhile, the Paigndo and Halig represent willing and diplomatic integrations, bringing unique perspectives and values into the empire without losing their roots.

Even within the Empire itself, not all is unity \- tensions simmer, and ancient traditions persist beneath the surface.
