# Humans

## *Waterborne Artefolk*

## Overview

Humanity is the most widespread and adaptable of the Artefolk, defined less by a singular origin than by their relentless will to survive and thrive. Though short-lived compared to other races, humans possess a flexibility that allows them to flourish in nearly every climate, every political structure, and every philosophical pursuit. They are engineers of cities, architects of empire, bearers of faith, and wielders of both sword and spirit. Whether united under common banners or fractured into resistance cells, humans are rarely idle, and their impact on Ressam \- whether through war, worship, or wonder \- can be felt in every corner of the world.

**Homeland:** Lustralis  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 to any Attribute, \-1 any Attribute  
**Age:** Adult \~18, Lives to \~70  
**Alignment:** Law  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 30 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Two of the following: Plethic, Batakluhk, Mafari or Zakhrafan  
**Starting Skills:** Gain \+1 rank in any one weapon Skill

### **Features**

**Adaptable:** Gain \+1 rank in any two different Skills of your choice, excluding weapon Skills (the Brawn & Melee and Finesse & Ranged categories).  
**Indomitable Spirit:** You have 3 Indomitable points, which you regain after a Field Rest or Long Rest. Immediately after you make an Attribute or Skill roll but before the DM declares the outcome, you can spend any number of remaining Indomitable points to increase the die result by 1 per point spent (to the maximum roll of the die). This only affects the die result, not any modifiers, and does not count as a natural roll for the purpose of critical hits or other effects that require a specific natural die result.

## Physical Appearance

Humans span a wide range of skin tones, features, and builds shaped more by region and labour than by any intrinsic racial trait, with eye colour running from deep brown to stormy grey, the latter more common in those with stronger ties to water. They bear no horns, tails, or fangs, relying instead on something subtler: the ability to mirror, adapt, and reshape themselves into whatever the moment demands.

## Origins and Mythology

Humans hold an ambiguous place in myth, unlike the Painter-descended Aelves or the divinely-forged Orkhs. Some traditions claim they were born from the Four Painters' leftover breath, an accident of inspiration rather than design; the Al'Nur Lehovil instead hold that Lehovil, the First Flame and Last Oasis, made them directly. Imperial myth ties humanity to the Four God-Dragons, who sheltered the first humans after the Sundering and promised a coming Second Sundering to judge the worthy from the lost. Older still, Mafsoleios traces itself to an age of deathless kings and blood rites the surface world has long forgotten.

## Culture and Society

Human societies range from the rigid hierarchies of the Holy Reino Empire to the loosely governed tribes of the Great Sand, but most share a regard for kinship, hospitality, and continuity \- what one generation builds, the next preserves or improves.

**Traditions and Rites:** Mafsoleios marks death with reverent silence and necromantic ritual; Vaelrundar casts runes in snow-melt and firelight at a child's birth; the Ederani Al'Nur treat guests as sacred and theft as a wound to the whole community; Batakluhk's dead are mourned in whispered chants long after their names leave record.

**Language and Names:** Each culture keeps its own tongue, though Gran Reino has become the de facto trade language of empire. Names carry regional flavour \- flowing Ederani vowels, sharp Paigndo consonants and prefixes \- and humans readily borrow and reinvent naming traditions to suit their own stories.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Halig instruments mimic birdsong and storm; Vaelrundar carves magic into stone with ritual blades; Gran Reino cathedrals stretch into the clouds as monuments to both faith and ingenuity.

## Homeland and Distribution

Humans are found across nearly all of Ressam, most dominant in the Holy Reino Empire, spanning from Vaelrundar's cold wetlands to the sun-washed Gran Mar. The Paigndo hold lush forests and plains; the Halig share jungle lands with the Tembels; the Ederani Al'Nur traverse the Great Sand in caravans. Mafsoleios stands apart as an ancient hub of necromantic study older than the Empire that now watches it warily, while Batakluhk continues its resistance against imperial assimilation.

## Relations with Other Races

Humans are both admired and resented \- their adaptability and numbers make them formidable allies and dangerous enemies. Dwergaz respect their engineering but scoff at their inconsistency; Aelves find their short memory and spiritual impatience frustrating; the Dzinari see them as dangerously curious; the Orkhs remember them mostly as prey or resistance. Yet humans trade and fight beside nearly everyone, and no single stereotype \- greedy, fickle, brave, brilliant \- fully contains them.

## Faith and Philosophy

Fe Alada, centred on the Four God-Dragons, dominates the Holy Reino Empire, preaching judgement and preparation for the coming Second Sundering. Older still is the Light of Lehovil, the Desert Nomads' monotheistic oral tradition of radical hospitality and moral integrity, its laws enforced by communal memory rather than temples. In Mafsoleios, undeath is a continuation of worship, not a curse; Vaelrundar honours Orrvath, a runic god of ice and fate. Each culture binds spirit to soil differently, but to be human is, fiercely or blindly, to believe in something greater.

## Variations and Subgroups

Humanity splinters into dozens of cultures. The Ederani Al'Nur keep tribal independence and spiritual fire in the desert; the Vaelrundar guard magical tradition in the frozen north; the Batakluhk remain in armed resistance against imperial conquest; the Paigndo and Halig represent willing, diplomatic integration into the Empire without losing their roots. Even within the Empire, unity is a surface \- old tensions still simmer beneath it.
