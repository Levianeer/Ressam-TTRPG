# Feliids

## *Windborne cursed of Water \- Luxuria (Lust)*

## Overview

The Feliids are a cursed offshoot of the Aelfen, once a solemn and tradition-bound people transformed by divine wrath into something wild, vibrant, and unrestrained. With feline ears, claws, and tails, they now live fast, laugh louder, and love fiercely \- rejecting the rigidity of their former lives for a culture of instinct, passion, and survival. Though many see them as impulsive or decadent, the Feliids have forged a powerful, multicultural maritime empire known as the Tagarian Coalition, whose glittering cities, opulent wealth, and charismatic rulers belie their primal roots.

**Homeland:** Felnis, Kalitona, Sokhara  
**Starting Attributes:** \+1 DEX, \+1 CHA, \-1 MIND  
**Age:** Adult \~16, Lives to \~60  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 35 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Tagaraal, Plethic  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 Acrobatics, \+1 Perception

### **Features**

**Low-light Vision:** You treat dim light as bright light within 60 ft.  
**Natural Weapons:** You gain a Claw attack that deals 1d4+DEX slashing damage (finesse).  
**Catfall:** You take half damage from falls, you do not go prone when taking falling damage.  
**Feline Agility:** Once per turn, when you take the Move action, you may double your movement speed for that turn. You cannot use this feature again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns.

## Physical Appearance

Feliids are lithe and short compared to humans, built for climbing, balancing, and sudden bursts of motion, with soft feline ears, retractable claws, and long furred tails. Skin tones range from dusky olive to pale cream with faint Aelfen undertones, and eyes run vivid gold, violet, or emerald, reflecting faintly in the dark. They burn out intensely rather than age gracefully, spending their short lives chasing sensation, purpose, and legacy.

## Origins and Mythology

Feliids were once a solemn Aelfen enclave on Chossris, devoted to ritual purity and philosophical mastery \- until their disdain for the primal and spontaneous drew an Earth God's wrath. Their punishment twisted them toward the feline archetype: minds sharpened but erratic, lives shortened, bodies changed. Where the Aelfen call it a tragic curse, the Feliids call it liberation \- to them, survival is beautiful, chaos is sacred, and every day is a gift clawed from the world.

## Culture and Society

**Social Structure:** The Tagarian Coalition unites three city-states \- Felnis, Kalitona, and Sokhara \- each nominally ruled by a *Koté* figurehead, though real power sits with the Council of Sages. Wealth and wit outweigh lineage; a beggar can rise on ambition alone, and even nobles must keep proving their worth.

**Traditions and Rites:** Births are feasted and danced; deaths are marked by wild festivals where mourners sing the departed's name until sunrise. Ritual duels over love, honour, or insult are common but rarely fatal, and tattoos, jewellery, and masks mark triumphs and loyalties. Worship is just as informal \- shrines appear in markets, homes, and aboard ships rather than temples, and every Feliid is expected to find a patron god through personal ritual, tattoo, or talisman.

**Language and Names:** *Tagaraal* is rapid and tonal. Names are chosen to reflect personality or aspiration, often gaining a nickname or title with renown (*Whisker-Wind*, *Cut-Claw*), and full names typically carry a clan or city affiliation.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Feliids excel at glasswork, dyeing, and shipbuilding \- fleet-identifying sail patterns and ceremonial masks are prized cultural works \- and prise invention, new designs earning their creators instant celebrity.

## Homeland and Distribution

Feliids originated on Chossris but now dominate much of the Gran Mar's coastal trade. Felnis, the ancestral capital, is famed for academies and tradition; Kalitona, on the Alhona peninsula, is the centre of naval power and mercantile wealth; Sokhara, in the cold jungles of Gelidia, is the main stop when trading with Dwergaz lands. Colonies and hybrid settlements dot Aurkhan and Gelidia beyond the three capitals, with occasional nomadic groups further inland.

## Relations with Other Races

Feliids are famously cosmopolitan, with one exception: relations with the Aelfen remain bitter, the Feliids resenting their progenitors' pity, the Aelfen mourning what they see as a fallen people.

Their least likely friendship is their warmest: the dour Dwergaz of Tor'Az, the only race in Ressam that wears no mask \- and among a people of performers, an honest audience is the rarest luxury. Coalition fleets carry Dwergaz goods to every coast, and Feliid captains, whose bright lives gutter out in sixty years, buy the one thing they can't make for themselves: their names entered into Terrevault's deathless tomes, to be remembered long after the songs end.

Outsiders underestimate them, mistaking seductive charm for weakness \- their true strength lies not in standing armies but in vast fleets and mercenary networks.
