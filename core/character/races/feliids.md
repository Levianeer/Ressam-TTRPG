# Feliids

## *Windborne cursed of Water \- Luxuria (Lust)*

## Overview

The Feliids are a cursed offshoot of the Aelfen, once a solemn and tradition-bound people transformed by divine wrath into something wild, vibrant, and unrestrained. With feline ears, claws, and tails, they now live fast, laugh louder, and love fiercely \- rejecting the rigidity of their former lives for a culture of instinct, passion, and survival. Though many see them as impulsive or decadent, the Feliids have forged a powerful, multicultural maritime empire known as the Tagarian Coalition, whose glittering cities, opulent wealth, and charismatic rulers belie their primal roots.

## Physical Appearance

Feliids are generally shorter than humans, averaging around 5 to 6 feet, with lithe, agile builds ideal for climbing, balancing, and sudden bursts of motion. Their most distinguishing features are their soft feline ears perched atop their heads, retractable clawed fingers and toes, and long, expressive tails covered in fur. Skin tones range widely, from dusky olive to pale cream, often with subtle undertones that hint at their Aelfen ancestry.

Eye colours are vivid \- often gold, violet, or emerald \- and reflect faintly in the dark. Their hair tends to be thick and unruly, often streaked with bold natural colors like red, silver, or black. Feliids mature quickly, reaching adulthood by 16 and rarely living beyond 60, though the most renowned sages and aristocrats have been known to reach 90. They age gracefully but burn out intensely, often spending their short lives in pursuit of sensation, purpose, and legacy.

## Origins and Mythology

According to both Aelfen myth and Feliid oral history, the Feliids were once a solemn Aelfen enclave on the island of Chossris, dedicated to ritual purity, philosophical mastery, and the reverence of wind and ceremony. But their disdain for the primal and the spontaneous drew the ire of an Earth God \- perhaps one of the ancient elemental spirits born of the Four Painters. As punishment, their village was transformed: minds sharpened but made erratic, lives shortened, and bodies twisted toward the feline archetype.

While some Aelfen believe this to be a tragic curse, the Feliids themselves see it as liberation. Freed from endless waiting and ponderous tradition, they now embrace the raw joy and sorrow of the moment. This worldview has deeply shaped their faith, society, and identity: to the Feliid, survival is beautiful, chaos is sacred, and every day is a gift to be clawed from the world.

## Culture and Society

**Social Structure:** The Feliids are led by the Tagarian Coalition, a loose alliance of three major city-states \- Felnis, Kalitona, and Sokhara \- each claiming to be the heart of Feliid civilisation. These cities are ruled nominally by the *Koté*, a princely figurehead, though true power lies with the Council of Sages, composed of merchant princes, guild leaders, and naval lords. The Feliids prize ambition and charisma over lineage; wealth and wit can elevate a beggar to power, while even nobles must prove their worth in the ever-churning social tide.

**Traditions and Rites:** Feliid rites are vibrant, sensual affairs. Births are celebrated with feasting and dance; death is marked by wild festivals, where the mourners wear bright colours and sing the departed's name until sunrise. Ritual duels \- whether over love, honour, or insult \- are common, but rarely fatal. Tattoos, jewellery, and masks are important cultural symbols, marking everything from personal triumphs to family loyalties.

**Language and Names:** The Feliid tongue is a rapid, fluid language rich in tone and nuance, known as *Tagaraal*. Names are expressive and chosen to reflect personality or aspiration, often with a poetic or performative flair. Many adopt nicknames or titles as they gain renown, such as *Whisker-Wind*, *Cut-Claw*, or *Moonstep*. Full names often include clan or city affiliation, like *Sahari of Kalitona* or *Tyvek Moonstep of Sokhara*.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Feliids are famed artisans, especially in glasswork, fabric dyeing, and shipbuilding. Their sails are woven with colourful patterns that identify fleets at sea, and their ceremonial masks are considered masterpieces of culture. Music and dance are integral to Feliid life, with every festival or council meeting punctuated by performance. Invention is also prised \- new designs, devices, and weapons often earn their creators instant celebrity.

## Homeland and Distribution

Feliids originated on the Island of Chossris, but now dominate much of the Gran Mar's coastal trade through the Tagarian Coalition. Their three primary cities are Felnis, the ancestral capital of Chossris, famed for its academies, temples, and tradition; Kalitona, a bustling port on the Alhona peninsula, centre of naval power and mercantile wealth; and Sokhara, a cold but bustling trade hub in Gelidia, known for exotic goods and deadly politics, the main stop when travelling to Dwergaz land.

They also maintain colonies, ports, and outposts across Aurkhan, Gelidia, and even close to Dwergaz lands, often in hybrid or multicultural settlements. While typically coastal or riverine, some nomadic groups exist inland, especially in disputed or wild territories.

## Relations with Other Races

Feliids are famously cosmopolitan and open-handed \- with one glaring exception. Their relations with the Aelfen remain bitter, marked by ancient disdain and modern skirmishes along the coasts of Aurkhan. The Feliids resent the pity and judgement of their progenitors, while the Aelfen mourn what they see as a fallen people.

Their warmest friendship is their least likely: the dour, sunless Dwergaz of the Tor'Az. To the Feliids - whose highest god governs mask and merit - the Dwergaz are irresistible for one reason above all: they are the only race in Ressam that wears no mask. Blunt, humorless, incapable of flattery, a Dwergaz's praise means exactly what it says, and among a people of performers there is no rarer luxury than an honest audience. The Coalition's fleets carry Dwergaz goods to every coast, its marines fight where Dwergaz gold is better spent than Dwergaz lives, and its captains - whose bright lives gutter out in sixty years - buy the one thing no Feliid can make: permanence, their names entered in the deathless tomes of Terrevault to be remembered long after the songs end. The Feliids, who get closer to the stone folk than anyone, have heard the rumors about what the Dwergaz hide underground - and being professionals of persona, they defend those rumors on the Dwergaz's behalf, cheerfully, without ever having been asked. The Dwergaz have never thanked them. Both peoples consider the matter settled.
Their reputation varies \- some see them as seductive tricksters, others as cunning survivors. Many outsiders underestimate their military power, unaware that their true strength lies not in standing armies but in their vast fleets and mercenary networks.

## Faith and Philosophy

The Tagarian Faith is a rich and multifaceted pantheon organised into six themed divisions, each representing a different facet of life. **For the full pantheon, see the Religions chapter.** It is a deeply practical and spiritual system \- celebrating survival, pleasure, connection, and purpose. Every Feliid is expected to find their patron or path among the gods, often marked by personal rituals, tattoos, or talismans.

Notable among these deities are the God of Mask and Merit, central to Feliid culture and governing status and public persona; the God of the Labyrinth Within, patron of those who seek personal transformation and enlightenment; and the God of Celebration and Mourning, venerated in all major rites of passage.

Worship is informal and often celebratory \- marked by storytelling, costume, food, and flame. There are no temples in the traditional sense; instead, shrines appear in markets, homes, and aboard ships.
