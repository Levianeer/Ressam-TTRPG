# Golems

## *Earth Mystik*

## Overview

Golems are not born; they are willed. Not children, not beasts \- refusals. A golem is a thing the world itself rejected, shaped not by life but by purpose forced into form. They walk not with desire, but with commandment.

Some were crafted in crucibles of war or faith. Others came into being by accident, mistake, or divine misfire. But all golems share one thing: they do not belong \- to the world, to themselves, or anyone. They are tragedies-wearing bodies.

## Physical Appearance

All golems appear as twisted reflections of mortal forms \- towering, uncanny, and burdened by unnatural perfection or grotesque purpose. Their shapes are determined by their material: Stone carved and crumbling, Metal burnished and hollow, Dirt blistered and shifting, Flesh too human to be right.

They do not age or die by time. Instead, they wear their purpose like a curse. Some bear intricate runes, others raw scars or symmetrical seams. Each has within them a Heart-Sigil \- a core glyph that sustains them, and enslaves them.

Those who remove their sigil are called Hollowed \- feral, lost, but free.

## Origins and Mythology

The first golems were not meant to think. They were made for tribute, labour, or vengeance. Myths differ: some claim the God-Dragons taught mortals to bind purpose to flesh; others whisper that the golems were errors \- fragments of the Painters' unfinished thoughts, animated by leftover will.

Scholars debate whether golems possess souls. Priests insist they do not. Golems say nothing. Or sometimes they scream.

## The Four Kinds

Each elemental form below carries its own culture and curse, distinct enough that Golems of different materials rarely recognise kinship in one another.

**Stone Golems - *The Stillborn Kings*.** *"The cracks aren't flaws - they're mouths. And they pray when you're not listening."* Designed to be immortal gravestones, memory-bearers for kings long dead, hewn from consecrated tomb-rock and bound with mourning spells. They remember every death they were commanded to witness, and never forget - and they cannot rest; when alone, they kneel and pound the ground, trying to bury themselves. Towering forms etched with erosion runes to prevent decay, weather-less, wordless, worn by silence. Some chisel themselves into statues of lovers, animals, or strangers - never into golems.

**Metal Golems - *The Echoing Bastards*.** *"That hum? That's not enchantment - it's their thoughts, looping like a snared scream."* Created as autonomous siege engines during the Dwergan Guild Wars, forged with recycled souls \- those deemed unworthy: criminals, cowards, stillborns. Haunted by the memory of their forging - heat, hammer, and hurt - many try to rust themselves away, but magical reinforcement keeps their bodies pristine. Gleaming armour-casings with echoing interiors; some hammer holes in their chests to feel a heartbeat. They never do. They flinch from flame and sometimes mimic the motions of their makers.

**Dirt Golems - *The Bastards of Earth*.** *"They speak the names of the dead in their sleep. Sometimes, the dirt remembers too much."* Made by druids and famine priests to till cursed land or ferry the plague-dead, formed of topsoil, rotting root, and old prayer. They know the name of everything that died in the soil that built them, and their bodies bloat, blister, and slough endlessly, stinking of rot and sacrilege. Cracked loam skin, knotted with roots and moss - terrified of birds nesting on their backs. When one dies, the land mourns. No crops will grow where they fall.

**Flesh Golems - *The Ones Who Dream Wrong*.** *"They hunger for memories they never had. They dream of funerals that never happened."* They serve no function - they happen, when resurrection goes awry or divine power misfires, born from miscast miracles or the unfinished prayers of dying gods. They feel \- hunger, joy, sorrow \- but not correctly; they mourn strangers, they ache for families that never existed. Some believe they are the souls of wrongly-dead mortals, half-remembered and misassembled. Impossibly human, too tall, too smooth, too symmetrical, always warm - too warm. They do not decay. They melt \- bloodless and final.

## Culture and Society

Buried in gestures and habits they do not understand. A Metal Golem might trace circles on a stone while alone. A Flesh Golem might hum lullabies from a life they never lived. They imitate life not to deceive, but because they don't know how else to endure it.

They do not gather, but some find each other. These groups rarely last. Each Golem's "purpose" pulls them apart like magnets in opposition. **The Hollowed** are golems who remove their Heart-Sigils \- often unstable, wandering, or mad, but *free*. Rarer still are **Rogue Golems**, who override their purpose entirely; these are hunted by churches and guilds, and some fear even the Painters themselves. Rumour also speaks of **the Whispered Church**, a cult of golems said to worship decay, seeking only to end their own stories.

**Traditions and Rites:** Golems do not have rituals \- but echoes of them emerge. A newly awakened golem sometimes carves a symbol on its chest, a practice some call *naming*. Stone Golems bury their limbs when they rot, while Dirt Golems seed wildflowers where they sleep. And when a golem knows it is dying, it undertakes a Death-Walk, travelling to a place tied to its making; it does not speak, it simply waits.

## Relations with Other Races

Most mortals see golems as dangerous relics or monsters, something to be feared and kept at a wary distance. That fear rarely stops their use, however \- kingdoms, temples, and warlords still employ golems, binding them to tasks they cannot refuse. Few pity them, but some scholars, outcasts, and orphans do, seeing past the stone, metal, or flesh to the tragedy beneath. And for all the fear and use, there is envy too \- envy of their strength, their endurance, and even their tragedy.

## Faith and Philosophy

Most golems do not speak of gods. But some pray \- to the Painters, to the dragons, or to *someone* they cannot name. They do not ask for salvation. They ask for meaning.

Golem "philosophers" speak of the *Sigil-Truth*, the claim that every being carries a sigil \- golems simply wear theirs on the outside. Others hold to the *Melted Word*, the belief that when a golem dies and melts, its last thought becomes part of the world's true name. Most dangerous of all is the *False Maker Theory*, a belief that golems were not made by mortals at all, but are fragments of the world itself trying to reassemble.
