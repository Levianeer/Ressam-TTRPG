# Dzinari

## *Wind Mystik*

## Overview

The Dzinari are pale, uncannily tall beings, Aelf-like in silhouette but far more alien in demeanour, for whom secrets function as both currency and craft. They treat a held secret as property and a traded one as a weapon, and rarely say anything plainly that could be implied instead. Most Dzinari answer to the **Council of Murmurs**, the shifting governing body of the Chorus of Murmurs; a rogue faction, the Gilded Hwispian Maw, answers instead to the Devaraja and no longer recognises the Council's authority.

**Homeland:** Gelidia  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 CHA, \+1 ARC, \-1 STR, \-1 END  
**Age:** Adult \~100, Immortal  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 30 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Dzinariel, and 1 of any other Language  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 in any two: Arcane Lore, Insight, or Deception

### **Features**

**Mystikal Heritage:** You have advantage on Wards vs charmed and fear effects. When killed, the soul explosively returns to primordial energy. The creature can only be restored back to life by means of a god's miracle.  
**Meditative Trance:** You don't sleep, instead you meditate 4 hours for the benefits of a Field Rest or Long Rest.  
**Windform:** Once per Field Rest or Long Rest, as an Major Action, become incorporeal wind for up to 1 minute. While in Windform: you are immune to nonmagical physical damage, you can fly up to 30 feet per turn, you cannot attack or cast spells, and you cannot interact with physical objects. The form ends early if you choose, if you take magical damage, or if you become unconscious.

## Physical Appearance

The Dzinari are lithe and statuesque, standing a head taller than most Aelves, with elongated limbs and unnervingly perfect, studied posture. Their pallid skin holds a faint glassy translucence, shifting silver and violet under starlight, and their mask-like faces are dominated by swirling, never-blinking eyes of liquid silver or storm grey. Every motion is deliberate, almost too slow, until it breaks into sudden, quick violence.

Before their **Pratidhvani**, young Dzinari exist in a liminal, semi-intangible state \- drifting like smoke, passing through walls and people, with no voice, no weight, and no full claim to reality until the rite is complete.

## Origins and Mythology

The Dzinari trace their lineage to before language \- not born of the Painters' brush but of their whispers, secrets too dangerous to forget and too vital to erase, given form in the liminal spaces between Nocturne and the mortal realm. Their floating city, Aegislith Veil, is built from these secrets directly: castles of frozen sound, whispered bargains, and bound confessions. Some Dzinari hold they were once part of the Painters' voice, split and scattered like dust across the stars; others, that they are the whispers left behind by gods too ashamed to speak.

## Culture and Society

Dzinari society runs on influence, riddles, and silence, where standing belongs not to rulers but to whoever knows what others wish to hide. A **Kula** is the basic social unit \- members bound together by each having gifted the others a secret never spoken again. Young Dzinari are guided through illusion and parable toward their Pratidhvani by a **Mayamandala**, a teaching order built around staged hallucination. Above both sits the **Council of Murmurs**, which convenes in the **Granthi-Sabha**, an ever-shifting court whose halls rearrange with the weight of unspoken truths \- governing the Chorus of Murmurs nation for every Dzinari who still recognises its authority.

Not all do. The **Gilded Hwispian Maw** broke from the Council generations ago and answers instead to the **Devaraja**, an ancient Dzinari who commands their elite **Golden Choir** by telepathy alone. The Council of Murmurs treats the Maw as heretical; the Maw treats the Council as a body too cautious to matter.

**Traditions and Rites:** At adolescence, a Dzinari hunts their own voice \- a spectral **Chhaya** that mocks its pursuer with riddles \- in the **Pratidhvani**; until captured they remain intangible and silent, and failure means exile as a **Nishabdavata**, a whispering wind haunting the Gelidian tundra. True names are never spoken publicly \- Dzinari go by metaphors instead (*"The Thorn Behind the Wind"*) \- and promises are carved physically into the city through **Pratijna-nirmana**, breaking one reshaping Aegislith itself, sometimes violently. Their endless social contest, the **Rahasyalila**, trades in collected secrets; revealing one gifted by another is punished by dissolution, the stripping of voice, name, and body.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** The Dzinari treat all creation as echo-sculpture: sound shaped by breath and silence into crystalline forms in their **Vayanashala**, buildings of solidified secrets that murmur faintly; an **Asthi-sangita**, whose vibrations bypass the ear entirely; and **Jiva-shloka**, texts readable only when spoken aloud in sequence.

## Homeland and Distribution

Most Dzinari loyal to the Council hail from Aegislith Veil, a drifting metropolis of frozen resonance floating over Gelidia's glacial plains, slipping partly into the Fields of Nocturne with the turning of secrets and the breaking of oaths. Its Vayanashala are whisper-forges where sound becomes structure; the Granthi-Sabha is its shifting parliament; and the **Sakshishila**, a sentient monolith, records every vow spoken within the city. Travellers rarely find Aegislith Veil on purpose \- most are led there by a forbidden secret, a broken oath, or a deal struck with a Dzinari wanderer. The Gilded Hwispian Maw, by contrast, holds court at Svarnanagaram on Trere itself \- the old imperial seat of the fallen pre-Sundering empire the Maw traces its legitimacy back to, inherited whole rather than built, and kept deliberately far less grand and esoteric than Aegislith Veil.

## Relations with Other Races

Other cultures find the Dzinari unnerving \- their obsession with secrets breeds mistrust, and their intangible youths are often mistaken for ghosts. They trade in information, illusions, and forgotten time rather than coin, rarely attend summits in person (sending sound-bound shadow echoes instead), and wage war through false intelligence and dream infiltration rather than open battle. The Gilded Hwispian Maw is regarded very differently: currently at war with nearly every major power in Ressam, it deals in alchemical slavery and naked expansion, and even other Dzinari treat its Golden Choir \- stripped of all capacity for communication, obedient only to the Devaraja \- as a symbol of how far the Maw has strayed.

## Faith and Philosophy

To the Dzinari, truth is neither sacred nor profane \- it is a shape to be played with. Identity is fluid, language is creation, and the self is a performance: they do not pray, they perform, masking truths in riddles and whispering vows to the wind.

## Variations and Subgroups

The defining split among Dzinari is political, not physical. The great majority answer to the Council of Murmurs and are raised through the Kula and Mayamandala system described above \- patient, indirect, playing a long game measured in generations. The Gilded Hwispian Maw rejects all of it: legitimacy through inherited empire rather than council consensus, the Devaraja's word in place of the Granthi-Sabha's endless deliberation, and open expansion in place of the long game. A Council Dzinari regards a Maw Dzinari as a cautionary tale; a Maw Dzinari regards a Council Dzinari as wasted potential.
