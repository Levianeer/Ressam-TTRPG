# Dzinari

## *Wind Mystik*

## Overview

The Dzinari are elusive, pale beings of unsettling poise \- uncannily tall, graceful, and Aelf-like in silhouette but far more alien in demeanour. Known for their honeyed words and cryptic games, they treat manipulation as art, secrets as currency, and knowledge as both blade and balm. Their every movement is calculated, their every word layered with implication. To converse with a Dzinari is to enter a maze with no walls.

**Homeland:** Gelidia  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 CHA, \+1 ARC, \-1 STR, \-1 END  
**Age:** Adult \~100, Immortal  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Medium (5-6 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 30 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Dzinariel, and 1 of any other Language  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 in any two: Arcane Lore, Insight, or Deception

### **Features**

**Mystikal Heritage:** You have advantage on Wards vs charmed and fear effects. When killed, the soul explosively returns to primordial energy. The creature can only be restored back to life by means of a god's miracle.  
**Meditative Trance:** You don't sleep, instead you meditate 4 hours for the benefits of a Field Rest or Long Rest.  
**Windform:** Once per Field Rest or Long Rest, as an Major Action, become incorporeal wind for up to 1 minute. While in Windform: you are immune to nonmagical physical damage, you can fly up to 30 feet per turn, you cannot attack or cast spells, and you cannot interact with physical objects. The form ends early if you choose, if you take magical damage, or if you become unconscious.

## Physical Appearance

The Dzinari are lithe and statuesque, standing a head taller than most Aelves at around seven feet, with elongated limbs and eerily perfect posture that seems studied rather than natural. Their pallid skin bears a faint, glassy translucence \- like moonlight filtered through ice \- rippling subtly with shifting hues of silver and violet under starlight.

Their faces are mask-like and serene, with high cheekbones, elongated features, and lips that rarely move. Most unsettling are their eyes: swirling voids of liquid silver or storm grey, constantly in motion yet never blinking, as if dreaming through the veil of waking life.

Every motion they make is too smooth \- a gliding precision that slips just past the boundaries of what feels human. They move like dancers caught mid-performance: slow, poised, deliberate \- until the moment erupts into sudden violence. Their combat is a shiver-inducing contradiction: crouched in insect-like stances one moment, then lunging in spinning, liquid arcs the next, weapons arcing like the trail of a comet. Watching a Dzinari fight is like witnessing a performance half-remembered from a dream \- beautiful, terrible, and wrong in some ineffable way.

Before they undergo their sacred Rite of Echoes, young Dzinari exist in a liminal state \- semi-intangible and dreamlike. They drift like smoke, their outlines stuttering or stretching unnaturally as they move. They pass through walls, objects, and even people, leaving behind faint afterimages and echoing dissonances when touched. Until the Rite is complete, they have no voice, no weight, and no full claim to reality.

## Origins and Mythology

The Dzinari trace their lineage to a time before language \- a race born not of the Painters' brush but of their whispers, the stray musings and half-finished thoughts given form in the liminal spaces between Realms. Where other races came from story and substance, the Dzinari emerged from secrets too dangerous to forget and too vital to erase.

Their city, Aegislith Veil, is built from these very secrets \- castles of frozen sound, whispered bargains, and bound confessions. Some believe the Dzinari were originally servants or reflections of Thryzzaral, fragments of unspoken truth made flesh.

## Culture and Society

Dzinari society is a swirling network of influence, riddles, and silence. Whisper-Kin are social units based not on blood but on oaths of secrecy and mutual revelation, and each member must gift the kin a secret no one else knows, which is never spoken again. The Silk Ring is an order of illusion-weaving mentors who guide young Dzinari through parables, dreams, and hallucinations until they are ready for the Rite of Echoes. The Riddle-Knot is the seat of their ever-shifting political court \- a maze-like structure whose halls rearrange based on the weight of unspoken truths. Power belongs not to rulers, but to those who know what others wish to hide.

**Traditions and Rites:** Upon reaching adolescence, a Dzinari must undergo the Rite of Echoes, hunting their own voice \- a spectral entity called a Vexrift, which teases and mocks its pursuer with riddles. Until captured, the Dzinari remains intangible and silent, and failure results in exile as a Sighing Silence \- a wandering, whispering wind cursed to haunt the Gelidian tundra. True names are never spoken aloud; publicly, Dzinari adopt metaphors or enigmas (*e.g., "The Thorn Behind the Wind" or "Unfolding Horizon"*), and only those bound in absolute trust may know one's true name. Promises are physical acts in Dzinari society as well, carved into the structures of their home through Oath-Forging, and to break an oath is to reshape the city \- sometimes violently. The Game of Whispers is a never-ending contest of social intrigue, where influence is amassed by collecting secrets and disarming opponents with words alone, and outsiders frequently mistake it for casual conversation \- until it's far too late. These traditions carry heavy taboos: speaking one's true name in public is considered soul suicide, revealing a secret gifted by another is punishable by dissolution \- the stripping of voice, name, and body, until nothing remains \- and children caught mimicking adult voices before their rite are muzzled with cords of silent thread, unable to speak or hear until their penance is served.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** The Dzinari consider all creation to be echo-sculpture \- shaping ephemeral things into lasting beauty, with sound as their medium and breath and silence as their tools. Echo-Sculpture shapes whispers into crystalline forms, fragile but impossibly sharp or resonant. Frozen Architecture produces buildings of translucent material formed from solidified secrets, each wall murmuring faintly and each corner echoing with emotion. The Opera of the Bones is a performance art where voices resonate so low they bypass ears and vibrate in the skeleton, and the more discomfort a piece creates, the more successful it is considered. Living Verses are poems woven into objects or architecture, readable only when specific words are spoken aloud in sequence.

## Homeland and Distribution

Most Dzinari hail from Aegislith Veil, a drifting metropolis of frozen resonance, forever floating over the glacial plains of Gelidia. The city exists partially within the Fields of Nocturne, slipping between planes with the turning of secrets and the breaking of oaths. The Loom-Halls are residential complexes and whisper-forges where sound becomes structure. The Riddle-Knot is the shifting parliament of riddlers, oath-binders, and truth-crafters. The Reflecting Core is a sentient monolith that records every spoken vow within Aegislith, its mirrored walls rippling with engraved promises, punishments, and truths long since forgotten.

Travellers rarely find Aegislith on purpose. Most are led there by accident \- lured in after speaking a forbidden secret, breaking an oath, or making a deal with a Dzinari wanderer.

## Relations with Other Races

Other cultures find the Dzinari unnerving, a mystique bred of mistrust: their obsession with secrets and riddles breeds suspicion, and their intangible youths are often mistaken for spirits or ghosts. In trade, Dzinari will exchange information, illusions, or even moments of forgotten time for artefacts they consider "emotionally resonant," and they rarely deal in coin. Diplomatically, they never attend summits in person; instead, they send shadow echoes, sound-bound replicas that speak in their stead and dissolve once the deal is struck. Warfare is rarely direct \- instead, they destabilise enemy command through false intelligence, fractured oaths, or dream infiltration, and when they do fight, it's with unnatural grace and terrifying unpredictability.

## Faith and Philosophy

To the Dzinari, truth is neither sacred nor profane \- it is a shape, and shapes are meant to be played with. Their belief system, if it can be called that, centres on the idea that identity is fluid, language is creation, and that the self is a performance.

They do not pray in the traditional sense. Instead, they perform: truths masked in riddles, names hidden in poems, and vows whispered to the wind.

Some among them believe they were once part of the original Painter's voice, split and scattered like dust across the stars. Others believe they are the whispers left behind by gods too ashamed to speak.
