# Dwergaz

## *Earthborne Artefolk*

## Overview

The Dwergaz are a race of stoic, earth-bound beings - cut, not born. Every Dwergaz was hewn by his clan's own rites from a living vein of stone, a son of the Great Mother and of no one else. The veins yield slowly, and fewer with each passing century, and everything the Dwergaz are follows from that arithmetic: a people who cannot replace their dead build machines to fall in their place, walls that outlast sieges, and wealth enough to make war against them unprofitable. Masters of craft and keepers of grudges, they value endurance over ephemeral glory, building not just fortresses but legacies. Though their gruff exteriors and xenophobic tendencies alienate outsiders, their subterranean cities thrive where trade, artistry, and vengeance intertwine - all beneath the rule of the Tor'Az Triumvirate, a guild-throne of unmatched industrial and military might.

**Weapon Training:** You gain \+1 Axes & Hammers, and \+1 in either Pistols or Long Guns.  
**Darkvision:** You treat nonmagical darkness as dim light up to 60 ft.  
**Dwarven Resilience:** You have advantage on END Ward against poison and take half damage from poison.  
**Grudge Bearer:** Once per Field Rest or Long Rest, when a creature deals damage to you or an ally, harms your property, or breaks a formal agreement with you, you may declare a grudge against them (no action required). You gain \+2 to attack and damage rolls against your grudge target. The grudge ends when the target dies, or when you declare a new grudge. You can have only one grudge at a time.

## Physical Appearance

Dwergaz are broad, compact, and exclusively male \- the Mother has never cut a daughter free. Skin runs granite grey to clay red, eyes gleam like polished gemstones, and faint mineral veining traces the grain of the seam that cut them. Vein-kin can recognise each other by this grain at a glance, and reading a stranger's grain is considered as personal as reading his letters.

A newly-cut Dwergaz has blunt features and an unset voice. Over his first four decades his features harden and sharpen, and adulthood is marked by a first craft or oath.

## Origins and Mythology

The Dwergaz are sons of the Great Mother, the living moon whose slow passes knead the deep stone of Ressam. Her essence pools in the veins from which every Dwergaz is cut. Clans disagree on which of the twin moons she is: three rival creeds, the Trailing, the Leading, and the Embrace, each hold a different position, and the dispute is old enough that guild wars have been fought over when the Cutting rites she governs may be performed.

Orthodox myth holds that Melacha, the Mother's eldest son, cut the first Dwergaz free from the stone at Karak's Spine. Their souls carried Nekama's oath, "To overcome is to remember," and they tunnelled to the world's heart to meet Mischar, who gave them the first trade-scales.

The veins, called the Mat'dûn, are finite and slowly running dry. Some hold this as a trial of endurance, others as punishment, others as simply the way of things. Heretics claim the race was instead forged in a betrayed god's wrath, and that the failing veins are a curse completing itself.

## Culture and Society

**Social Structure:** Guilds hold power, led by the Tor'Az Triumvirate. Dwergaz recognise no bloodlines; kinship follows the vein a Dwergaz was cut from, and status is earned through craft or combat.

**The Cutting:** New Dwergaz are hewn from the Mat'dûn by rite, timed to each clan's lunar creed. A birthing-vein's location is a hold's most closely guarded secret; revealing one to an outsider is the only crime in Khazduul with no fixed punishment.

**Stonebinding:** An oath-bond between two Dwergaz, whether shield-brothers, craft-partners, or lovers \- the rite makes no distinction. The pair carve interlocking rings from a single geode.

**The Returning:** The dead are crushed to gemstone dust. The honoured are returned to the Mat'dûn; the rest are set into the clan hall's mortar.

**Grudge Annals:** The Bookkeepers of Terrevault record every slight in lead-bound tomes. Dwergaz see this as accounting rather than spite: a vein-cut life can never be replaced, so a Dwergaz death is treated as a debt no repayment can settle.

**Language and Names:** *Khazduul* has no word for "forgive." Names reflect deeds or crafts and carry no gender.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Dwergaz are known for living engines: self-repairing bridges and siege engines powered by harvested daemon souls. Doctrine holds that a machine built to stand in a Dwergaz's place is worth building, since it spares a life that cannot be replaced.

## Homeland and Distribution

The Dwergaz's primary hold is the subterranean empire of Tor'Az, anchored by Karak's Spine, its capital, and Tradehaven, a cosmopolitan port carved into a cavern ceiling. Every great hold is built around a moonwell, a shaft bored to align with the correct moon on the correct nights; a hold whose moonwell is lost or destroyed is considered to be in crisis. Tradehaven has no moonwell at all, which the deep clans regard as one more mark against it. Remote mining outposts dot Ressam's mountain ranges beyond the great holds, often clashing with surface-dwellers over mineral rights.

## Relations with Other Races

Allies are few. Tradehaven's neutrality forces temporary partnerships with merchant leagues, and the Dwergaz arm nearly everyone, including their customers' enemies, on the reasoning that a people who cannot grow their numbers must grow their leverage. Their clearest enemies are the Orkhaden Horde, accused of stealing Mischar's first scales, and the Aelves, whom they dismiss as flighty "rootless twigs."

Their closest bond is an unlikely one: the Feliids of the Tagarian Coalition. A maritime race has no interest in what lies beneath the stone, so the two peoples meet at Tradehaven and Sokhara. Feliid fleets carry Dwergaz engines to every coast in exchange for contracts the Dwergaz consider scrupulously honoured. Feliid captains also pay the Bookkeepers of Terrevault to record their families' names and deeds, not as grudges but as legacies, and the Dwergaz keep those records for centuries after the last descendant has forgotten them.

"Stubborn as a Dwergaz grudge" is an accurate stereotype. "Greedy as a Mischar pirate" is less so, since Dwergaz wealth is reinvested rather than hoarded. Outsiders also believe the Dwergaz hide their women underground, a rumour the Dwergaz have never bothered to correct.

## Variations and Subgroups

The Dwergaz split into two branches. The Durmhezzar of Terrevault are traditionalists who hold that the failing veins are the Mother's trial, and that enduring it is a form of worship. The Dromvalkaz of Tormaz, who live in the shadow of the Infernal Gate, hold a colder view: that a race that waits to die deserves to. Led by Dwarin the Industrious and the experimental Dromgadol Guild, the Dromvalkaz are ash-skinned and ember-scarred, and prize invention over sanctity; their soul-bound machines, such as the Tierratuar, blur the line between life and artifice.
