# Strygs

## *Night-Terror \- The fear of death*

## Overview

The Strygs are incarnate nightmares \- creatures born not of flesh, but of dread. Spawned from the primal fear of death and the unknown, they slither into the waking world from the Fields of Nocturne, bringing with them an aura of despair and unmaking. Unlike the Varulf, who still carry the echo of mortal origins, the Strygs are wholly other: reflections of suffering so potent they bled themselves into existence. They do not kill to feed. They drain to erase. Whispered of in frightened tales and cursed scripture, the Strygs lurk on the edges of civilization \- where shadows gather, and where hope falters.

**Homeland:** Fields of Nocturne  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 DEX, \+1 STR, \+1 CHA, \-2 END  
**Age:** Do not age  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** You retain the Size of the mortal you were before transformation.  
**Base Speed:** 35 feet  
**Starting Languages:** You retain the Languages of the mortal you were before transformation.  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 in any two Skills

**Level Adjustment:** \+2

### **Features**

**Darkvision:** You treat nonmagical darkness as dim light up to 60 ft.  
**Shroud of Lies:** You gain \+2 to Deception and Intimidation, advantage on Stealth outside sunlight.  
**Vampiric Regeneration:** While below 10 \+ your Level in HP, you regain 1 HP at the start of each turn. This regeneration is suppressed while in direct sunlight or submerged in running water.  
**Blood Drinker:** You gain a Bite attack (1d6+STR piercing). If it deals damage, regain HP equal to damage dealt, this only occurs once per turn.  
**Undead Nature:** You don't need food, water, or air but must feed on blood weekly or gain 1 Exhaustion.  
**Sunlight Weakness:** While in direct sunlight: you receive \-2 to all rolls, cannot heal, magical or otherwise.  
**Stake Through Heart:** If you're reduced to 0 HP, or hit while Dying by a Stake, you are instantly destroyed.

## Physical Appearance

To behold a Stryg is to feel the air thin and the warmth flee. They are often unnaturally tall and slender \- creatures stretched between realms. The more feral among them appear gaunt and corpse-like, with skin like bleached bone or cold ash. Sunken eyes burn low and smouldering, twin coals flickering in bottomless pits. Their hair falls in wild, matted strands or wiry tufts, and their features shift between a cruel, bestial snarl and an almost tragic elegance, depending on the flicker of the light. Each movement is a quiet disturbance \- unnervingly fluid, as if the world resists their passage. Where they tread, colour, warmth, and sound seem to recede.

Yet for every horror in the dark, there is beauty behind the mask. The more self-controlled Strygs can pass as human \- pale, yes, but disarmingly ordinary, their monstrous truth hidden behind calm eyes and careful smiles.

Strygs do not age in any mortal sense. They are born whole from the nightmare realm of Nocturne, and though they wear flesh in the waking world, their true form matures as their Dreamself deepens \- fed by violence, reflection, and the fears they harvest or endure. To see a Stryg is to glimpse what lingers between sleep and waking, beauty and nightmare, flame and shadow.

## Origins and Mythology

The first of their kind, Thaecryx, is said to have come into being by biting through its own "Dream-self's" throat \- tearing free from the shared subconscious of dying mortals. Strygs were not created by the Painters, nor born from living stock, but instead are existential fractures \- a wound in the world made flesh. They are the dreams of the hunted, given spine and teeth.

Unlike true predators, Strygs do not drink blood for sustenance, but for ritual. The act is sacred \- a sacrament through which they bathe their Dreamself. To consume blood is to steep themselves in identity, to soak in another being's lived truth and pain. The most ancient among them have tasted the blood of kings, martyrs, and monsters, layering themselves in centuries of blood.

Their mythology is stitched from contradiction, often blending suffering, identity, and oblivion into paradoxical scripture. Some heretical texts claim the Strygs are the answer to an unspoken question Thryzzaral once asked itself. They do not believe in salvation, only unraveling \- and they see themselves as both a symptom and cure for the disease of hope.

To the Strygs, life is not sacred \- it is transactional. Suffering is both the ink of truth and the coin of power.

## Culture and Society

There is no unified Stryg empire or domain. Instead, Strygs gather in cryptic cabals known as Shrouds \- loose, often fractious cells bound by shared memory, secrets, or mutual hunger. But most are lone predators; some manipulate entire cities from the shadows.

**Traditions and Rites:** Strygs do not marry, birth, or die as mortals do. Instead, they form, through terrible acts or emotional ruptures. Rites such as The Hollowing (in which a Stryg devours the last dream of a dying soul) or Kindling (the first time a Stryg unleashes their Bloodfire) mark their progression.

**Language and Names:** They have no native tongue, but Strygs often speak poetically. Traditionally Strygs give themselves a new name, usually a title or concept: Vinthe the Hollow, Cinderweft, Ash of Sisters, Never-Born. Mortal aliases are used for infiltration.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** Strygs create nothing conventional. Their "art" is in destruction, subversion, and decay. Some craft architecture out of memories or weave tapestries of fading dreams. Others script labyrinths that alter as you read them. Beauty, to a Stryg, lies in dissonance.

## Homeland and Distribution

Strygs hail from the Fields of Nocturne \- a semi-spiritual realm shaped by collective human terror, accessed in moments of trauma, madness, or magical breach. In Ressam, they are most often encountered in places touched by death and despair: war-fronts, haunted ruins, or cursed cities.

Rumours swirl of Strygs operating within the world's elite, feeding not on bodies, but on the slow spiritual rot of decadence and lies. They are rarely found in open wilderness unless called by some greater darkness.

## Relations with Other Races

Feared and reviled, the Strygs are the stuff of hushed legend and holy warnings. Few races tolerate their presence, and most religious orders \- especially the Hunters of Sibbell and the Chevaliers des Vagues \- view them as abominations. The Aelfen shun them as spiritual poison; Dwergaz forge weapons specifically for Stryg slaying.

Some Dzinari, however, speak of the Strygs with an uneasy respect, seeing in them a reflection of their own secrets or pain. Whispers claim that Strygs have subtly altered the course of entire empires through proxy wars and engineered collapses.

## Faith and Philosophy

Strygs do not worship, but they believe. Their central philosophy revolves around the concept of the Dreamself \- a metaphysical shadow-self formed from emotion and memory inside of Nocturne. They bleed this Dreamself to conjure Bloodfire: a sentient flame of burning, volatile blood.

To the Stryg, identity is pain, and pain is power. They perform rituals that mutilate their own souls, invoking flames that scream in lost voices and consume the past. Each ignition is a prayer of unmaking, offered not to gods, but to absence itself.

Some scholars believe Strygs are seeking something \- a return to unreality, or perhaps the full unravelling of Thaecryx's will.
