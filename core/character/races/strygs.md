# Strygs

## *Night-Terror \- The fear of death*

## Overview

The Strygs are nightmares given flesh, spawned from the primal fear of death and the unknown and drawn into the waking world from the Fields of Nocturne. Unlike the Varulf, who still carry the echo of a mortal life before their curse, a Stryg has no mortal life to remember \- it is dread that grew a spine and teeth. A Stryg doesn't kill to feed; it drains a victim to erase them, and treats the act as a sacrament rather than a meal.

**Homeland:** Fields of Nocturne  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 DEX, \+1 STR, \+1 CHA, \-2 END  
**Age:** Do not age  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** You retain the Size of the mortal you were before transformation.  
**Base Speed:** 35 feet  
**Starting Languages:** You retain the Languages of the mortal you were before transformation.  
**Starting Skills:** \+2 in any two Skills

**Level Adjustment:** \+2

### **Features**

**Darkvision:** You treat nonmagical darkness as dim light up to 60 ft.  
**Shroud of Lies:** You gain \+2 to Deception and Intimidation, advantage on Stealth outside sunlight.  
**Vampiric Regeneration:** While below 10 \+ your Level in HP, you regain 1 HP at the start of each turn. This regeneration is suppressed while in direct sunlight or submerged in running water.  
**Blood Drinker:** You gain a Bite attack (1d6+STR piercing). If it deals damage, regain HP equal to damage dealt, this only occurs once per turn.  
**Undead Nature:** You don't need food, water, or air but must feed on blood weekly or gain 1 Exhaustion.  
**Sunlight Weakness:** While in direct sunlight: you receive \-2 to all rolls, cannot heal, magical or otherwise.  
**Stake Through Heart:** If you're reduced to 0 HP, or hit while Dying by a Stake, you are instantly destroyed.

## Physical Appearance

A Stryg's build is unnaturally tall and slender, and its presence alone tends to thin the air and chase off warmth. The more feral among them are gaunt and corpse-pale, sunken-eyed, hair falling in wild matted strands, their features shifting between bestial snarl and a tragic kind of elegance. The more self-controlled pass easily as ordinary humans \- pale but disarmingly calm, the truth hidden behind a practiced smile. Strygs do not age; what changes instead is how deep their **Somnumbra** runs, fed over time by violence, reflection, and harvested fear.

## Origins and Mythology

The first Stryg, Thaecryx, is said to have bitten through its own Somnumbra's throat, tearing free from the shared subconscious of dying mortals. Strygs are existential fractures rather than creations of the Painters or living stock \- the dreams of the hunted, given spine and teeth. They drink blood as ritual, not sustenance: each feeding steeps a Stryg's Somnumbra in another being's lived truth and pain, and the oldest among them carry centuries of blood from kings, martyrs, and monsters layered one atop the next. To a Stryg, life isn't sacred \- it's transactional, and suffering is both the ink of truth and the coin of power.

## Culture and Society

Strygs have no unified empire. Most are lone predators; the rest gather into **Latebrae**, loose and fractious cells bound by shared memory or hunger, and a few operate alone yet manipulate entire cities from the shadows.

**Traditions and Rites:** Strygs don't marry, birth, or die as mortals do \- they form, through terrible acts or emotional rupture. The **Vorago**, in which a Stryg devours a dying soul's last dream, and the **Accensio**, the first time a Stryg unleashes its Bloodfire, mark the two turning points of a Stryg's early existence.

**Language and Names:** Strygs have no native tongue, and speak in a deliberately poetic register \- giving themselves titles or concepts as names (*Vinthe the Hollow, Cinderweft, Never-Born*) while keeping a mortal alias in reserve for infiltration.

**Arts and Craftsmanship:** What a Stryg calls art tends toward destruction, subversion, and decay: architecture built from memory, tapestries of fading dreams, labyrinths that shift as they're read. To a Stryg, beauty lies in dissonance.

## Homeland and Distribution

Strygs hail from the Fields of Nocturne, the semi-spiritual realm shaped by collective human terror, and reach the waking world through trauma, madness, or magical breach. In Ressam they cluster wherever death and despair already linger \- war-fronts, haunted ruins, cursed cities \- and rumour holds that a few feed not on bodies but on the slow spiritual rot of decadence among the world's elite.

## Relations with Other Races

Strygs are feared and reviled \- the stuff of hushed legend and holy warning. Most religious orders, especially the Hunters of Sibbell and the Chevaliers des Vagues, treat them as abominations outright; the Aelfen shun them as spiritual poison, and Dwergaz smiths forge weapons purpose-built for Stryg-slaying. Some Dzinari are the exception, speaking of Strygs with uneasy respect \- they recognise something of their own relationship to secrets and pain \- and rumour credits Strygs with quietly reshaping entire empires through proxy wars and engineered collapses.

## Faith and Philosophy

Strygs do not worship, but they believe, and their philosophy centres on the Somnumbra \- a metaphysical shadow-self of emotion and memory formed within Nocturne, which a Stryg bleeds to conjure Bloodfire. To a Stryg, identity is pain and pain is power: they mutilate their own souls in rituals that invoke flames screaming with lost voices, treating each ignition as a prayer of unmaking offered not to any god, but to absence itself. Some believe they're working toward a return to unreality, or the full unravelling of Thaecryx's will.

## Variations and Subgroups

The clearest line among Strygs is between the feral and the composed. A newly-formed or undisciplined Stryg reads as barely-restrained hunger \- corpse-pale, wild-eyed, unable to pass among mortals for more than a moment. A Stryg who has survived long enough to master its Somnumbra instead learns restraint, sometimes for centuries, wearing a mortal alias as comfortably as its true face. Most of either kind stay solitary; a smaller number bind themselves into a Latebra for protection or shared hunting ground, and a rarer few forgo any cell at all in favour of ruling a city from behind a mortal proxy, patient enough to let empires rise and fall around a single unaging hand.
