# Skeggs

## *Fireborne cursed of Earth \- Invidia (Envy)*

## Overview

Skeggs are wiry, sharp-eyed subterranean kin of the Orkhs, infamous for their cunning, adaptability, and brutal efficiency in the dark. While Orkhs rage and roar beneath war banners, Skeggs thrive in silence and shadow \- skirmishers, saboteurs, and poisoners who live and die by a different code of strength. They are frequently underestimated by surface folk for their small size and feline gait, but to their enemies, the name Skegg means ambush, venom, and vanishing before vengeance can be dealt.

**Homeland:** Inggaz (underground)  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 DEX, \-1 END  
**Age:** Adult \~16, Lives to \~60  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Small (3-4 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 25 feet, climbing speed 25 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Grazn  
**Starting Skills:** \+1 Survival, \+1 Medical Lore

### **Features**

**Darkvision:** You treat nonmagical darkness as dim light up to 60 ft.  
**Bristling Spines:** When you are hit by melee attack, you may use a Reaction to deal 1d4+DEX piercing damage to the attacker.  
**Tunnel Fighter:** You have advantage on Stealth checks while underground. You can move through the space of any Medium or larger creature as if it were difficult terrain.  
**Poisoned Quills:** Three times per Field Rest or Long Rest, you may coat one weapon with venom as a Minor Action. The next creature damaged by that weapon within 1 minute takes an additional 1d4 poison damage (no Ward) and must succeed on an END Ward DC 18 or be poisoned until the end of their next turn.

## Physical Appearance

Skeggs are small, digitigrade humanoids with lean limbs and rodent-like features wrapped in oil-slick fur, moving with a twitchy, feral grace. Twin horns curve from their skull, and a mantle of porcupine-like spines bristles along their back and shoulders, used for intimidation, communication, and, when needed, as weapons. Glands beneath each spine metabolise hallucinogenic fungus into a venom used both to defend and, ritually, to share memory through ingestion or injection. Their eyes are large and dark-tuned, their claws fine climbing hooks, and most wear scavenged armour or hide reinforced with bone and volcanic glass.

## Origins and Mythology

Born from the same rootstock as the Orkhs, Skeggs are an evolutionary recoil rather than a forward march \- descendants of warbands who fled downward into smoke-choked caverns when the skies burned too brightly to survive, and were changed by the retreat rather than broken by it. Their elders speak rarely, but when they do it is of ancestral visions \- fire, hunger, betrayal, silence \- not metaphors: Skegg blood remembers, and their toxins and dreams carry warnings forward. They do not believe the world can be conquered. Only endured.

## Culture and Society

Skegg society is decentralised and semi-nomadic, organised around **Mawholds** \- matriarchal tunnel communities led by **Deepmothers** (or Spine-Wives), chosen for instinct and foresight rather than strength. **Broods** are communal family groups raised without known fathers, loyalty owed to the maw rather than the parent; **Tunnelseers** are lorekeepers and alchemists who read the earth's tremors and brew toxins from fungus and rot; **Clawkin** are warriors trained to move without sound; and **Horn-Born**, Skeggs with unusually long or twisted horns, are believed touched by ancestral rage.

**Traditions and Rites:** Names are earned only after a successful hunt or escape \- an unnamed Skegg isn't considered fully alive. At adolescence, Skeggs undergo the Poisoning Rite, a ritual venom-bite from an elder; survivors are said to "remember the fire." The dead are dissolved in fungal beds rather than buried, their memories returning to the tunnels, while offenders are muzzled and marked with ash rather than exiled. Adulthood is marked by the Ceremony of the Spine, when back-spines are ritually sharpened and polished.

**Language and Names:** *Grazn*, a harsh chittering dialect of Old Orkhish, is heavy with tongue-clicks and non-verbal cues \- tail flicks, spine gestures, scent trails. Names are monosyllabic (*Zruk, Tikka, Vrahn*) with earned suffixes (*-fang, -burrow, -black*); true names are whispered only to kin, and revealing one to an outsider is ultimate trust or ultimate surrender.

## Homeland and Distribution

Skeggs dwell in the deep shadows of Inggaz, their Underholds threading through old war fronts and sunken ruins once held by the Orkhaden Horde. Spine-Gate is a trap-laced defensive nexus between major burrows; Fangroot, a fungal citadel lit by ghost-lanterns, holds the largest known Deepmother conclave; and Ashcoil is a nomadic Mawhold that rides atop a migrating magma beetle colony. They rarely surface except to trade, hunt, or sabotage, wearing dark robes and masks to conceal scent and spines when they do.

## Relations with Other Races

Orkhs see Skeggs as cowardly cousins; Skeggs see Orkhs as suicidal fools, though some warbands quietly hire Skegg saboteurs. Humans find them oddly endearing, which Skeggs tolerate so long as it benefits them. The Dwergaz are bitter foes blamed for collapsed tunnels and poisoned springs, sabotaged with silent glee, and the Aelves consider Skeggs vermin \- a feeling that is mutual. Trade is possible but tense; currency means little to them next to information or safety.

## Faith and Philosophy

Skeggs don't worship gods but memory \- ancestral instinct preserved in blood and bone. Everything a Skegg sees or suffers is believed passed down through venom, dream, or scar: to survive is to contribute to the memory, and to die well is to leave a usable warning.

There is no word for "hope" in Grazn, but eleven words for "endure." Their core philosophy, the Withdrawal, holds that the strong wait and strike only when the future is within reach. A whispered heresy among the young, the Hollow Flame, holds that rebirth from fire is possible \- a reversal of the Collapse. Such dreamers rarely live long.
