# Skeggs

## *Fireborne cursed of Earth \- Invidia (Envy)*

## Overview

Skeggs are wiry, sharp-eyed subterranean kin of the Orkhs, infamous for their cunning, adaptability, and brutal efficiency in the dark. While Orkhs rage and roar beneath war banners, Skeggs thrive in silence and shadow \- skirmishers, saboteurs, and poisoners who live and die by a different code of strength. They are frequently underestimated by surface folk for their small size and feline gait, but to their enemies, the name Skegg means ambush, venom, and vanishing before vengeance can be dealt.

**Homeland:** Inggaz (underground)  
**Starting Attributes:** \+2 DEX, \-1 END  
**Age:** Adult \~16, Lives to \~60  
**Alignment:** Chaotic  
**Size:** Small (3-4 ft)  
**Base Speed:** 25 feet, climbing speed 25 feet  
**Starting Languages:** Grazn  
**Starting Skills:** \+1 Survival, \+1 Medical Lore

### **Features**

**Darkvision:** You treat nonmagical darkness as dim light up to 60 ft.  
**Bristling Spines:** When you are hit by melee attack, you may use a Reaction to deal 1d4+DEX piercing damage to the attacker.  
**Tunnel Fighter:** You have advantage on Stealth checks while underground. You can move through the space of any Medium or larger creature as if it were difficult terrain.  
**Poisoned Quills:** Three times per Field Rest or Long Rest, you may coat one weapon with venom as a Minor Action. The next creature damaged by that weapon within 1 minute takes an additional 1d4 poison damage (no Ward) and must succeed on an END Ward DC 18 or be poisoned until the end of their next turn.

## Physical Appearance

Skeggs are small, digitigrade humanoids with lean, powerful limbs and rodent-like features wrapped in dense, oil-slick fur. They average just under five feet tall and move with a twitchy, feral grace. Their most distinct features are the twin regal horns curving from the sides of their skull and the mantle of long, porcupine-like spines that bristle along their back and shoulders \- used for intimidation, communication, and, at times, as lethal weapons, along with a long rat-like tail.

Skegg physiology includes specialised glands beneath their skin at the base of each spine. These glands metabolise alkaloids and hallucinogenic compounds from their diet \- primarily the deathcap-like fungi of Inggaz. Over time, this builds into spine-secreted venom, used both defensively and in ritual sharing of memory through ingestion or injection.

Their eyes are large, reflective, and tuned to the dark, and their claws have evolved into fine, climbing hooks. Most wear scavenged armour or dark hide leathers reinforced with bone and volcanic glass.

## Origins and Mythology

Born from the same rootstock as the Orkhs, Skeggs are a divergent offshoot \- an evolutionary recoil rather than a forward march. Their lore speaks of a time when the skies turned red with fire and Orkh warbands burned too brightly to survive. The Skeggs fled downward, seeking refuge in smoke-choked caverns and scorched ruins. In this retreat, they were not broken \- they changed.

Their elders speak rarely, but when they do, it is of ancestral visions: fire, hunger, betrayal, silence. These aren't metaphors. The Skegg blood remembers, and it shares \- their toxins carry memories, and their dreams pass warnings. They do not believe the world can be conquered. Only endured.

## Culture and Society

Skegg society is decentralised and semi-nomadic, based around "Mawholds" \- tight-knit, matriarchal tunnel communities ruled by survivalists known as Deepmothers or Spine-Wives. These leaders are chosen not by strength, but by instinct, memory, and foresight \- those who sense threats in tremors others ignore. Mawhold life is organised around a handful of roles: Broods are family groups, often raised communally with no known fathers, since loyalty is owed to the maw rather than the parent. Tunnelseers are lorekeepers and alchemists who interpret the vibrations of the earth and concoct toxins from fungus and rot. Clawkin are warriors and ambushers, raised to move without sound and strike without hesitation. Horn-Born are Skeggs with especially long or twisted horns, believed to be touched by ancestral rage.

**Traditions and Rites:** Names are gifted only after a successful hunt or escape, always earned; a Skegg without a name is not considered fully alive. At adolescence, Skeggs undergo the Poisoning Rite \- a ritual exposure to psychogenic venom in which their elders "bite" them; those who survive are said to "remember the fire." Skeggs do not bury their dead \- they dissolve them in fungal beds, allowing their memories to return to the tunnels. Punishment by exile is rare; instead, offenders are muzzled and left at a tunnel's edge, marked with ash, and the world may do what it will. When a Skegg reaches adulthood, the Ceremony of the Spine sees their back spines ritually sharpened and polished \- symbols of both pride and responsibility.

**Language and Names:** Skeggs speak a harsh, chittering dialect of Old Orkhish known as Grazn \- full of snarls, tongue-clicks, and growled inflections. Much of their communication is non-verbal: tail flicks, spine gestures, and scent trails. Names are monosyllabic or staccato-based (*Zruk, Tikka, Vrahn*), with earned suffixes (*-fang, -burrow, -black*) added after deeds. True names are whispered only in darkness and only to kin \- revealing one's true name to an outsider is an act of ultimate trust or ultimate surrender.

## Homeland and Distribution

The Skeggs dwell in the deep shadows of Inggaz, beneath blackened ridges and cooled lava plains where no sun dares shine. Their Underholds thread through old war fronts, volcanic chimneys, and sunken ruins once held by the Orkhaden Horde. Among these strongholds, Spine-Gate is a defensive nexus between several major burrows, bristling with poisonous traps and bone spires; Fangroot is a fungal citadel buried in the roots of a petrified grove, lit by ghost-lanterns and home to the largest known Deepmother conclave; and Ashcoil is a nomadic Mawhold that rides atop a migrating magma beetle colony \- rare, but terrifying in battle.

Skeggs rarely emerge unless they need to trade, hunt, or sabotage. Above ground, they wear dark robes and masks to conceal their scent and spines.

## Relations with Other Races

The Orkhs are disdainful older cousins to the Skeggs: Orkhs view Skeggs as cowards, while Skeggs view Orkhs as suicidal fools, though some warbands now quietly hire Skegg saboteurs despite rarely admitting it. Humans treat Skeggs with odd affection, often calling them "cute" or "clever," and Skeggs gladly tolerate this ignorance so long as it benefits them. The Dwergaz are bitter foes beneath the stone; Skeggs blame them for collapsed tunnels and poisoned springs and sabotage their workings with silent glee. The Aelves consider Skeggs vermin, a feeling that is mutual.

Trade with Skeggs is possible, but always tense. They deal in toxins, scavenged relics, and smoked meats. Currency means little to them \- information or safety buys more.

## Faith and Philosophy

Skeggs do not worship in the way surface-dwellers do. Their belief is not in gods but in memory \- ancestral instincts preserved in blood and bone. They believe that everything a Skegg sees or suffers is passed down in some form, through venom, dream, or scar. To survive is to contribute to the memory. To die well is to leave a usable warning.

There is no word for "hope" in Grazn. But there are eleven words for "endure." Their core philosophy, known as the Withdrawal, holds that the strong do not charge \- they wait, watch, and strike only when the future is within reach. Among young Skeggs, a whispered heresy called the Hollow Flame persists: belief in rebirth from fire, a reversal of the Collapse. Such dreamers rarely live long.
