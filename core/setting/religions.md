## The Fe Aladan Faith

**Overview.** The Fe Alada is a widespread and grand religion that spans nearly the entire world. Its followers believe that the God-Dragons are the supreme creators of all existence and that without their guidance, the world would descend into darkness. The religion is so extensive that it functions as its own empire, encompassing many vassal kingdoms. It also maintains a small but incredibly well-funded military which, although typically not aggressive, can be easily provoked.

A blade-saint is worshipped as their one and only Saint, Maarkest, the Patron Saint of Martyrdom, said to have helped the God-Dragons during the Sundering.

Its headquarters rest in the castle state of Gran Reino, where the religion's head, High Lord Victor Ardan, rules over all of the Holy Gran Reino Empire.

## The Elar'laevien

**Overview.** The primary religion of the Aelves. A belief that the world was not merely created \- it was painted, a living artwork known as Elar'laevien. The Four Painters are not gods to be worshipped, but divine artists whose brushstrokes shaped reality. Aelves do not pray; they contemplate, breathe, and listen. Sacred groves serve as temples, each attuned to a Painter's style \- wind, fire, stone, or water.

Those of this faith seek harmony in all acts, believing life itself is part of an eternal composition. Their spiritual leaders, the Liraevin ("those who listen"), act as philosophers, storytellers, and oracles chosen by the groves, not hierarchy.

## The Ash'shene

**Overview.** The Ash'shene are a heretical offshoot of the Elar'laevien, the primary faith of the Aelves that teaches the world is a living artwork painted by the Four Painters, whose brushstrokes shaped reality itself. While the Elar'laevien seek harmony and contemplation within sacred groves attuned to wind, the Ash'shene believe that the Wind has been poisoned with the madness of the dead since the Sundering. Rejecting the gentle whispers of the Wind, they worship Ash and Fire as the purified form of spirit, and Fire as a sacred destroyer and revealer of hidden truths. To shield themselves from the Wind's deceitful howls, which they say conceal pain and lies, the Ash'shene wear white painted masks, turning away from the serene balance of the groves to embrace a harsher path of purification through flame and ash.

## The Tagarian Pantheon

**Overview.** The Tagarian pantheon is one of practicality; each god serves a specific domain and they rarely interact with mortals. Usually Feliids will dedicate themselves to only a couple of gods, usually of the same type. But most do not actively worship their gods, instead they are accepted as necessary parts of life to be respected but not revered.

### Pantheon of Survival and Function

* **God of Sustenance.** Domain over food, hunger, harvests, and feasting.
* **God of Breath and Blood.** Governs air, water, circulation, and the balance of bodily humors.
* **God of Rest and Slumber.** Patron of sleep, dreams, and physical recovery.
* **God of Flame and Warmth.** Keeper of fire, shelter, and protection from the elements.

### Pantheon of Security and Stability

* **God of Walls and Waters.** Overseer of fortifications, city gates, canals, and safe harbors.
* **God of Law and Oath.** Embodiment of contracts, order, and civic stability.
* **God of Coin and Counting.** Guardian of wealth, trade, accounting, and merchant safety.
* **God of Shadow and Watchfulness.** Patron of guards, vigilance, and protection from unseen threats.

### Pantheon of Family and Connection

* **God of Kin and Hearth.** Ruler of family, lineage, and household bonds.
* **God of Union and Yearning.** Patron of romantic love, desire, and intimate connection.
* **God of Brotherhood and Fealty.** Overseer of camaraderie, guilds, alliances, and sworn companionship.
* **God of Celebration and Mourning.** Lord of festivals, funerals, and the rituals of shared life.

### Pantheon of Respect and Status

* **God of Mask and Merit.** Arbiter of reputation, fashion, honor, and social rank.
* **God of Craft and Creation.** Patron of artisans, invention, mastery, and prestige through skill.
* **God of Victory and Applause.** Deity of fame, triumph, acclaim, and public achievement.
* **God of Command and Crown.** Representative of leadership, nobility, and the divine right to rule.

### Pantheon of Fulfillment and Purpose

* **God of the Labyrinth Within.** Guide of introspection, personal growth, and philosophical questing.
* **God of Vision and Voice.** Patron of artists, prophets, and those who seek to express their truth.
* **God of the Far Horizon.** Lord of exploration, ambition, and pushing the limits of the known world.
* **God of Sacred Madness.** Embodiment of divine inspiration, epiphany, and the edge between genius and madness.

### Pantheon of Unity and Meaning

* **God of the Living Tapestry.** Deity of interconnection, fate, and the grand design of all things.
* **God of Mercy and Sacrifice.** Patron of martyrdom, selflessness, and unity through compassion.
* **God of Silence and Stars.** Keeper of the eternal, the unknowable, and the divine beyond comprehension.
* **God of Reflection and Return.** Overseer of reincarnation, legacy, and the soul's journey beyond death.

## Al'Nur Lehovil

### *The Light of Lehovil*

**Overview.** Al'Nur Lehovil is the central and all-encompassing faith of the Desert Nomads and Alsahli, both deeply spiritual and unyielding people who wander the Great Sand. They believe in a singular divine figure \- Lehovil, God of Gods, Shepherd of Men (and, by extension, Alsahli), He of the End and Beginning \- who once walked among them in the First Age. According to the stories they pass down, he led their ancestors through a time of great calamity, guiding them to the dunes they call home, a land which protects them better than any wall, before he vanished into the desert. His disappearance is interpreted not as death, but as ascension, and his return is prophesied to come in a time of great need for his people, to protect and guide them to a paradise of plenty.

It is said in extraordinary times he sends prophets on his behalf to speak his word and channel his will; over the ages these prophets passed on lessons in the form of stories from the past of their people, long forgotten, which hold defining morals. To the nomads these stories are the greatest treasure of their people.

The Light of Lehovil governs more than just faith \- it binds the nomads socially, legally, and politically. Great caravans travel from one holy site to another, led by priests who memorize the entire scripture and recite it beneath the stars. While the Desert Nomads are not imperialistic, they fiercely defend their sacred lands and pilgrimage routes, often with zeal.

# The Dwergan Pantheon

**Overview.** The Dwergaz believe in a host of different deities, from the God of Cupboards to the God of Mines, but the primary three gods, considered above them all, are Mischar, Nekama, and Melacha. Above even the Triad - above naming, above prayer - stands the Great Mother, from whose veins every Dwergaz was cut. **For her place in the Dwergaz creation myth, the birthing-veins, and the dispute of the three creeds, see the Dwergaz race page.**

### The Great Mother - *Matka*

**The Mother of the race, who is one of the twin moons - though which moon, no two clans agree.** She is not worshipped as the gods are worshipped; one does not pray to the ground one stands on. She is *attended*: her passes tracked, her tremors read, her yields awaited. She has no teachings, for she has never spoken. She has no temples, for every hold is one. She has no heraldry and no symbol - her sign is the tremor, and her scripture is written in the deep stone for those patient enough to read it. In Khazduul she is only *Matka*; which light in the sky the word points to depends entirely on who is speaking.

### Mischar

**The god of trade, commerce, wealth and, to some, greed - and the Mother's one daughter.** Alone among the Dwergaz, Mischar was never cut from a vein: where the Mother hewed her sons from stone, her daughter she *hid*, buried at the very heart of Ressam, where the first-cut sons tunnelled for an age to find her. Why the Mother made one daughter, and why she concealed her deepest of all, no creed has answered. Mischar's teachings encourage the pursuit of wealth but have many tales warning against excess. A heretical sect believes the acquisition of wealth above all to be the greatest good; they tend to turn to piracy and banditry. Her symbol is a balanced scale - the scale that weighs what is owed. However, pirates dedicated to her often use flags with the scale tipped, a skull lifted by a pile of coins, their intentions blunt and direct. Her heraldry is often gold, silver and purple.

### Nekama

**The god of knowledge, memory, honour and revenge.** He represents passionate commitment, whether to a goal, a bond, or just as often, righting wrongs. His teachings tell that one should not forget the past, good or bad, as forgetting often leads to repeating past mistakes. Among a people whose dead can never be replaced, his doctrine cuts deeper still: a Dwergaz life is a debt no payment can settle, and so his open book is understood as a ledger - the record of what is owed to the fallen. Where Mischar's scale weighs the debt, Nekama's book remembers it; the two are invoked together in the oldest oaths, *sworn on the scale and the book*. His lessons have inspired the obsessive keepings of the Bookkeepers of Terrevault and a culture among the Dwergaz of pursuing retribution for slights both great and small. Heretics of Nekama teach that while remembrance is essential, forgiveness and compassion are the only roads to peace, inner and outer. Nekama is most often represented with an open book; heretics sometimes display this book as closed or sealed. His heraldry is often parchment white and leather red.

### Melacha

**God of artists, craftsmen, perfectionism and self-doubt - the First Cutter, the Mother's hand.** It was Melacha who took up the first chisel and cut his brothers free from the vein, and every Dwergaz since is counted among his works. He teaches those who strive to forever seek improvement, to endlessly chase perfection - that true mastery lies in the endless striving for better. His followers are driven, often tormented, by their own standards, inspired by the belief that nothing is ever truly finished - and the doctrine means what it says: the race itself is his unfinished masterpiece, still being cut, never complete. His symbol is a chisel piercing an incomplete circle - the chisel entering the vein, the circle of the race not yet closed - and his heraldry bears bronze, charcoal grey, and pale jade.

Some heretics claim perfection is divine and must remain unattainable, destroying their works in devotion. But the gravest of Melacha's heresies is quieter, and it lives in Tormaz: the teaching that Melacha's chisel is not a relic but an *office* - that any sufficiently masterful hand may take it up, and cut new sons where the Mother has gone silent. The Dromgadol Guild has never professed this creed aloud. Terrevault has never needed to hear it aloud to know it.

## Vessel

### *Shepherd of Souls*

**Overview.** At the helm of this pantheon is Vessel, a mortal-god of transition, compassion, and one of the few good deities of necromancy. Unlike the dread lords of undeath or the cold arbiters of the afterlife, Vessel is a singer, a guide, and a protector of souls \- especially those who died too soon or in suffering. His followers believe death is not an end but a crossing, and those who lived with kindness will be welcomed into the next life with song and solace.

His faith is one of hope, but also justice: those who inflict cruelty are doomed to repent in servitude until they earn forgiveness.

## Tythe

### *God of the Lost and Second Chances*

**Overview.** Tythe is a mysterious and enigmatic deity who often appears at the crossroads of life and death. She is known for offering pacts to those on the brink of death, granting them a second chance in exchange for a future act, often one of violence or sacrifice. Tythe's presence is often marked by an omen of impending bloodshed, and her worshippers, particularly warlocks, seek her favor for the power she can bestow.

She is often worshipped by warlocks, assassins, and those who have been spared by her intervention.

Those spared by her are marked by a burn that never heals.

## Henri Gachet Gautier

### *God of Cities, Castles, and Civilization*

**Overview.** Once a fallen angel whose kingdom was destroyed by the God-Dragon of water, Henri Gachet Gautier ascended to godhood after avenging his lost home. He is now revered as the protector of cities and civilization, embodying the ideals of defense, construction, and community. His teachings emphasize the protection of one's home and people, and the importance of avenging any wrongs done to them.

His worshippers are Les Falaises, or the Order of Protectors (builders, healers, and defenders) and the Chevaliers des Vagues (knights of the waves, a self-righteous faction often found hunting the "evil" of this world).

His castle rests at the northernmost point of Lustralis, the Château Dubien. Although Henri Gachet Gautier has long since ascended, his influence has grown greatly.

He is one of the few gods with "Godspawn," angelic creatures known as Syraph, which appear as porcelain figures with feathered wings, in long flowing tunics, wielding flaming swords.

His symbol is a sun rising over a castle palisade.

## Davil

### *God of Thieves, Winds and Skies*

**Overview.** Sir Davil, also known as the Heroic Pirate God-King of Thieves, Winds, and Skies, is a god revered for his embodiment of trickery, rebellion, and liberation. He is the ruling deity of Pearling Bay, an island sanctuary for outcasts, revolutionaries, and those who reject traditional authority. Sir Davil's divine presence is marked by his ability to protect the island through supernatural means, conjuring headwinds and storms that make it difficult for unwanted visitors to reach its shores.

Sir Davil's followers celebrate cunning, deception, and the redistribution of wealth as acts of rebellion against unjust hierarchies. His influence inspires a culture of shared resources and mutual support, making Pearling Bay a beacon of hope for those seeking freedom and a new way of life.

The island of Pearling Bay is governed by Davil's Core Tenets:

* **The fire is for all.** Hoard nothing. What you gather, you share. What you build, you build for the kin.
* **The tide lifts all canoes.** No one shall rise so high another must drown. Break the oar of the tyrant.
* **Speak your truth, even if it roars like a storm.** Let no voice be shackled, not even your own.
* **To strike when struck is holy.** Vengeance is not sin; it is balance.
