## Age of Mythos

Before time, matter, or space, there was nothing \- and its God, Thryzzaral \- an utterly alien being whose existence defied comprehension. Alone in the void, it grew mad, for even its labyrinthian mind could not endure the absolute nothing. To escape its torment, its mind fractured, an act of cosmic dissociation that tore a rift in the void. Through this rift, the universe was born, an infinite canvas to contain creation.

From the fractured mind of the first God emerged the four Great Artists, its splintered personalities given form. Each embodied emotional facets of existence, and together they represented entropy \- the inevitable cycle of change and decay. The Great Artists moved through the new universe, creating suns, shaping planets and sculpting moons.

When their grand work was complete, they turned their attention to one world unlike any other they had made: Ressam. The planet brimmed with potential, uniquely capable of fostering life. Declaring it their masterpiece, the Artists rested upon its surface, pouring the last of their essence into its soil. Their divine energy seeped deep into the earth, planting the seeds of life that would one day grow into plants, animals, and eventually the intelligent races.

As they slept, their lingering creativity ignited a new transformation. The elemental pools where the Artists rested absorbed the essence of their paint, gaining sentience over millennia. These pools, born of the Artists' power, took on draconic forms, becoming the four elemental God-Dragons: Infierno, Rocas, Tempeste and Cascate. Unlike their creators, the dragons sought permanence. They revered the beauty of the world but despised its fleeting nature, and they rose against the Artists. Absorbing what remained of their divine energy, the dragons became the eternal maintainers of the universe, their purpose clear: to halt the chaos of entropy and ensure that the masterpiece would never fade.

Though the Great Artists were gone, their legacy endured. The divine energy present in all things responded to the beliefs and thoughts of these beings, giving rise to Gods, magic, and the endless stories of creation. Unknown to the people of Ressam, the God-Dragons still exist, watching from the shadows and preserving the fragile balance of the world, ensuring that creation may last forever.

## The Sundering

Aldric Grimthorn, a wizard of unmatched ambition and power, sought to control the God-Dragons, falsely believing their existence fueled the world's catastrophes. But his arrogance led to disaster. The dragons, enraged by his attempts, turned their fury upon him. In a desperate act of self-preservation, the wizard invoked ancient magic and banished the dragons from the mortal plane, sending them into the void.

For a brief moment, the world knew peace, and Aldric thought he had finally prevailed, but the banishment was far from a solution. The dragons, isolated and festering, were changed. When they finally broke free, their anger had grown into a cold contempt. They decreed that intelligent races' hubris was a danger to the balance of creation. In their eyes, the wizard's actions were proof that mortals were much too like their forefathers of creation. Under the leadership of the Tempeste, the dragons began the annihilation of the great nations of the world.

The Tempeste, cunning and shrewd, took on a human form and founded a faith in secret. Posing as a prophet and saviour, he spread the creed 'The Fe Alada', a proclamation of the dragons' divine return. The faithful, unaware of their leader's true nature, became unwitting agents in the destruction of their own world.

Aldric was all too aware of what he had done and, wracked with guilt, he searched for a solution. He uncovered a dire truth: the spell he used to banish them had linked his soul to the dragons. This bond made him immortal and powerful, but also rendered the dragons vulnerable. Desperate to undo the devastation he had unleashed, the wizard enlisted the greatest adventurers and mercenaries from across the land.

Among them were five extraordinary individuals tasked with an impossible mission: slay the God-Dragons. Through the wizard's mastery of teleportation, they struck swiftly, travelling the breadth of the world to confront each elemental dragon in turn. These battles were titanic, shaking the very fabric of the land.

Though killing the dragons did not destroy them permanently, it severed the wizard's bond and cleansed the dragons' maddened minds, forcing them into a dormant state. The adventurers succeeded, halting the annihilation and sparing the wizard's life.

This calamitous series of events became known as The Sundering, a pivotal moment in history that revealed the existence of the dragons to the world. Though peace returned, the legacy of the dragons endured. As the Fe Alada, strengthened by their leader's perceived martyrdom, grew stronger than ever, its influence reached every corner of human society.

## Age of Revival

The Age of Revival \- a misnomer whispered by hopeful bards and delusional preachers. In truth, it was an age of fragile recovery, riddled with mistrust and scars too deep to heal. The Sundering had not just shattered cities and kingdoms \- it had fractured the very spirit of the world. Nations, weary and broken, turned inward, unwilling to extend hands already bloodied by war and betrayal.

The God-Dragons' destructive wake stood as a grim monument to the sins of mortals. Whole regions, cities and towns remained uninhabited. Those who returned to these ruined lands often found themselves at the mercy of bandits and thieves.

Borders hardened into battlegrounds as nations hoarded resources, unwilling to risk their survival for the good of others. Old rivalries reignited as the strongest kingdoms exploited each other, seizing land under the guise of reclamation. Trust was a fleeting commodity, bartered away as easily as food or gold.

Even within the walls of the newly built cities and the kingdoms that somehow survived, life was tumultuous. The shadows of the dragon's faithful endured, its influence thriving in the chaos. They brought stability but their existence ensured the dragon's legacy lived on.

And yet, in the darkness, there was some small light of hope. A deep resilience was born \- though it was a grim and hard time, those who survived showed strength and perseverance in the face of annihilation.

Now the world rebuilds...

## The Third Age (1-402 RC)

### The Dawn of Recovery (1-50 RC)

The Third Age began with the end of the Sundering, marking humanity's calendar with the Reino Concordium system. The surviving nations emerged from the ashes fundamentally changed \- smaller, hardier, and acutely aware of their mortality. The God-Dragons' devastation had taught every survivor a brutal lesson: weakness invited annihilation.

What followed was not true revival, but desperate reconstruction. The Holy Reino Empire consolidated power around the Fe Alada faith, using religious unity to bind together scattered human settlements. The Greater Groves withdrew into their sacred forests, while the Dwergaz Triumvirate sealed their mountain halls. Each nation began the long process of rebuilding not just their cities, but their capacity for war.

### The Great Arms Race (50-200 RC)

By the middle of the first century, it became clear that the Sundering had not created peace \- it had created a power vacuum. Nations that had once relied on ancient alliances now stood alone, forced to choose between military strength and extinction. The Tagarian Coalition leveraged their maritime supremacy to expand across coastal territories, while the Tor'Az Triumvirate transformed their legendary craftsmanship into an industrial war machine.

The Holy Reino Empire's aggressive expansion of Fe Alada doctrine during this period laid the groundwork for future conflicts. Their systematic persecution of southern populations and conversion campaigns created deep religious tensions that would later explode into open warfare.

### The Orkhaden Resurgence (200-300 RC)

The rise of God-King Khor and the unification of the Orkh tribes fundamentally altered Ressam's balance of power. What had once been scattered warbands became the unified Orkhaden Horde under Ghorzuk Firemaw's strategic leadership. Their ancient occupation of central Halig transformed from passive control into active expansion, while their pressure on Inggaz forced both the Triumvirate and Coalition to divert resources to defensive positions.

This period also saw the emergence of the Chorus of Murmurs as a major power, their expansion into northern Terrevault creating the ongoing Winter War with the Triumvirate.

### The Modern Crisis (350-402 RC)

The current era is defined by the emergence of the Gilded Hwispian Maw as an existential threat. Their alchemical slavery and expansionist doctrine, fuelled by their new leader's dogma, has triggered multiple simultaneous conflicts:

**The Dzinari Wars**

* **The Dunestrife War:** Desert Nomads and Alashar tribes unite against Hwispian expansion into their sacred lands.
* **The Northwall War:** The Holy Reino Empire's northern colonies fight desperately against Hwispian forces, their resistance bolstered by useful local faiths the Empire tolerates as bulwarks against Dzinari influence.

**The Succession Crisis**

The secession of Asalter island (circa 390 RC) over trade rights has created a naval standoff between the Holy Reino Empire and Tagarian Coalition. While both powers are engaged in larger conflicts, this dispute represents a fundamental challenge to the Empire's authority and the Coalition's growing maritime dominance.

**The Eshzera War**

The most dramatic recent development (circa 400 RC) was the sudden reversal of alliances in Aurkhan. The Holy Reino Empire's unexpected peace with the Greater Groves and subsequent material support for the Aelves against the Tagarian Coalition shocked diplomatic observers. Most believe this represents a calculated move to weaken the Coalition while they're overextended in multiple theaters.

**The Haligean Crisis**

The sacred site occupation by Orkhaden forces has created the unusual spectacle of the Holy Reino Empire and Tagarian Coalition cooperating with local human tribes. This temporary alliance, driven by Halig trade offerings, demonstrates how even bitter rivals can unite against Orkh expansion.

### Current State (402 RC)

Ressam stands at a crossroads. The Tagarian Coalition maintains naval supremacy but faces enemies on multiple fronts. The Holy Reino Empire struggles with religious persecution in the south while fighting Dzinari expansion in the north. The Tor'Az Triumvirate's industrial might is divided between the Winter War and Inggaz skirmishes. Meanwhile, the Gilded Hwispian Maw continues its methodical conquest, turning populations into unwilling soldiers through chemical dependency.

The various blockades, trade wars, and shifting alliances suggest that the current multipolar conflict system is unsustainable. Whether Ressam will see a great consolidation war or continued fragmentation depends on which power can successfully navigate the complex web of modern warfare, economics, and diplomacy that defines this volatile age.

### Key Ongoing Conflicts (402 RC)

* **Dunestrife War:** Nomads vs. Hwispian Maw.
* **Northwall War:** Holy Reino Empire vs. Hwispian Maw.
* **Eshzera War:** Greater Groves vs. Tagarian Coalition.
* **Haligean War:** Multi-alliance vs. Orkhaden occupation.
* **Winter War:** Tor'Az Triumvirate vs. Chorus of Murmurs.
* **Inggaz Skirmishes:** Orkhaden Horde vs. Triumvirate \& Coalition.
* **Asalter Crisis:** Naval tensions between Empire and Coalition.
* **Southern Persecutions:** Holy Reino Empire internal religious conflicts.
