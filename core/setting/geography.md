## The Mourning Star

### *The Servant Sun*

In defiance of cosmic order, Ressam commands its own star, the Mourning Star; it traces a slow orbit around the planet itself \- a testament to divine power that once shaped this world. This impossible arrangement marks Ressam as cosmically unique, a world that was once so blessed by the gods that even the heavens bent to serve it.

**Effects on Ressam**

* **Unnatural Seasons.** As Mourning Star orbits, it creates seasonal variations unlike any normal world. The star's changing distance and angle produce complex climate patterns, with some regions experiencing multiple "summers" and "winters" as the sun's path shifts.
* **Divine Radiation.** The star's proximity and its orbital mechanics envelop Ressam in divine energies that once supported life. Although not deadly as all beings inherently possess the holy ability to resist them \- they signify the inevitable decline of the once-grand world, as this immunity will ultimately falter.
* **Gravitational Strain.** The divine forces binding a star to Ressam strain the world itself, warping gravity and shaking the land with ceaseless quakes. The world groans under the weight of its own divine legacy.

**Cultural Significance**

* **Symbol of Lost Glory.** To Ressam's inhabitants, the orbiting sun serves as a daily reminder of their world's former divine favor and current abandonment. It represents both the height of godly power and the depths of their fall.
* **Theological Despair.** Many faiths interpret Mourning Star as a prisoner, bound to serve a world whose gods are dead. Others see it as a faithful servant, continuing its duty even after its masters' destruction.
* **Calendars of Decay.** The star's orbit creates complex timekeeping systems, but also marks the gradual decay of the divine arrangement. Astronomers track subtle changes in Mourning Star's path, predicting when the cosmic miracle might finally collapse.

## Patéras and Mitéra

### *The Father and the Mother*

Patéras and Mitéra are Ressam's twin moons, orbiting in a synchronized path with Patéras leading and Mitéra trailing. This arrangement creates a gravitational resonance that causes subtle shifts in their positions while maintaining their sequence.

**Effects on Ressam**

* **Tides.** The moons' combined gravitational pull generates complex tidal patterns, with high tides occurring up to four times daily. These rapid shifts have led to coastal ecosystems uniquely adapted to frequent changes and cultures that synchronize activities like fishing and trade with the moons' movements.
* **Light and Shadows.** Mitéra and Patéras are small and bright, moving quickly across the sky, providing frequent but brief illumination. Nights are rarely completely dark, with alternating dim and bright phases. Their light creates rapidly shifting shadows, adding an eerie quality to the nighttime landscape.
* **Seismic Activity.** The gravitational interplay between Ressam and its moons contributes to frequent minor tremors, especially in tectonically active regions like Inggaz.

**Cultural Significance**

* **Religious Symbolism.** To many, the tandem orbit symbolizes divine harmony, with Patéras as a guiding protector and Mitéra as a nurturing follower. Themes of balance, protection, and unity are central to many belief systems. The Dwergaz, whose people are cut from a birthing-vein believed to be one of the two moons, dispute which of the two the honour belongs to - see the Dwergaz's own page for their creeds on the matter.
* **Mythology.** Legends portray Patéras as a steadfast leader and Mitéra as a loyal companion, weaving their movements into tales of pursuit, loyalty, and the creation of time and tides.
* **Calendars and Timekeeping.** The moons' crossings serve as a natural celestial clock, shaping daily schedules, intricate calendars, and seasonal festivals. Alignments often mark the start of new days or special occasions.
* **Navigation and Astrology.** The moons' orbits guide sailors and travellers, while astrologers attribute meaning to their alignments, using them to predict events and divine favour.
* **Art and Literature.** The dynamic relationship between Mitéra and Patéras inspires widespread artistic expression. They are often depicted as celestial dancers or parents, their movements symbolizing unity and duality. This influence extends to architecture, aligned with moonrise and moonset, and poetry that uses their interplay as metaphors for connection.

Mitéra and Patéras are more than celestial phenomena; they are vital to Ressam's natural world and cultural identity, shaping its tides, tremors, and the imaginations of its people.

## Continents

### Aurkhan

**Overview.** Aurkhan is the northernmost continent, of boundless life and verdant greenery. Its dense jungles stretch as far as the eye can see, pulsating with divine energy. Rivers snake through the landscape, creating wetlands and lagoons teeming with exotic creatures. The air is thick with humidity and the buzz of unseen life.

**Inhabitants.** The Aelves are the largest population in Aurkhan, maintaining many groves and enclaves. The Tapio can also be found here, tending to the jungle as its spiritual caretakers, living in harmony with the land.

**Key Locations.**

* **Sylvara Canopy.** A sprawling arboreal city built into the treetops, where Tapio elders meet.
* **Choking Wastes.** A perilous taiga, overrun with carnivorous plants and ancient ruins of a long dead nation.

---

### Lustralis

**Overview.** Lustralis is the largest western continent, a place of great extremes, with sprawling deserts, lush coastal rainforests, rolling savannahs, and coral-rich coastlines. Its heart is dominated by the Great Salt Expanse, a vast, arid basin where shimmering mirages and dangerous predators lure travelers to their doom. Despite its dangers, Lustralis is a land of innovation and survival.

**Inhabitants.** Humanity and the reptilian Alsahli have made this diverse land their home. Humans thrive in coastal cities and fertile plains, while the Alsahli form nomadic bands in the deserts. Varulf haunt the misted woods and forgotten valleys further inland \- feared rather than counted among Lustralis' settled peoples.

**Key Locations.**

* **Gran Reino.** The largest human kingdom, home to a massive castle where the empire gets its namesake.
* **Eyre Basin.** A shimmering salt flat rumored to hide ancient artifacts.
* **Verdant Cliffs.** Forested highlands where cascading waterfalls supply water to nearby human settlements.
* **The Great Sand.** A massive desert within the Scorched Dunes, roamed by the Desert Nomads and Alsahli, guided by the stars and ancient trails.
* **The Château Dubien.** A castle dedicated to those in service to the God Henri.

---

### Inggaz

**Overview.** Inggaz is a southern continent that connects to Terrevault. The north is made up of expansive plains of molten fury, where rivers of lava carve paths through jagged obsidian rocks, while the south is frigid and frozen, reaching extreme colds. The air is thick with sulphur, and geysers of steam and ash burst from the ground, creating a fiery, ever-changing landscape. Inggaz's regions include volcanic plateaus, basalt cliffs, and fields of hardened magma that glisten under the dim red light of its perpetual fiery haze.

**Inhabitants.** The Orkhs and Khoridae dominate this harsh land, thriving in its brutal environment. Orkhs live in warbands scattered across the lava fields, while Khoridae inhabit fortified citadels perched atop basalt cliffs.

**Key Locations.**

* **Molten Steppes.** A labyrinth of lava flows and hardened crust, where Orkhs engage in ritual combat to prove their strength.
* **Ashen Spires.** Towering formations of cooled magma housing Khoridae strongholds.
* **The Infernal Gate.** A colossal fissure believed to be a gateway to the Fields of Nocturne, where Daemons occasionally emerge.

---

### Terrevault

**Overview.** Terrevault is the furthest north eastern continent, made up of towering mountain ranges, steep terraced valleys, and meandering rivers that carve through lush landscapes. The peaks of the continent are shrouded in mist, creating an ethereal beauty that inspires awe. Dense forests and hidden caves dot the mountains, concealing treasures and dangers alike.

**Inhabitants.** The industrious Dwergaz dwell within the mountain halls, crafting masterpieces of stone and metal, while the fierce Tierratuar roam the rugged terrain, guarding their nomadic clans' secrets.

**Key Locations.**

* **Tradehaven.** The only above-ground Dwergaz city, meant as a giant trading bazaar for which the Dwergaz sell their services and goods. Possibly the most diverse city in Ressam.
* **Karak's Spine.** The highest mountain range in Ressam, home to the Dwergaz capital of Karak Tor, a fortress of stone and steel.
* **Jade Chasm.** A lush valley with terraced farms, waterfalls, and sacred sites.
* **Labyrinthine Caverns.** Underground tunnels roamed by the Tierratuar, who fiercely hunt anyone who enters.

---

### Gelidia

**Overview.** Gelidia is a large island continent between Aurkhan and Terrevault in the north. A frozen expanse of tundra, glaciers, and icy fjords. Towering mountain ranges and frozen lakes dominate the landscape, while perpetual blizzards scour the land. It is a harsh, unforgiving continent, but its stark beauty and isolation foster deep spiritual introspection.

**Inhabitants.** The ethereal Dzinari find solace in the solitude of Gelidia, crafting philosophies and magic in their icy sanctuaries. The agile and instinct-driven Feliids roam the snowy forests, their feline forms perfectly suited to the cold.

**Key Locations.**

* **Frostspire Citadel.** A shimmering palace of ice where the Dzinari convene.
* **Aurora Glades.** A frozen forest illuminated by magical auroras, revered as sacred by the Feliids.
* **Glacial Abyss.** A massive crevasse said to lead to ancient, buried secrets of the Great Artists.
* **Aegislith Veil.** The ethereal home of the Dzinari.

---

### The Gran Mar

**Overview.** The Gran Mar, or Central Sea, is the largest body of brackish water that connects the five major landmasses together. Within its waters, navigation is perilous, but many live on the islands that sit off the coasts.

**Inhabitants.** Many inhabit the massive floating trade hubs and islands that dot the Gran Mar, but none more so than the Humans and Feliids, both of whom make up the sailors and maritime crew of most ships.

**Key Locations.**

* **Pearling Bay.** A free-port and commune ruled by a mortal-god of trickery. The island thrives as a sanctuary for outcasts, revolutionaries, and anyone else who rejects authority.
* **The Eternal Storm.** Massive rogue storms crash and rampage across the center of the ocean.
* **Asalter.** Once one of Gran Reino's most profitable trading ports. Having grown tired with the tax structure of Gran Reino, they have seceded, with the Tagarian Coalition graciously offering them protection. Gran Reino wishes to dispute this, however they don't want to risk an open war.

### The Southlands

**Overview.** The Southlands are a cursed land, filled with horrors, murderous beasts, and ravenous undead. The very soil is infested with the dark, ancient powers of the long-dead empire that once inhabited these grounds, causing the earth to tremble with malice. The dead are denied peace and rise again to wander eternally in pain, sorrow, and hunger. The wildlife is twisted and misshapen, exhibiting unnaturally aggressive and savage behaviour. In the darkest corners, unspeakable horrors lie in wait, eager to tear apart both mind and flesh.

**Inhabitants.** Very few inhabit the Southlands, being too inhospitable for more, but the Tagarian Coalition have made some small outposts there, making up the only major settlements as they continue to fight the land itself.

**Key Locations.** Unknown \- shrouded in uncertainty.

### Halig

**Overview.** The Halig Plains stretch across a temperate highland plateau, where gentle grasslands give way to diverse biomes that shift dramatically with distance from the center. With temperatures ranging from frigid to boiling hot, this fertile land once knew peace under the stewardship of its harmonious people. Now, the shadow of war darkens the interior as the brutal Orkhaden Horde claims the heartland, forcing the displaced Halig to defend their sacred traditions from the edges of their ancestral home.

**Inhabitants.** Those of Halig are contemplative and community-minded people whose culture revolves around collective harmony and spiritual reverence for their land. They live in communes that emphasize shared resources and peaceful coexistence. The invading Orkhaden Horde \- known simply as "Sweeping Death" to the Halig \- are a fierce warrior culture that has seized the central grasslands, disrupting centuries of tranquil pastoral life.

**Key Locations.**

* **Baltash.** The largest Tembel commune and the beating heart of Halig commerce, this settlement serves as both a trading hub and a beacon of hope for the displaced. Its markets bustle with refugees and merchants alike, while its leadership coordinates resistance efforts.
* **Aappiyam.** A sacred cultural site featuring a magnificent natural formation of towering rock spires that rise like ancient fingers toward the sky \- one of the tallest landmarks in all of Halig. This holy place now lies under Orkh occupation, its desecration a source of deep anguish for the Halig people.
* **The Northern Reaches.** Where the grasslands fade into lush tropical seasonal forests and dense rainforests, creating a humid refuge for Halig communities that have fled the central occupation.
* **The Southern Wetlands.** A maze of marshes and temperate rainforests where mist-shrouded Tembel sanctuaries offer shelter and spiritual guidance to those seeking refuge from the war.

### Trere

**Overview.** Trere is Ressam's most southwestern continent: its eastern edge joins Halig, its northern reaches share a land border with Lustralis along the Great Sand, and the South Seas divide it from the Southlands further south still. The land shifts through a full gradient of biomes as it runs south: the dunes of the Great Sand give way to open savanna, then tropical seasonal forest, then deciduous woodland, before dissolving into temperate rainforest and wetland at its southern coast. Three empires once ruled here before the Sundering. Two left recognizable heirs \- Mafsoleios and the Gilded Hwispian Maw both trace back to Trere's fallen dynasties \- while the third has all but vanished from memory, its remnants said to linger somewhere between the Southlands and Trere itself.

**Inhabitants.** Mafsoleios, on the western coast, and Svarnanagaram, further inland, are Trere's only true settlements, and both stand apart from the rest of the continent in almost every way \- a lich-ruled, agrarian city-state and a fortress-capital inherited by the Gilded Hwispian Maw, amid otherwise scattered, decentralized peoples. Away from their walls, pockets of Alashar Tribes and Desert Nomads range the northern Great Sand \- the same desert, and the same peoples, that continue north into Lustralis. South of the Eldritch Passage, Trere Island is close to empty, its coastline marked only by a handful of Tagarian Coalition colonies.

**Key Locations.**

* **Mafsoleios.** One of Trere's two major settlements, ruled by the Undying Senate \- see Nations for its government, economy, and the Grand Gnosion.
* **Svarnanagaram.** The Gilded Hwispian Maw's capital and the other of Trere's two major settlements \- the old imperial seat of one of Trere's three fallen pre-Sundering empires, inherited whole by the Maw rather than built. Far less grand and esoteric than the Chorus of Murmurs' Aegislith Veil, it remains blunt fortified stone \- see Nations for the Maw's government and the Golden Choir.
* **The Sea of Trere.** The waters off Mafsoleios' western coast, home to the city's only dock.
* **The Eldritch Passage.** The sea route separating mainland Trere from Trere Island \- avoided less for any real danger than for its evil reputation, unpredictable tides, ceaseless quakes, and near-constant foul weather, one of the regions most affected by the moons. Used almost exclusively by Tagarian Coalition traders bound for Mafsoleios and Holy Reino Empire prison ships making the long voyage north to Noskirk Colony, past the Sawolleas Sea.
* **Trere Island.** A largely abandoned landmass south of the Eldritch Passage, home only to a few small Tagarian Coalition colonies serving as naval waypoints.

## Planes

### Mortal Plane

**Overview.** The foundation of reality \- a realm where the fundamental laws of existence operate with predictable consistency. Unlike the chaotic Fields of Nocturne, where nightmares reshape the landscape with each dreamer's terror, the Mortal Plane remains anchored by immutable physical laws. Here, gravity pulls downward, time flows forward in an unbroken stream, and space extends outward in measurable, navigable dimensions.

This is the realm of observable reality: stars burn in fixed constellations, mountains endure for millennia, and rivers carve predictable paths to the sea. Magic may exist here, but it operates within established principles rather than the wild, unpredictable manifestations born from collective unconscious fear. Where Nocturne's mercury-fire sky reflects impossible geometries, the Mortal Plane's heavens follow celestial mechanics that astronomers can chart and predict.

Most importantly, the Mortal Plane provides stability. Its landmarks don't shift with the whims of sleeping minds, its distances remain constant, and its inhabitants can build lasting civilizations knowing that their foundations won't dissolve with the next nightmare. This reliability makes it both a sanctuary from dimensional chaos and the anchor point from which other planes \- like the ever-changing Fields of Nocturne \- can be understood and, perhaps, eventually navigated.

### Fields of Nocturne

**Overview.** The dimensional plane, the Fields of Nocturne, is an ever-changing hellscape made up of the nightmares and dreams of the mortal races. It technically mirrors our universe; however, traversing off of familiar landmarks is impossible, as the collective energy of all those who dream ensures that Nocturne is always changing form. This makes traversing it nearly impossible, and even for its inhabitants, it's still perilous.

If you ever dared to look up in Nocturne, you'd see a sky, not of the beyond, but of an endless sea of mirror-like fire, desaturated and blue. But the fire reflects like light; if you don't peer too closely, it appears as a sea of mercury.

Old ruined structures and landmarks act as the only familiar sights in Nocturne.

In Inggaz there is a rift called the Infernal Gate. It allows a small portion of the creatures of Nocturne to escape. Most things die to the massive Orkh armies that see the portal as a religious trial, but the more crafty and subtle nightmares, most commonly Daemons and Strygs, can escape.
