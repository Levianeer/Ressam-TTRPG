## The Holy Reino Empire

**Overview.** A colossal, technologically advanced republic, predominantly made up of humans. This empire is centered at the north region of Lustralis and its influence carries a massive weight across the realm.

**Key Details:**

* Led by the High Lord, Victor Ardan, as both an emperor and pope.
* Its national currency is the Reno, one of the more valuable currencies.
* Its capital is Gran Reino, which holds the massive castle-state of the same name.
* The centrepiece of the Fe Alada's influence.
* The Empire maintains one of the largest land-based militaries in the realm.
* **The Portatores:** The breakthrough elite of the Holy Reino Empire, clad in impossibly heavy full plate reminiscent of jousting armor but designed for war on foot. They wield combination weapons, massive hackbuts fitted with halberd heads on the stock, allowing them to devastate at range and in melee alike. Recruitment demands both exceptional physical strength and unwavering faith in equal measure, for their armor is ritually blessed by Fe Alada clerics in a binding ceremony that allows the wearer to move freely within their iron shell. Without faith, the armor becomes a prison. In combat they advance in absolute silence, no war cries, no commands, no sound but the thunder of their guns and the crash of their halberds. Off the battlefield they are men with bonds and humor; in formation they become faceless instruments of divine judgment, identical and interchangeable. They serve as both shock troops for critical assaults and propaganda pieces, paraded through cities and stationed as living statues at cathedral doors. To face them is to face an inevitable wall of silent iron that shoots you at distance and butchers you up close.

## The Greater Groves

**Overview.** A massive council of united Aelfen groves. Originally created out of necessity during the Sundering, the groves decided to stay united even after the cataclysm had stopped, fearing attacks from any of the other great nations. The Greater Groves make up a large portion of Aurkhan.

**Key Details:**

* Led by Thalorien.
* The economy of the groves is based on a trade barter system, however they have been known to accept the Renos and the Crowns.
* Its capital changes each decade between the largest of the groves, in an effort to balance the power a single grove can maintain.
* Its military is relatively small, however Aelfen warriors often make up for their lack of numbers with superior skill and tactics.
* One of the least diverse nations, rarely even letting outsiders inside.

## The Tor'Az Triumvirate

**Overview.** A collection of underground cities and mining complexes under the rule of the legendary Triumvirate Three - the most powerful Dwergaz guild masters in all of Terrevault. It is the wealthiest industrial power in Ressam and, by population, one of the smallest of the great nations. These two facts are not a coincidence: Tor'Az is a nation that cannot replace its dead, and everything about it - the machines, the walls, the wealth, the silence - exists to make sure it never has to.

**Key Details:**

* Tor'Az is led by Durnir the Ironhand of the Barazel Guild, Runvar the Unshakable of the Matgadel Guild, and Dwarin the Industrious of the Dromgadol Guild. Each seat rests on a different pillar of survival: Barazel commands the wealth, Matgadel wards the deep halls and what lies beneath them, and Dromgadol holds the war-frontier of Tormaz.
* The economy of Tor'Az is diverse and massive, helped further by the sheer economic power of the city of Tradehaven. The Triumvirate arms nearly everyone - selling siege engines even to the enemies of its customers - on the standing doctrine that a nation which cannot grow its numbers must grow its leverage.
* Karak's Spine is the capital, officially for security reasons, keeping the Triumvirate away from Tradehaven when possible. What the official reason omits is what is being secured: the deep places of the capital are the most heavily warded ground in Ressam, and no outsider has ever learned why and lived to publish it.
* Its military is sizable, but its true advantage lies in unparalleled production capacity - a wealth of siege engines and machines of war fielded on a doctrine the engineers call "the arithmetic": no Dwergaz stands where iron can stand instead. Foreign generals note that Tor'Az wins its wars while losing astonishingly few soldiers, and mistake this for caution rather than necessity.
* A very xenophobic nation - though its wariness is less hatred than security, and it maintains exactly one open door: Tradehaven, among the most diverse cities in Ressam, where you can find almost anything or anyone. The deep clans tolerate the city as a necessary profanity; every secret Tor'Az keeps is kept by making sure outsiders never need to look past it.
* The Triumvirate is quietly divided against itself. Terrevault's orthodox guilds regard Dwarin's Dromgadol and its creations with open theological hostility, the three lunar creeds cannot agree on the calendar by which the nation's most sacred rites are performed, and the ongoing wars in the north and in Inggaz are bleeding Tor'Az of the one resource it cannot manufacture.

## The Orkhaden Horde

**Overview.** The Orkhaden "Horde" is a fearsome coalition of Orkh warbands united under the banner of the God-King Khor, a legendary Orkh warlord who ascended to near godhood. Born from the molten fury of Inggaz, the Horde embodies the destructive and creative power of fire, valuing strength, loyalty, and conquest above all else. Unlike the rigid hierarchies of other nations, the Horde operates as a loose but deadly confederation, where power is seized through combat and maintained through sheer force.

**Key Details:**

* The capital is the Pyral Spire, a fortress carved into the heart of an active volcano in Inggaz. Its throne room is a cavern of molten rock, where challengers must prove their worth by surviving a trial by fire.
* The current "Champion" of the God-King Khor is Ghorzuk Firemaw, a cunning and brutal tactician infamous for his un-Orkh-like strategic mind and his ability to turn enemies against each other.
* Primarily based on raiding, slavery, and tribute. The Horde trades looted goods, weapons, and captives with factions like the Tagarian Coalition (for exotic weapons) or the Tor'Az Triumvirate (for Dwergaz siege engines).
* The Orkhaden Horde maintains a decentralized but overwhelming force. Each warband is led by a Warlord, who answers only to Ghorzuk and the God-King's priesthood. Their tactics rely on shock assaults, fire magic, and the terrifying Khoridae.

## The Alashar Tribes

**Overview.** Lacking an official capital, the Alashar form a loose collection of tribes in the foothills of the mountains that make up the eastern border of the Great Sand. These communities are led by a local and hereditary "Hetyah," but in times of war, will often elect a central leader from among them.

**Key Details:**

* The largest village in Alashar is Tushtai, which is not that much larger than most of Alashar's villages, however it is the easiest to reach for outsider traders.
* Skilled craftsmen make various goods such as weapons and pottery, which are highly valued for their intricate decoration. The most lucrative export, however, is the wealth of well-trained mercenaries.
* Although on paper Alashar's military is insignificant, in wartime it can call on a massive reserve of highly skilled professional soldiers.
* The Alsahli are generally not negatively predisposed towards outsiders, but it depends highly on the village.

## The Tagarian Coalition

**Overview.** The Feliid Tagarian Coalition is an alliance between three major city-states, each of which sees itself as the capital. The first is Felnis, on the Island of Chossris, the largest island off the coast of Aurkhan and the original home of the Feliids. The next largest is Kalitona, on the Alhona peninsula on the northwestern tip of Inggaz. And finally the most distant but lucratively positioned near Dwergaz territory is Sokhara, located in the southern jungles of Gelidia. In addition to the three major cities, numerous ports and colonies litter the coasts of the Gran Mar.

**Key Details:**

* The Tagarian Coalition is officially ruled by the "Koté," though most of the political weight comes from the wealthy merchant princes that form the Council of the Sages.
* The Coalition's territory has expanded a lot since its formation, now controlling the coastal islands of Aurkhan and portions of Gelidia.
* The Tagarian Coalition is known for its fine glasswork, expensive fabrics and quality shipbuilding, which are its most vital economic contributions.
* The military might of the Coalition is often understated, as the actual size of their military is hard to estimate because they rely heavily on mercenaries for their footmen. But it's known that their naval fleet is second only to the Gran Reino Empire's, though the size of their ships far dwarfs that of the Reino Empire.
* The Tagarian Coalition is a surprisingly welcoming nation and generally accepts other races, with an exception for the Aelves, with whom they have been engaged in ongoing skirmishes since landing on the mainland of Aurkhan.
* **The Donati:** The Council of Sages' answer to the great families' private forces, orphans plucked from the streets as young children, raised outside any house allegiance, and reborn as the Council's hands. Led by the "il Datore," currently a young prodigy known as Soren Vel. Their training forges them into siblings and rivals both, culminating in the mask ceremony where they receive porcelain faces shaped as stylized great cats: snarling leopards, cold-eyed panthers, imperious lions. Each mask is assigned based on years of observation, their superiors telling them who they have become. The porcelain is deliberately impractical, a taunt that says you will never touch me. When masks crack, they are repaired with gold filling, highlighting cracks \- failures survived; veterans' faces are seamed with gleaming history. Beneath the masks they wear dark, expensive fitted clothing suitable for ship work and wetwork alike, claws ungloved. Armed with rapiers, daggers, and pistols in the swashbuckler style, they serve as assassins, spies, and enforcers against overreaching families. The great houses sneer and call them Strays; the Donati wear it like a badge. They maintain their own rank structure, with their leader answering directly to the Sages. Their name means "The Given" \- given life, given purpose, given faces. In return, they give everything.

## The Chorus of Murmurs

**Overview.** The Chorus of Murmurs is the empire of the Dzinari, enigmatic, wind-born beings that embody secrets and manipulation. Their society revolves around the acquisition and control of knowledge, which they hoard in their shifting city of Aegislith Veil that moves across their land. Their existence is tied to the Fields of Nocturne, and their voices are both their greatest weapon and their most sacred possession.

**Key Details:**

* Aegislith Veil, their capital, is built from frozen whispers \- solidified secrets stolen from mortals. Its location drifts with the wind, appearing only to those unlucky enough to stumble into its whispers.
* The ruling order, the Council of Murmurs, consists of those who exemplify knowledge and manipulation. Decisions are made through cryptic proverbs, riddles, and whispers, and outsiders who hear their councils often leave maddened by the contradictions and implications of their meanings.

## The Gilded Hwispian Maw

**Overview.** The Gilded Maw is a rogue faction of Dzinari who have abandoned the subtlety of the Chorus of Murmurs in favor of brutal, narcotic-fueled domination. They have perfected Deapsang, a hallucinogenic drug distilled from the crystallized screams of addicts, which grants mortals superhuman strength and euphoria \- but at a terrible cost. Those who consume it become physically dependent not just on the substance, but on continued access to it; withdrawal is fatal, making escape from the Maw's influence impossible once enslaved.

The faction is led by the countless-named Highest Devaraja of the Golden Throne. This singular being has transcended mortal limitations to become a "mortal god" \- possessing power that rivals divine beings while retaining the flawed, ambitious nature of his Dzinari heritage.

**Key Details:**

* **Alchemic Slavery:** The Gilded Maw are master alchemists who see themselves as "liberators" freeing mortals from the burden of choice by creating perfect chemical dependency. Their Deapsang ensures absolute loyalty through biological necessity \- slaves cannot rebel because rebellion means death.
* **Expansionist Conquest:** Unlike the Chorus, who manipulate from the shadows, the Gilded Maw carves out territory openly through systematic conquest. However, their expansion follows a careful strategy of legitimizing their rule \- they must make their conquests appear rightful and inevitable, building genuine belief in their authority rather than simply overwhelming opposition through raw force.
* **The Golden Choir:** An elite guard of Dzinari soldiers who have undergone surgical removal of not just their voices, but their entire capacity for communication or expression. This procedure \- considered the ultimate dishonor in Dzinari culture \- strips them of the ability to convey ideas through any means: speech, writing, gesture, or art. They exist in perfect psychological isolation, able to receive the Devaraja's telepathic commands but incapable of response, betrayal, or even communication with each other. The Devaraja appears to them as the sole source of beauty and connection in their otherwise silent existence, creating absolute psychological dependency alongside their loyalty. They serve as both honor guard and elite heavy infantry, their combat prowess undiminished by their sacrifice, and they are easily the strongest infantry unit in Ressam \- a threat to even minor immortal gods.
* **Strategic Patience:** The Maw's expansion is methodical rather than reckless, guided by their leader's understanding that true dominion requires the conquered to accept their defeat as legitimate. They wage wars of attrition and psychological manipulation as much as military campaigns, ensuring that each victory strengthens their claim to rightful rule rather than simply adding territory through force.
* **Svarnanagaram:** The Maw's capital sits on Trere itself \- the old imperial seat of one of the continent's three fallen pre-Sundering empires, inherited whole rather than built. Where the Chorus of Murmurs raised Aegislith Veil from centuries of whisper-magic, Svarnanagaram remains blunt, fortified stone, kept deliberately unchanged as proof of the Maw's claim to rightful inheritance.

## The Mafsoleios City-State

**Overview.** A small city-state on the western coast of Trere, mostly made up of humans. Led by a council of powerful Liches, known only as the Undying Senate. A relatively benevolent state, especially for Liches.

**Key Details:**

* The government of Mafsoleios is led by a council of the most powerful Liches in the Realm, the Undying Senate, which handles the state's affairs. More municipal issues are managed by a collection of apprentices and aspiring Liches known as the Shadow Senate.
* Mafsoleios' economy is largely agrarian. Fish, grain and wine make up its exports. However within the walls of Mafsoleios, numerous artisans ply trades.
* Hosts the largest port in Ressam, only being rivaled by some of the trade cities in the Holy Reino Empire.
* The city maintains a standing army made up of living soldiers, however the bulk of their military strength lies in the innumerable elite fighting force of undead.
* Soldiers who die are honoured through a necromantic ritual they celebrate as a funeral, giving the soldier a second chance to defend its home in eternal undeath \- famously one of the few ways a soul is retained after death.
* Mafsoleios contains the largest known collection of written works in the world, as part of its Grand Gnosion, an institution dedicated to the collection and preservation of knowledge from around the world. It draws scholars from across Ressam to study within its halls.

## The Ederani Al'Nur / The Desert Nomads

**Overview.** The Desert Nomads are a loose commune of human tribes, unified only by the bonds of shared faith, who travel with their herds across the arid expanses of Lustralis, particularly the Scorched Dunes and the foothills bordering the Great Sand. They are survivors, adapted to the merciless sun, shifting sands, and the dangers that lurk beneath them. Their way of life is defined by resilience, honor, and an unbreakable bond with the desert.

**Key Details:**

* The Nomads maintain no capital, but the largest gathering site is Madur, a semi-permanent oasis settlement where trade and diplomacy occur.
* Similarly to the Alashar, their main export comes from their skilled craftsmen. Their most lucrative exports are decorated blades, pottery, intricate tapestries and well-trained warriors-for-hire.
* Though decentralized, their warriors are renowned. They excel in guerrilla tactics, hit-and-run raids, and survival warfare.
* The Desert Nomads maintain an uneasy trade alliance with the Alashar, seeing their existence as taboo, but those less conservative will trade openly.

## Global Factions

### The Fellowship of Saint Cadogan

**Overview.** A highly secretive organisation, the Fellowship of Saint Cadogan is a group of scribes and scholars dedicated to protecting and preserving knowledge, built upon the tenets of Saint Cadogan, a scribe who was thrown upon a pyre of burning books on the order of a long forgotten tyrannical magistrate of the Gran Reino Empire.

**Key Details:**

* The Fellowship of Saint Cadogan has no central base of operations, instead they maintain a network in almost every single corner of every society.
* Outwardly they preach the philosophy of a martyred scholar, who was cast upon a pyre for trying to prevent a book burning.
* The organisation's true intentions are incredibly tight-lipped.

## Local Factions

### The Pirate Lords of Pearling Bay

**Overview.** Once a quiet island of whispering pines under the control of the Tagarian Coalition, Pearling Bay is now a raucous freeport and commune ruled by Sir Davil, the Heroic God-King of Thieves, Winds and Skies \- a mortal-god of trickery, rebellion, and liberation. The island thrives as a sanctuary for outcasts, revolutionaries, and those who reject authority, operating under a system where resources are shared, hierarchies are mocked, and wealth is "redistributed" (often by force or sleight of hand).

**Key Details:**

* The port is a hub of smuggling, piracy, and underground trade, openly hostile to the Coalition's merchant princes.
* Its people \- thieves, escaped slaves, dissidents, and heretics \- live by one rule: "From each according to their cunning, to each according to their need."
* Although not particularly powerful as a military force, Davil protects the island through bad headwinds and foul storms, making even reaching the island difficult.
