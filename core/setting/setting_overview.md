The world of Ressam is broken. The God-Dragons' wrath during the great Sundering left entire nations as ash and bone, their survivors crawling from the ruins to rebuild what they could salvage. Now, in the Third Age, 402 Reino Concordium (RC), those same survivors have grown into hardened powers, and they prepare for war. This is a world of Renaissance-era brutality where divine magic mingles with gunpowder, blade and ancient grudges that fester in the dark of rebuilt cities.

Above, the Mourning Star burns brightly in its subservient orbit around the world \- a dying sun bound by divine chains. The twin moons Patéras and Mitéra follow their endless chase. **For the sun, the moons, and their effects on the world, see Geography.**

This chapter covers the setting itself: its history, geography, nations, religions, and legendary figures. **For the ancestries player characters can belong to, see the Races chapter** - each race's own page also covers its culture, mythology, and place in the world in more depth than fits here.

## Currency

The fractured nations of Ressam mint coins that reflect their values and ambitions:

**Crowns & Scales** endure because the Dwergaz forge them to outlast empires. These practical coins \- brass Crowns reinforced with silver cores and humble brass Scales worth a hundredth each \- survive in blood-soaked battlefields and plague-emptied markets alike.

**Renos & Ferrins** proclaim the Holy Reino Empire's iron dominance. Each silver Reno bears High Lord Victor Ardan's stern visage, a reminder that imperial authority reaches into every transaction, while copper Ferrins ensure even the poorest feel the Empire's weight.

**Mar Scrips** represent the maritime innovation of the Tagarian Coalition \- paper currency sealed with magic against forgery and water damage, as essential in the salt-soaked ports of Asalter as they are among the free pirates of Pearling Bay.

In the great trading cities, currency exchanges buzz with the constant calculation of weight, purity, and magical worth.

## Language

**Plethic** serves as the common tongue of trade and diplomacy, though its fluid nature adapts to whoever speaks it. The musical **Tagaraal** of the Feliids is heard in every major port, while the deep, echoing **Khazduul** of the Dwergaz carries the weight of ancient oaths. The wind-touched **Aurkhani** of the Aelves remains largely confined to their sacred groves.

Human tongues are wonderfully diverse: flowing **Batakluhk** with its liquid consonants, scholarly **Mafari** rich in philosophical terms, and desert-worn **Zakhrafan** that carries across sand dunes. More exotic languages intrigue scholars \- the clicking **Grazn** of tunnel-dwelling Skeggs, harsh **Khorian** battle-shouts, the riddle-speech **Dzinariel** of wind-born mystiks, and the dangerous **Daemonic** that whispers corruption.

Most mysterious of all is **Dragon's Script** \- ancient glyphs that seem to shift meaning based on the reader's understanding, found carved into ruins that predate even the mortal races.
