Leadership represents your ability to attract, command, and retain those who serve under you \- whether loyal soldiers, hired laborers, or specialists who believe in your cause (or your coin). This system covers two types of followers: **Followers** (non-combatants who handle mundane tasks) and **Cohorts** (trained warriors who fight alongside you).

Followers exist to fill party gaps and handle logistics. Cohorts exist to provide tactical support. Neither should overshadow player characters \- they're support, not replacements.

---

## Followers

Followers are common folk who work for you \- servants, porters, scribes, grooms, cooks, and laborers. They have no combat training and will flee, surrender, or die if drawn into violence.

### **What Followers Are**

- **Non-combatants:** They do not fight. Ever. If combat occurs, they hide, flee, or get killed.  
- **Specialists or laborers:** Each follower has a function (cook, porter, scribe, stable hand)  
- **Expendable assets:** You probably don't know their names. They're staff, not companions.  
- **Level 1:** Followers never advance. They're common folk with common skills.

### **Follower Limitations**

- **No combat stats:** Followers don't have HP, Evasion, or AR in any meaningful sense. If targeted, they die or they flee.  
- **No player-created sheets:** The DM creates followers as needed. You describe what role you're hiring; the DM provides the person.  
- **Require upkeep:** Followers expect payment and reasonable treatment (see Upkeep below).

### **Acquiring Followers**

Followers are hired, not recruited. Visit a settlement and spend coin.

| Follower Type | Weekly Wage | Example Roles |
| :---- | :---: | :---- |
| Unskilled Labor | 1 Crown | Porter, torch-bearer, camp cook, groom |
| Skilled Labor | 5 Crown | Scribe, translator, healer's assistant, craftsman |
| Specialist | 10 Crown | Alchemist's apprentice, navigator, animal trainer |

**Availability:** Common labor is always available in settlements. Specialists require larger towns or specific circumstances (DM discretion).

### **Follower Capacity**

There's no hard cap on followers \- you can hire as many as you can afford and manage. However:

- **Practical limits:** Each follower needs food, shelter, and supervision  
- **Social limits:** Travelling with a small army draws attention  
- **Leadership check:** If commanding 5+ followers simultaneously, the DM may call for Leadership checks to keep them organized

### **Sample Follower (No Full Statblock)**

**Tomas, Camp Porter:**  
*Function:* Carries up to 100 lb of gear, sets up camp, tends fire  
*Wage:* 5 Crown/week  
*Notes:* Will not enter dungeons. Flees at first sign of danger. Complains about the weather.

## Cohorts

Cohorts are trained combatants who follow you into battle \- squires, mercenary sergeants, apprentice warriors, or devoted soldiers. They have full statistics, can fight, and grow alongside you.

### **What Cohorts Are**

- **Combat-capable:** They have full stat blocks and fight in combat  
- **Loyal but not suicidal:** They follow orders but have self-preservation instincts  
- **Player-defined (optional):** You may create your cohort's character sheet (same rules as a player apply), or the DM can provide one  
- **Limited in number:** You can only keep so many Cohorts per player

### **Maximum Cohorts**

**Maximum Cohorts: 1\.** With CHA 9+, you may instead maintain up to 2, but while you have two cohorts, both are one additional level lower (your level −3).

### **Cohort Level**

**Cohort Level \= Your Level \- 2 (minimum 1\)**

| Your Level | Cohort Level |
| :---: | :---: |
| 1-2 | 0 |
| 3 | 1 |
| 4 | 2 |
| 5 | 3 |
| ... | ... |
| 12 | 10 |

Cohorts advance when you do. They learn from you, grow with you, and share your experiences \- but they're never quite your equal.

### **Acquiring Cohorts**

Cohorts aren't hired like common labor. They're recruited through:

- **Roleplay:** Impress someone with your deeds, save their life, share a cause  
- **Reputation:** Your name draws warriors who want to serve  
- **Training:** Take on a squire or apprentice and invest time

**Leadership Check:** When attempting to recruit a cohort, make a Leadership \+ CHA check. The DC depends on the potential cohort's quality and your reputation:

| Circumstance | DC Modifier |
| :---- | :---: |
| You saved their life | \-5 |
| Shared ideology/faith | \-3 |
| Your reputation precedes you | \-2 |
| Cold recruitment (no prior relationship) | \+3 |
| They have better options | \+5 |

Base DC 15\. Success means they agree to follow you.

### **Cohort Upkeep**

Cohorts expect more than common wages \- they expect a share of the spoils and respect.

| Upkeep Type | Cost | Notes |
| :---- | :---: | :---- |
| Weekly wage | 25 Crown | Base expectation for a professional |
| Equipment | Variable | You provide or replace their gear |
| Share of treasure | 10% | Standard expectation from loot |

**Failure to pay:** A cohort denied wages or respect makes a Morale check (see below). Repeated failures lead to desertion.

### **Cohort Creation**

**Option 1: Player Creates:** You build your cohort's character sheet using standard character creation rules, but at their current level (your level \- 2). You choose their skills, attributes, and equipment within reason.

**Option 2: DM Provides:** Describe what you're looking for ("a crossbowman," "someone who knows medicine," "a shield-bearer"). The DM creates an appropriate cohort.

Either way, the DM has final approval. Your cohort is an NPC \- they have their own personality, limits, and will.

### **Cohorts in Combat**

- **Initiative:** Cohorts act on your initiative, immediately after your turn  
- **Commands:** You can issue one command per round (no action required). Without commands, cohorts defend themselves and stay near you.  
- **Independent action:** Complex tactics require Leadership checks (DC 10-15)  
- **Death:** Cohorts reduced to 0 HP follow normal dying rules. You can stabilize them.

## Morale & Loyalty

Followers and cohorts aren't mindless automatons. They have limits.

### **Morale Checks**

The DM calls for Morale checks when followers/cohorts face situations that test their commitment:

- Half their allies are dead or fled  
- Their leader (you) falls unconscious or flees  
- They're ordered to do something suicidal  
- They haven't been paid in weeks  
- They witness something terrifying

**Morale Check \= 1d12 \+ Your Leadership \+ Your CHA vs. DC 15**

- **Success:** They hold. Loyalty intact.  
- **Failure:** They break. Followers flee or surrender. Cohorts retreat or refuse orders.

### **Loyalty Modifiers**

| Condition | Morale DC Modifier |
| :---- | :---: |
| You've fought beside them for months | \-3 |
| You saved their life | \-3 |
| Shared faith or cause | \-2 |
| Well-paid and equipped | \-2 |
| They've seen you win against odds | \-2 |
| You've lost their respect (cruelty, cowardice) | \+3 |
| Unpaid wages | \+3 |
| Witnessing your apparent death | \+5 |

### 

### **Desertion & Betrayal**

Followers who fail morale checks simply leave. They might steal supplies, but rarely turn hostile.

Cohorts who fail morale checks may:

- Refuse a specific order but stay loyal  
- Retreat from combat but return later  
- Desert if treatment continues to be poor  
- Turn hostile if you've genuinely wronged them (rare, requires narrative cause)

## Death & Replacement

### **Follower Death**

Followers die easily. If caught in combat, targeted by area effects, or facing any real danger, assume they're dead unless you specifically protect them. There's no mechanical process \- common folk die in wars.

**Replacement:** Hire new followers in the next settlement. They're interchangeable.

### **Cohort Death**

Cohorts are characters. Their death should matter.

- **Dying:** Cohorts reduced to 0 HP follow standard dying rules (END rounds to stabilize)  
- **Resurrection:** Same rules as player characters (if your setting allows it)  
- **Replacement:** You can recruit a new cohort, but this takes time and roleplay. You can't just grab another one immediately.

**Mourning period:** You cannot recruit a new cohort for 1 week per level the dead cohort had reached. This represents the emotional investment and reputation concerns of losing someone under your command. However, the DM should be reasonable, a hated Cohort probably won’t be mourned.

## Quick Reference

### **Followers**

- **Cap:** None (practical limits apply)  
- **Level:** Always 1  
- **Combat:** None \- they flee or die  
- **Sheet:** DM creates as needed  
- **Upkeep:** 5-30 Crown/week depending on skill

### **Cohorts**

- **Cap:** CHA ÷ 3 (max 3\)  
- **Level:** Your Level \- 2 (min 1\)  
- **Combat:** Full participation  
- **Sheet:** Player may create (DM approval)  
- **Upkeep:** 50 Crown/month \+ equipment \+ 10% treasure share

### **Key Formulas**

| Calculation | Formula |
| :---- | :---- |
| Maximum Cohorts | CHA ÷ 3 (min 1, max 3\) |
| Cohort Level | Your Level \- 2 (min 1\) |
| Morale Check | 1d12 \+ Leadership \+ CHA vs. DC 15 |
| Recruitment DC | 15 ± circumstance modifiers |

## Sample Cohort

**Mira Valdros, Squire** *Level 2 Human*

**HP:** 22 | **Evasion:** 11 | **AR:** 6 (Gambeson) **Initiative:** \+4

**Attributes:** STR 5, REF 4, END 5, DEX 4, MIND 4, ARC 2, FAI 3, CHA 3

**Skills:** One-Handed Blades \+3, Shields \+2, Athletics \+2, Armorer \+2, Riding \+2, Perception \+1

**Equipment:**

- Shortsword (1d6 piercing, Light, 11-12 crit)  
- Targe Shield (+1 AR when blocking)  
- Gambeson (6 AR)  
- Armorer's Tools

**Personality:** Eager to prove herself, follows orders without question, secretly terrified of dying before making her name.

## DM Guidance

**Keep it simple.** This system is intentionally rules-lite. When in doubt:

- Followers do their jobs or flee. No rolls needed for routine tasks.  
- Cohorts follow orders unless the order is suicidal or contradicts their nature.  
- Morale checks happen when dramatically appropriate, not constantly.  
- If players abuse the system (army of cohorts, treating followers as ablative meat shields), apply social consequences \- reputation matters in Ressam.

**Followers are furniture.** Don't name them unless it matters. They carry things, cook food, and die in droves when dragons attack. That's their role.

**Cohorts are characters.** Give them personalities, flaws, and goals. Let them grow. Make their deaths mean something. A cohort who's been with the party for six months and dies saving the party should hit harder than a random NPC death.
