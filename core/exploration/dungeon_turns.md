## The Turn

Exploring a dungeon, ruin, or other dangerous site runs on a slower clock than combat. A **Turn** represents **10 minutes** of careful, in-fiction time \- distinct from combat's 6-second round (see Combat) and hex travel's day-long pace (see Traveling).

The DM keeps one shared tally for the whole party, not a clock on each character's sheet. Mark off a Turn whenever the party does something that costs one.

---

## What Costs a Turn

This isn't an exhaustive list \- rule it, don't look it up. As a guideline, a Turn passes when the party:

- Searches a room carefully for traps, secrets, or loot  
- Picks a lock or forces a stuck door  
- Moves cautiously through unfamiliar or hazardous ground  
- Rests briefly to catch their breath (not a full Short Rest)  
- Listens at a door, or otherwise takes a moment to be careful

Fast, decisive actions (opening an unlocked door, glancing into a room, drawing a weapon) don't cost a Turn. When in doubt, ask: would a real party spend real minutes doing this? If yes, it's a Turn.

---

## Light Tracker

Light sources burn down in Turns, tracked on the same shared party tally (see Core Rules, Light and Vision):

| Source  | Duration             |
|:--------|:---------------------|
| Candle  | 6 Turns              |
| Torch   | 6 Turns              |
| Lantern | 36 Turns / oil flask |

Mark a light source's Turns against the party tracker as they elapse, not against whichever character happens to be holding it. When a source runs out, it goes dark immediately \- plan ahead.

---

## Wandering Encounters

The same tally that tracks Turns and light also drives wandering-encounter checks: one clock, two jobs. The DM sets a check frequency appropriate to how dangerous or populated the area is \- roughly every 2-4 Turns in an active dungeon, less often somewhere quiet. Being loud, resting in the open, or lingering in one spot are all good reasons to check early.

**Example:** The party spends 2 Turns searching a ransacked study, then 1 Turn forcing open a jammed door. That's 3 Turns off the shared tracker \- their torch (6 Turns) is half spent, and the DM rolls a wandering check per their usual frequency for this level of the dungeon.
