# Issues

Open worldbuilding questions, contradictions, and flagged-for-later items.
Not brainstorms (those go in `work/brainstorm/`) - these are specific,
addressable gaps or decisions someone needs to make before related lore can
be written with confidence.

  explicitly out of scope for this lore bible until rescoped. When it's
  picked up, it should probably get its own `kb/` treatment (likely its own
  subdirectory, since it's a large, self-contained body of work) rather than
  being folded into the existing structure as an afterthought.
- **The Black Blades** - the Greater Groves' signature military order,
  named but "*To be written.*" in `core/setting/nations.md`. Compare to the
  Portatores/Donati/Golden Choir treatment (`kb/world/factions.md`) when
  drafting - it should get the same one-dense-paragraph treatment.
- **Mafsoleios' Trere origin isn't in `nations.md` yet.** The Gilded
  Hwispian Maw's half of this is now resolved (see Resolved, below) - its
  capital Svarnanagaram makes the Trere-dynasty link explicit. Mafsoleios'
  own entry still doesn't state it. Small addition, not urgent - fold in
  next time that entry is revisited.
- **Noskirk Colony and the wider Sawolleas Sea/Inggaz-Terrevault region
  need a detail pass.** Noskirk Colony (Reino Empire penal colony) is named
  but unplaced beyond "near the Sawolleas Sea" - see `kb/world/vocab.md`.
- **Which moon is the Dwergaz Great Mother** - Patéras or Mitéra - is
  deliberately unresolved/disputed in canon (three creeds disagree), and the
  author confirmed (2026-07-19) it should stay standardized/settled
  eventually but is deferred for now. Do not resolve unilaterally; only
  touch this if a specific piece of new lore needs it settled, and treat
  that as a real canon decision requiring sign-off, not a background detail
  to quietly pick.
- **Divine Radiation immunity fading** - `geography.md` states the
  Mourning Star's radiation immunity "will ultimately falter" but gives no
  timeline. Worth deciding whether this stays a distant background stake or
  becomes an active plot lever before writing anything that depends on it.

## Resolved

- **Devaraja/Alsahli connection** (2026-07-20). The Devaraja who leads the
  Gilded Hwispian Maw today is the same being who, in the deep past, cursed
  a race of human conquerors into the Alsahli - see
  `kb/characters/mortal-gods.md`, Devaraja, and
  `core/character/races/alsahli.md`'s Origins and Mythology. Which god's
  favoured children the conquerors killed stays deliberately unspecified,
  same treatment as the Dwergaz Great Mother question below - don't
  resolve it unilaterally.
- **Gilded Hwispian Maw's capital** (2026-07-20). Named Svarnanagaram - the
  old imperial seat of their Trere-empire predecessor, inherited whole
  rather than built, deliberately blunter/less esoteric than Aegislith
  Veil. Written into `core/setting/nations.md` and `geography.md`, synced
  to `kb/world/nations.md` and `kb/world/geography.md`, and reflected in
  the `lore-bible/work/drafts/dzinari.md` race-lore rewrite.
- **Saint Maarkest's tier** (2026-07-19). He's a mortal-god entry, but
  because he's secretly Tempeste rather than an ambiguous case - see
  `kb/characters/mortal-gods.md`, Maarkest, and the hidden/GM-only note on
  Tempeste in `kb/characters/immortal-gods.md`. This information is
  hidden/GM-only: do not state it in `core/setting/` prose. See "Hidden
  canon" in `lore-bible/CLAUDE.md`.
- **Trere** (2026-07-19). Written into `core/setting/geography.md` in full
  (Overview/Inhabitants/Key Locations: Mafsoleios, the Sea of Trere, the
  Eldritch Passage, Trere Island, the three pre-Sundering empires) and
  synced to `kb/world/geography.md`, `kb/world/vocab.md`,
  `kb/world/nations.md`, and `kb/timeline/`.
- **Mafsoleios: Trere or Lustralis?** (2026-07-19). Confirmed as author
  error - Mafsoleios was always meant to be on Trere. Corrected in
  `core/setting/nations.md` (was "western coast of Lustralis," now "western
  coast of Trere") and in `kb/world/geography.md`.
- **Trere's third lost empire and the Southlands' curse** (2026-07-19).
  Confirmed by the author to be the same empire. `core/setting/
  geography.md`'s Trere entry references this as legend ("said to linger
  somewhere between the Southlands and Trere"), matching the existing
  Southlands text without needing to rewrite it.
- **Trere/Lustralis/Sawolleas Sea geography** (2026-07-19). Corrected after
  an initial mix-up: Trere shares a land border with Lustralis (via the
  Great Sand); the Sawolleas Sea actually separates Inggaz and Terrevault,
  not Trere and Lustralis. See `kb/world/geography.md` and
  `kb/world/vocab.md`.
