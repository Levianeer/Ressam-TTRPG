# Factions

Named orders/units/groups worth tracking independent of their parent
nation - either because they recur, because they're the "signature
institution" of their nation (see `kb/styles/voice.md` on that pacing
device), or because they operate across/outside national lines. Source:
`core/setting/nations.md` unless noted.

## National signature orders

- **The Portatores** (Holy Reino Empire) - elite heavy infantry in ritually
  blessed full plate wielding hackbut-halberd combination weapons. Faith is
  load-bearing, literally - without it the armor becomes a prison. Fight in
  total silence; used as both shock troops and propaganda (living statues at
  cathedral doors).
- **The Black Blades** (Greater Groves) - *to be written* in canon. Flagged
  in `kb/issues/`.
- **The Donati** (Tagarian Coalition) - the Council of Sages' private
  enforcers, raised from orphaned children outside house allegiance. Led by
  the "il Datore" (currently Soren Vel, see `kb/characters/mortals.md`).
  Identity marked by porcelain great-cat masks assigned by their superiors
  (not chosen), cracks gold-filled rather than hidden. Armed rapier/dagger/
  pistol, swashbuckler style; serve as assassins, spies, enforcers. Great
  houses call them "Strays" as an insult; the Donati embrace it. Name means
  "The Given."
- **The Golden Choir** (Gilded Hwispian Maw) - elite Dzinari guard,
  surgically stripped of all capacity for expression (not just speech - any
  communication at all) as "the ultimate dishonor" in Dzinari culture.
  Receive the Devaraja's telepathic commands, cannot respond or communicate
  with each other. Strongest infantry unit in Ressam - "a threat to even
  minor immortal gods."

## Global factions

- **The Fellowship of Saint Cadogan** - secretive scribes/scholars
  preserving knowledge, no central base, network in nearly every society.
  Named for Saint Cadogan, a scribe martyred (burned) for opposing a book
  burning. True intentions deliberately unclear in canon - useful as a
  faction to keep genuinely ambiguous rather than resolve prematurely.

## Local factions

- **The Pirate Lords of Pearling Bay** - see `kb/characters/mortal-gods.md`,
  Sir Davil. Governs by his four Core Tenets (quote verbatim from
  `core/setting/religions.md` if reused). Anti-hierarchy freeport, sanctuary
  for outcasts/revolutionaries; formerly Tagarian Coalition territory,
  seceded.
