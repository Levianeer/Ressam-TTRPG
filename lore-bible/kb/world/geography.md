### Trere detail

Written into `core/setting/geography.md` (2026-07-19) - full prose lives
there now, this is the condensed version:

- Land border with Lustralis along the Great Sand (north); land connection
  to Halig (east); sea-divided from the Southlands by the South Seas
  (south). **Not** bordered by the Sawolleas Sea - that's the Inggaz/
  Terrevault border, unrelated to Trere; don't reuse that mix-up.
- **Mafsoleios** - one of Trere's two major settlements, western coast. See
  `kb/world/nations.md`.
- **Svarnanagaram** (added 2026-07-20) - the other major settlement; the
  Gilded Hwispian Maw's capital, inherited whole from their Trere-empire
  predecessor rather than built. Far less grand/esoteric than the Chorus of
  Murmurs' Aegislith Veil - blunt fortified stone. See `kb/world/nations.md`.
- **The Sea of Trere** - the waters off Mafsoleios' coast; the city's only
  dock.
- **The Eldritch Passage** - sea route between mainland Trere and Trere
  Island. Bad-luck reputation more than real danger - genuinely rough tides/
  quakes/weather (moon-amplified), used almost only by Coalition traders and
  Reino Empire prison ships bound for **Noskirk Colony** (a distant northern
  penal colony, not on Trere itself - past the Sawolleas Sea, near Inggaz/
  Terrevault. Needs its own home once that region gets a detail pass -
  logged in `kb/issues/`).
- **Trere Island** - largely abandoned, a few small Tagarian Coalition naval
  colonies only.
- **Three empires ruled Trere before the Sundering.** Two left recognizable
  heirs: Mafsoleios and the Gilded Hwispian Maw. The third is lost to
  memory - confirmed by the author to be the same "long-dead empire" already
  credited in `core/setting/geography.md`'s Southlands entry, its remnants
  said to linger between the Southlands and Trere. Worth cross-referencing
  from a Southlands detail pass later.

## Planes

- **Mortal Plane** - stable, predictable physical laws; the anchor plane.
- **Fields of Nocturne** - the dream/nightmare plane, constantly reshaped by
  collective dreaming, mirrors the Mortal Plane's geography but can't be
  navigated off familiar landmarks. Sky reads as a "sea of mercury." Reached
  via the Infernal Gate (Inggaz) - most things that come through die to Orkh
  armies who treat the portal as a religious trial; Daemons and Strygs are
  the ones crafty enough to slip past.
