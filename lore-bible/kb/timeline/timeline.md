# Timeline

Dated event ledger. Kept as facts/dates, not prose - `core/setting/
history.md` has the narrative version (mythic register for the first two
eras, chronicle register after). Update this whenever a 402 RC war,
alliance, or succession changes, and reconcile `kb/canon/canon.md`'s
"Current 402 RC snapshot" at the same time.

## Undated / mythic era

- **Age of Mythos** - Thryzzaral's fracturing births the universe; the Great
  Artists shape it and make Ressam their masterpiece; the God-Dragons
  (Infierno, Rocas, Tempeste, Cascate) emerge from the Artists' spent
  essence and become the world's eternal maintainers.
- **Pre-Sundering Trere** - three empires rule the continent of Trere. Two
  leave recognizable heirs after the Sundering (Mafsoleios, the Gilded
  Hwispian Maw); the third is lost to memory, its remnants said to linger
  between the Southlands and Trere - very likely the same "long-dead
  empire" the Southlands' curse is already attributed to. See
  `kb/world/geography.md`, Trere detail.

## The Sundering (pre-RC, calamity that sets the RC calendar's zero point)

- Aldric Grimthorn attempts to control the God-Dragons, believing them
  responsible for the world's catastrophes; banishes them instead, bonding
  his soul to them in the process.
- The banished dragons return embittered; under Tempeste (in secret human
  form) found the Fe Alada and begin annihilating the great nations.
- Aldric hires five adventurers (including Voa Shadowhorn) who, via his
  teleportation, strike each God-Dragon in turn - killing (not permanently)
  and cleansing their madness, forcing them dormant.
- Cascate, one of the four, destroys the kingdom of the (then-mortal/angel)
  figure who later ascends as Henri Gachet Gautier.

## Age of Revival (pre-RC, aftermath)

- Fragile, mistrustful recovery; nations turn inward, hoard resources,
  reignite old rivalries. The Fe Alada's influence grows in the chaos it
  helped cause. No hard date given in canon - ends where Third Age/RC
  dating begins.

## The Third Age (1-402 RC and counting)

| Period | RC range | Headline |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| The Dawn of Recovery | 1-50 RC | RC calendar begins; Holy Reino Empire consolidates around Fe Alada; Greater Groves and Dwergaz Triumvirate withdraw/seal themselves |
| The Great Arms Race | 50-200 RC | Power vacuum, not peace; Tagarian Coalition expands via naval supremacy, Tor'Az industrializes; Fe Alada's aggressive expansion under the Empire sows future religious conflict |
| The Orkhaden Resurgence | 200-300 RC | God-King Khor unifies the Orkh tribes; Orkhaden Horde active expansion under Champion Ghorzuk Firemaw; Chorus of Murmurs rises, Winter War with Tor'Az begins |
| The Modern Crisis | 350-402 RC | Gilded Hwispian Maw emerges as existential threat; multiple simultaneous wars begin (below) |

## Key ongoing conflicts (as of 402 RC - update in place as these resolve)

| Conflict | Sides | Status note |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Dunestrife War | Desert Nomads + Alashar Tribes vs. Hwispian Maw | sacred-land defense |
| Northwall War | Holy Reino Empire vs. Hwispian Maw | Empire's northern colonies, tolerating local faiths as a bulwark |
| Eshzera War | Greater Groves vs. Tagarian Coalition | Empire unexpectedly aids the Aelves here (see Eshzera War note below) |
| Haligean War | multi-alliance (Empire + Coalition + local tribes) vs. Orkhaden occupation | unusual rival-cooperation, driven by Halig trade offers |
| Winter War | Tor'Az Triumvirate vs. Chorus of Murmurs | northern Terrevault |
| Inggaz Skirmishes | Orkhaden Horde vs. Tor'Az Triumvirate & Tagarian Coalition | resource-diversion pressure on both |
| Asalter Crisis | Holy Reino Empire vs. Tagarian Coalition (naval standoff) | Asalter seceded from the Empire circa 390 RC over trade rights; Coalition offers protection; Empire disputes but won't risk open war |
| Southern Persecutions | Holy Reino Empire, internal | religious persecution of southern populations |

**Eshzera War note (circa 400 RC):** the Empire's sudden peace with the
Greater Groves and material support for the Aelves against the Coalition is
the most recent major realignment in canon - read as a calculated move to
weaken an overextended Coalition. Treat this as the most "live" thread in
402 RC if a story needs an active political hook.

## Notes for future updates

- If a listed war resolves, move it out of "Key ongoing conflicts" into a
  new "Resolved conflicts" section here (don't just delete it - the outcome
  is itself canon-worthy) and update `kb/canon/canon.md`'s snapshot.
- New wars/crises get added to the table with the same shape: sides +
  one-line status note.
