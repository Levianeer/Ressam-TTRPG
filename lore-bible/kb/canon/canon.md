# Canon (condensed reference)

Cross-cutting settled facts distilled from `core/setting/` that don't
belong to one single `kb/` domain file. Split into per-domain files once
any one section outgrows a quick read. Sources noted per section.

## Cosmology: the Mourning Star

*The Servant Sun.* Ressam's own star, orbiting the planet (not the reverse)
- proof of the world's former divine favor. Source:
`core/setting/geography.md`.

- **Unnatural Seasons** - orbital changes in distance/angle produce complex
  climates; some regions get multiple "summers"/"winters" per cycle.
- **Divine Radiation** - envelops Ressam in divine energy that once
  sustained life; not currently deadly because all beings have an inherent
  holy resistance to it, but that immunity is stated to be fading over time
  - a slow-burn setting-wide stake worth remembering for future lore
    (nothing in canon dates when/if it fails).
- **Gravitational Strain** - the binding strains the world, causing
  ceaseless quakes.

Culturally read as lost glory (a reminder of divine favor and abandonment),
a prisoner or a faithful servant depending on the faith, and the basis for
"Calendars of Decay" - astronomers track the orbit's gradual decay.

## Cosmology: the twin moons

*Patéras and Mitéra - the Father and the Mother.* Synchronized orbit,
Patéras leading, Mitéra trailing. Source: `core/setting/geography.md`.

- Four high tides daily; coastal cultures sync fishing/trade to it.
- Frequent, brief illumination - nights are rarely fully dark.
- Contributes to Terrevault-region seismic activity.
- One of the two moons is the Dwergaz's **Great Mother/Matka** (see
  `kb/characters/immortal-gods.md`) - which one is disputed between clans,
  deliberately unresolved in canon. Don't resolve it unilaterally in new
  lore; if it needs resolving for a specific story reason, log it in
  `kb/issues/` first.

## Calendar

Four seasonal quarters, each split into two ~44-48 day months: Gaudivivo
(spring), Irafoco (summer), Tristemara (autumn), Pavoreombra (winter). Full
festival list (with day-of-month and which culture observes it) lives in
`core/setting/calendar.md` - it's already a condensed almanac, no further
distillation needed here.
