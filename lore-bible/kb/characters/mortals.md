# Mortals

Notable named figures with no divine status. Source: `core/setting/heroes.md`
unless noted; cross-referenced against `nations.md`/`history.md` where a
figure also appears there. Keep naming consistent with whichever
`[Given Name], [Epithet]` or `[Title] [Name]` form canon already uses (see
`kb/styles/voice.md`, Naming convention).

## Aldric Grimthorn

Human master wizard, the only one capable of long-range teleportation.
Contested legacy: deeply linked to both the God-Dragons' banishment/return
during the Sundering *and* to stopping their wrath - his banishment spell
bonded his soul to the dragons, making him immortal and powerful but the
dragons vulnerable. Hired the five adventurers who slew (not permanently)
the God-Dragons. Some worship him as a hero, many consider him irredeemable.

## Voa Shadowhorn

Legendary wizard, one of the five who helped stop the Sundering. Pioneered
'Shadow Magic' after the cataclysm as a way of processing the loss of her
comrades; world-renowned master of it, with her own students/legacy.

## Alfred Sibbell Hunter

Legendary Human Night-Terror hunter - one of only a few to survive a journey
to the Fields of Nocturne.

## Ghorzuk, Firemaw

Uncharacteristically cunning Orkh; current "Champion" (a title granted by
God-King Khor to his most capable followers). Known for a strategic mind
that turns enemies against each other.

## Master Ozah Bale

Oldest known living Tembel - nearly 10 feet tall, almost entirely immobile.
Pilgrimage destination; most visitors are actually his students attending to
his health rather than seekers of counsel.

## The Tor'Az Triumvirate Three

The three Dwergan guild masters who jointly rule the Tor'Az Triumvirate -
each seat resting on a different pillar of survival (`nations.md`):

- **Durnir, the Ironhand** - Barazel Guild (wealth). Little is publicly
  known about him beyond a trail of missing people/corpses following anyone
  who asks too much.
- **Runvar, the Unshakable** - Matgadel Guild (wards the deep halls).
  Earned his title leading a campaign against the largest Tierratuar clan,
  slaying their near-god-like leader.
- **Dwarin, the Industrious** - Dromgadol Guild (the war-frontier of
  Tormaz). Ran the experiments on captured Orkhs/Daemons that produced the
  Tierratuar race as a weapon; the Dromgadol Guild is quietly suspected
  (never confessed) of teaching that Melacha's chisel is an office anyone
  masterful enough can take up - see `immortal-gods.md`, Melacha's heresies.

## Torin Elmbrow

Ranger, unparalleled tracker; protects the wilderness from exploitation,
master of stealth/camouflage.

## Thalorien of the Greater Groves

Leader of the Greater Groves (the Aelfen grove-council). Rose to prominence
during the Sundering by uniting the groves through knowledge and diplomacy;
credited with the Groves' subsequent peace/prosperity.

## High Lord Victor Ardan

Ruler of the Holy Reino Empire, both emperor and pope, head of the Fe Alada.
Note (`kb/styles/voice.md`): his `heroes.md` entry is the one padded/
repetitive outlier in that chapter's otherwise terse register - don't use
its prose as a model, but the facts (unites-factions, expansionist,
influential) are canon.

## Vespertine the Gilded

Mysterious Dzinari, radiant/enigmatic, associated with light and
illumination; appeared suddenly with unknown origins/motives. Uses her
powers to aid people in dark times - a beacon-of-hope figure rather than a
faction leader.

## The Koté

Prince of the Tagarian Coalition, figurehead of the Council of Sages. Young,
spoiled, but genuinely talented and charming; most aristocrats don't respect
him due to age/inexperience, but his ambition may yet let him seize real
control of the Council.

## Soren Vel

Current "il Datore" - leader of the Donati, the Tagarian Coalition's
orphan-raised enforcer order (see `kb/world/factions.md`). Described as "a
young prodigy."
