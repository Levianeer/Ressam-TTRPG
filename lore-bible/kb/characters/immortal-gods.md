# Immortal Gods

Cosmic in origin, not once-mortal. See "Character tiers" in
`lore-bible/CLAUDE.md` for the tier definition and why it matters for new
writing. Source: `core/setting/history.md`, `core/setting/religions.md`,
`core/setting/geography.md`.

## Thryzzaral

The first God - an "utterly alien being whose existence defied
comprehension," alone in the void before creation. Went mad from absolute
isolation; its mind fractured, tearing the rift through which the universe
was born. Source of the Great Artists (below). Status: fate unclear/unstated
past the fracturing - flag as an open question in `kb/issues/` if new lore
needs to touch this.

## The Great Artists (the Four Painters)

Ressam's Aelven religion (Elar'laevien) calls them **the Four Painters**;
`history.md` calls them **the Great Artists** - same beings, two names, used
by different cultures. The splintered personalities of Thryzzaral, each
embodying an emotional facet of existence and together representing entropy.
Shaped suns, planets, and moons; declared Ressam their masterpiece and
poured their remaining essence into it, which seeded life and (via the
elemental pools they rested in) gave rise to the God-Dragons. Gone as active
beings since - their "legacy" (divine energy responsive to belief) is the
stated origin of Gods and magic generally.

Aelven doctrine (`religions.md`, Elar'laevien) holds they are not to be
worshipped as gods but venerated as artists whose brushstrokes shaped
reality - the world itself is "painted," not merely created. Groves are
attuned to a Painter's style: wind, fire, stone, or water.

## The God-Dragons

Elemental dragons born when the pools the Great Artists rested in absorbed
their essence and gained sentience. Rose against their creators, absorbed
what divine energy remained, and became "the eternal maintainers of the
universe" - opposed to entropy/change, unlike the Artists. Still exist,
dormant/watching since the Sundering.

- **Infierno** - fire
- **Rocas** - earth
- **Tempeste** - air/storm; led the God-Dragons during the Sundering, took
  human form and founded the Fe Alada in secret as "a prophet and saviour."
  **Hidden/GM-only (do not state plainly in `core/setting/` prose - see
  "Hidden canon" in `lore-bible/CLAUDE.md`):** that human form was the
  swordsman **Maarkest**, now venerated as the Fe Alada's one Saint (Patron
  of Martyrdom). Canon's public version (`religions.md`) has Maarkest "said
  to have helped the God-Dragons during the Sundering"; the true version is
  that Gran Reino blames the Sundering on the five adventurers who fought
  the dragons, and Maarkest/Tempeste "gave his life" trying to stop them -
  a martyrdom the faith he himself founded now venerates. When the
  God-Dragons were slain-and-reconstituted at the Sundering's end, Tempeste
  returned to the world as Maarkest again, but with no memory or awareness
  of his own divinity - he currently wanders as a lost swordsman. Full
  write-up: `kb/characters/mortal-gods.md`, Maarkest.
- **Cascate** - water; destroyed the kingdom of Henri Gachet Gautier (see
  `mortal-gods.md`) before Henri's ascension

Status as of 402 RC: dormant, minds cleansed of the madness the Sundering's
banishment caused, per `history.md`. Whether "dormant" could change is an
open worldbuilding lever - track any decision to move on it in
`kb/timeline/`.

## The Tagarian Pantheon

The Feliid pantheon - practical, domain-specific gods that "rarely interact
with mortals"; most Feliids respect rather than actively worship them. All
16 are named only by domain-pair, no proper names given in canon (e.g. "God
of Sustenance," "God of Breath and Blood," "God of Sacred Madness"), grouped
into five sub-pantheons: Survival and Function, Security and Stability,
Family and Connection, Respect and Status, Fulfillment and Purpose. Full
list: `core/setting/religions.md`.

## Al'Nur Lehovil

The singular divine figure of the Desert Nomads' and Alsahli's faith (also
called Al'Nur Lehovil) - "God of Gods, Shepherd of Men (and, by extension,
Alsahli), He of the End and Beginning." Walked among his people in the First
Age, led their ancestors through a calamity to the Great Sand, then
vanished - interpreted as ascension, not death. Return prophesied. Speaks
through prophets in extraordinary times.

## The Dwergan Triad + the Great Mother

`religions.md` is explicit that above even the Triad "stands the Great
Mother, from whose veins every Dwergaz was cut" - she is one of the twin
moons (Patéras or Mitéra), though which one is disputed between clans/creeds
(see `core/character/races/dwergaz.md` for the creation myth and the dispute
in full - out of scope for the current race-lore-quality caveat since this
is the cosmology, not the culture write-up). She has no teachings, temples,
or worship in the normal sense - she is *attended*, not prayed to.

- **Mischar** - trade, commerce, wealth, greed; the Mother's one daughter
  (not cut from a vein like her brothers - hidden instead). Symbol: a
  balanced scale.
- **Nekama** - knowledge, memory, honour, revenge. Symbol: an open book.
- **Melacha** - artists, craftsmen, perfectionism, self-doubt; "the First
  Cutter," who freed his brothers from the vein. Symbol: a chisel piercing
  an incomplete circle.

Each has an associated heretical sect (see `religions.md` for detail) -
worth knowing before inventing new Dwergan lore so a new heresy doesn't
collide with an existing one.
