# Mortal Gods

Ascended from mortal (or mortal-adjacent) origin, and read as personal and
flawed even at world-shaking power - the opposite feel from the immortal
gods' cosmic distance. "Mortal-god" is used explicitly in canon (the
Devaraja, Sir Davil), which is why this tier exists as written vocabulary,
not just an organizing convenience. See "Character tiers" in
`lore-bible/CLAUDE.md`. Source: `core/setting/religions.md`,
`core/setting/nations.md`, `core/setting/heroes.md`.

## God-King Khor

Once an Orkh warlord; ascended to godhood after banding together all the
great Orkh tribes during the 2nd Era. Worshipped among Orkhs as their
saviour. Rules (in absentia/through doctrine) the Orkhaden Horde; grants the
title of "Champion" to his most capable followers - currently held by
Ghorzuk Firemaw (see `mortals.md`). Capital/seat: the Pyral Spire, a
volcano-fortress in Inggaz.

## Vessel

*Shepherd of Souls.* A mortal-god of transition, compassion, and one of the
few *good* deities of necromancy - explicitly contrasted with "the dread
lords of undeath" and "the cold arbiters of the afterlife." A singer/guide
for souls, especially those who died too soon or in suffering. Faith is
hopeful but not toothless: cruelty is repaid with servitude until forgiveness
is earned.

## Tythe

*God of the Lost and Second Chances.* Enigmatic deity who appears at the
crossroads of life and death, offering pacts to the dying - a second chance
in exchange for a future act, often violent or sacrificial. Marked by an
omen of impending bloodshed. Worshipped mainly by warlocks and assassins,
and by those she's personally spared, who bear a burn that never heals.

## Henri Gachet Gautier

*God of Cities, Castles, and Civilization.* Formerly a fallen angel whose
kingdom was destroyed by the God-Dragon Cascate; ascended to godhood after
avenging his lost home. Protector of cities/civilization, defense,
construction, community. Worshippers: Les Falaises (Order of Protectors -
builders, healers, defenders) and the Chevaliers des Vagues (self-righteous
monster-hunting knights). Has "Godspawn" - angelic Syraph, porcelain-faced
winged beings with flaming swords. Seat: Château Dubien, northernmost
Lustralis. Symbol: a sun rising over a castle palisade.

## Sir Davil

*Heroic Pirate God-King of Thieves, Winds, and Skies.* Embodiment of
trickery, rebellion, liberation. Rules Pearling Bay, an island sanctuary/
freeport for outcasts and revolutionaries under a shared-resources,
anti-hierarchy ethos. Protects the island via conjured headwinds/storms
rather than military force. Governs by four Core Tenets (see
`core/setting/religions.md` for the full text - worth quoting verbatim if
Davil or Pearling Bay recurs, the phrasing is load-bearing).

## The Devaraja (Highest Devaraja of the Golden Throne)

An ancient Dzinari, "countless-named," who leads the Gilded Hwispian Maw.
Canon states outright he "has transcended mortal limitations to become a
'mortal god'... while retaining the flawed, ambitious nature of his Dzinari
heritage." Discovered fundamental cosmic secrets and has the power to heal
the world's fading divine immunity but chooses tyranny instead (Deapsang
addiction, the Golden Choir). Constrained by unspecified cosmic forces that
would erase him if he pushes reality too far - a real mechanical/narrative
limit, not just flavor text, if he's used as an antagonist in future lore.
See `kb/world/factions.md` for the Gilded Hwispian Maw's structure.

**Confirmed by the author (2026-07-20):** this is the same Devaraja who,
in the deep past, cursed a race of human conquerors into the Alsahli as
punishment for slaughtering "a god's favoured children" during a war fought
for plunder rather than glory - see `core/character/races/alsahli.md`'s
Origins and Mythology. Fits his "countless-named, ancient" framing; this is
one act among a long, otherwise unrecorded history. Which god's children
were killed is deliberately left unspecified in canon - don't resolve this
unilaterally if it comes up again, treat it the same as the Dwergaz
Great Mother question.

## Maarkest

*Patron Saint of Martyrdom.* The Fe Alada's one Saint, venerated for having
"given his life" trying to stop the five adventurers Gran Reino blames for
the Sundering. Publicly, that's the whole story - and that's exactly how
`core/setting/religions.md` should stay worded (vague: "said to have helped
the God-Dragons during the Sundering").

**Hidden/GM-only - not stated in `core/setting/` prose:** Maarkest is not a
mortal at all. He is **Tempeste**, the God-Dragon of air/storm, in the human
form he took to found the Fe Alada in secret (see
`kb/characters/immortal-gods.md`). His "martyrdom" is Tempeste's own defeat
during the Sundering. When the God-Dragons were slain-and-reconstituted, he
returned to the world as Maarkest again - but with no memory of his own
divinity. He currently wanders Ressam as a lost, ordinary swordsman, unaware
that an entire faith venerates him, and unaware that he founded it.

This is why he's filed here and not in `immortal-gods.md`: functionally, in
the present day, he *is* what this tier describes - personal, flawed,
diminished, walking among mortals - even though his true nature/origin is
immortal-god-tier. Tier placement follows how a figure currently presents
and is used in a story, not just origin; flag any similarly split case in
`kb/issues/` rather than picking a tier silently.

**Don't reveal this in-world casually.** If a story needs Maarkest or the
Maarkest/Tempeste connection, the correct default is: NPCs and public texts
only know the martyr legend, and Maarkest himself doesn't know either. Any
scene that has a character learn the truth is a real story beat, not
incidental exposition.
